# C030 - Manuscript Conclusion Says Two Review Rounds; Its Method Section Says Three

## Status

Open — pending author review.

## Contradiction

Side A — the conclusion:

> subjected the whole to two rounds of documented adversarial review with an explicit integrate-or-bound discipline (`drafts/paper.md:684`; identical in Spanish, `drafts/paper.es.md:729-730`)

Side B — the method section and abstract of the same document:

> The loop ran three rounds, all now fully resolved and propagated. (`drafts/paper.md:541`; Spanish `drafts/paper.es.md:583`) — enumerating round 1 (18 attacks), round 2 (15), round 3 (2, from the simulated external review), consistent with the abstract (`drafts/paper.md:41-42`).

## Why they cannot both be true

Two and three are different numbers for the same loop, inside one document. Section 10 predates the third round and was not updated when the external-review round entered Section 7 and the abstract.

## Author-intent side

Side B — the three-round structure is factual (35 = 18 + 15 + 2) and is presented as a methodological contribution.

## Proposed treatment

Update the conclusion sentence (EN and ES) to "three rounds". One-word fix in each language.

## Severity

High — a referee reading the conclusion against the abstract catches the manuscript contradicting itself on its own method.
