# C031 - Twenty Reference Entries Are Never Cited in the Manuscript Body

## Status

Accepted — corrected in the one-hundred-sixteenth residual cleanup.

## Contradiction

Side A — the reference apparatus: `drafts/paper.md` References (57 entries) includes Abbott 1988, Akerlof 1970, Andreoni 1990, Arnstein 1969, Bovens 2007, Buchanan & Tullock 1962, Dahl 1961, Dahl 1971, Dulleck & Kerschbamer 2006, Fishkin 2009, Fung 2006, Habermas 1996, Hirschman 1970, Holmström & Milgrom 1991, Jensen & Meckling 1976, Kydland & Prescott 1977, Levi 1988, North 1990, Olson 1965, Thompson 1980.

Side B — the manuscript body: none of these twenty is cited anywhere in the text, parenthetically or narratively (each author string occurs exactly once in the document — in the References list itself). Verified for both `drafts/paper.md` and `drafts/paper.es.md`; the reverse direction is clean (every in-text citation has an entry).

## Why they cannot both be true

A reference list asserts "these works are cited in this paper." Twenty entries assert citations that do not exist. Referees and reference-checking tools flag exactly this; it also inflates the apparent engagement with the literature — against the corpus's own no-decoration citation rule.

## Author-intent side

Unclear — two defensible readings. These works entered via the corpus (they anchor attacks/defenses), so either (a) trim them from the paper's list (the list documents the *paper's* citations; the corpus record keeps the fuller apparatus), or (b) cite each where its content already does implicit work (e.g., Thompson 1980 at the many-hands passage `drafts/paper.md:549`, Dulleck & Kerschbamer at thin markets, Hirschman at participation decay, Jensen & Meckling at role separation) — several have natural, non-decorative homes.

## Proposed treatment

Author's choice between (a) trim and (b) targeted in-text citations; a mixed approach is likely right — cite the ~8 with obvious homes, trim the rest. Either way, EN and ES must move together.

## Severity

Medium — no factual falsehood, but a guaranteed referee flag and a tension with the project's own citation discipline.
