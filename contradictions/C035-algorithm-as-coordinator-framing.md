# C035 - Algorithm-as-Coordinator Concept Card vs the Qualified Allocation Framing

## Status

Accepted (qualify) — corrected in the one-hundred-sixteenth residual cleanup: the card now distinguishes direct per-citizen choice from aggregated signal construction, with the E4 evidence and its preconditions.

## Contradiction

Side A — `knowledge/concepts/algorithm-as-coordinator.md:15-22`: "posts become projects; likes or interactions become resource assignments; people decide what they value; the system coordinates the resulting allocation" — a pure-aggregation analogy in which individual citizen decisions drive allocation.

Side B — the evidence-qualified framing: `drafts/paper.md:522` ("the honest description is inspectable intermediation with a citizen-correctable default"), `docs/93` (A025: most citizens route allocation through defaults, profiles, or delegation), and E1-E3 (the salience-following crowd funds salience).

## Why they are in tension

The card's analogy describes exactly the direct-choice regime the simulation qualifies: under realistic attention, the passive share (governed by the weight vector) — not per-project individual decisions — carries allocation quality. Read alone, the card promises a mechanism the evidence says works differently.

## Author-intent side

Genuinely both. The card captures the author's aspiration — and E4's R4 regime (allocation weights built from aggregated citizen signals) partially vindicates it *at the aggregate level*: citizens' dispersed signals can drive the vector even when individual attention is low. The tension is between "each like is an allocation" (not supported) and "aggregated citizen knowledge constructs the allocation weights" (measured viable).

## Proposed treatment

Author's call. Suggested: keep the card, add a qualifying paragraph distinguishing the two mechanisms — direct per-citizen choice (real but minority) and aggregated signal construction (E4) — and note the social-proof caveat (independence, A027).

## Severity

Low — conceptual card, internal; but worth resolving before external readers cite it.
