# C034 - H058 Presents Open Mode Without the A023 Deployment Gate

## Status

Open — pending author review.

## Contradiction

Side A — `knowledge/hypotheses/H058-operating-modes-for-transition.md:113-117` (the canonical operating-modes card): "### 4. Open mode … Projects operate under the ordinary distributed protocol without institutional pre-publication moderation. Control happens through transparency, fiscalization, reputation, complaints, guarantees, and observability." — no deployment condition stated; the card contains zero references to A023 or docs/91.

Side B — `docs/91_OPEN_MODE_GATING_AND_A023_RESOLUTION.md:14`: "open mode must not deploy until constitutional-level decision mechanics are resolved and published."

## Why they cannot both be true

The canonical card describes open mode as an available configuration; the accepted resolution forbids deploying it until a named precondition is met. A country implementer reading H058 alone would configure a mode the corpus gates. A023 was propagated to H017 and H009 but never into H058's own open-mode section.

## Author-intent side

Side B for the gate's existence — but note the author's standing position: open/semi-open is the designed trajectory, so the fix must state the gate without recasting open mode as undesirable.

## Proposed treatment

Add one sentence to H058's open-mode section: open mode deployment is gated on resolving and publishing constitutional decision mechanics (`docs/91`), and note E4's measured viability of open scope construction as the trajectory's evidence. Also add the gate to the card's remaining-design-questions list.

## Severity

High — the canonical modes card contradicts a gating resolution on the architecture's end state.
