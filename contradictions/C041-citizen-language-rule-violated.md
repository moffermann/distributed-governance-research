# C041 - C009's Citizen-Language Rule Is Violated by the Citizen-Facing Status Maps

## Status

Accepted — corrected in the one-hundred-sixteenth residual cleanup.

## Contradiction

Side A — the accepted rule, `docs/49_LAYERED_CITIZEN_STATE_TRANSLATION_AND_C009_RESOLUTION.md:187-189`: "Citizen-facing language should avoid terms like: - execution-ready; …" (unless the user opens technical detail); the resolution's own aggregate list uses "Ready for execution".

Side B — the citizen-facing status maps: `docs/30_PROJECT_LIFECYCLE_AFTER_PUBLICATION_FLOW.md:667-671` "Citizen-facing statuses should remain simple: … Execution-ready", with the same label used as citizen-facing in `docs/12_OPEN_PROJECT_PARALLEL_CLOSURE_MODEL.md:190` and `docs/29:984-995`.

## Why they cannot both be true

A resolution rules a term out of default citizen-facing language; three flow documents present that exact term as the recommended citizen-facing status. One of the two conventions must yield.

## Author-intent side

Likely Side A (C009 is the accepted resolution; "Ready for execution" is its compliant label), but the author may instead consider "Execution-ready" acceptable and amend C009's example list — both are defensible.

## Proposed treatment

Author picks the canonical citizen label ("Ready for execution" per C009, or amend C009). Then align docs/12, docs/29, docs/30 to it.

## Severity

Medium — a normative rule contradicted by the documents that implement it.
