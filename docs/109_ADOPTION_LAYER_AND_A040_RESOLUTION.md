## Resolution decision

A040 is founded as a missing deployment-design layer — and the author's review added the boundary that resolves its sting. The manuscript's research question is architectural, not political: the state's current form was designed more than two hundred years ago; with the technology that exists today, could an architecture change improve on the status quo? The paper's answer is that it can be done and delivers more value per unit of budget. Whether any particular authority *wants* to do it is a different question, which the paper never claimed to answer, and the attack's demand that it do so conflates feasibility with willingness. What the attack correctly identifies is that the *corpus as a deployment design* owes the answer to the conditional question: **given** an authority that has already decided to adopt, how must the instruments be configured so that adoption is survivable — "evaluate me from here onward". The accepted resolution is an adoption layer, conditional by construction, with every accountability instrument untouched.

Under [[P007-integrate-or-bound-rule|knowledge/principles/P007-integrate-or-bound-rule.md]], this bounds the thesis (feasibility and efficiency, not political will) and integrates the deployment layer as configuration defaults and one design document — no new Core v0 entity.

## Rule added to Core v0

**The adoption layer.** Deployment configuration defaults for the authority that has decided to adopt, entering force only at adoption.

Minimum elements:

- **The thesis boundary, stated:** the manuscript's Section 9 and the publishable model state explicitly that the architecture's feasibility and delivered-value results do not depend on any authority's willingness; adoption preconditions are deployment contingencies, named so that no reader mistakes their absence for a defect of the thesis;
- **Prospective baseline framing as the pilot default:** instrumentation begins at adoption; the visibility gap is published as the adopter's declared starting line — measure me from here — with pre-instrumentation estimates reported separately, impersonally, and as context, following the corpus's existing prospective pattern ([[88_FISCAL_COMMITMENT_PROFILE_AND_A021_RESOLUTION|docs/88_FISCAL_COMMITMENT_PROFILE_AND_A021_RESOLUTION.md]]: declared profile forward, no backward audit);
- **An adoption-theory design note** naming the three adopter archetypes — the post-scandal challenger (adopts for contrast; needs the prospective frame), the mandating higher government (adopts by transfer condition; the procurement-platform precedent), and the conditioned external funder (adopts as the price of money) — and the instrument configuration each requires;
- **A credit-attribution surface:** the adopter's completed projects carry citizen-visible verification records — delivered value proven by the system's own data, an asset no non-adopting rival can counterfeit; early-win sequencing (small, fast, visible projects first) is recorded as legitimate pilot configuration, priced honestly by the verification layer itself;
- **Official-protection defaults:** first-cycle timeout resolutions attribute institutionally (the office, not the person), with a published good-faith safe harbor and capacity-calibrated deadlines per [[103_ADMINISTRATIVE_CAPACITY_DECLARATION_AND_A035_RESOLUTION|docs/103_ADMINISTRATIVE_CAPACITY_DECLARATION_AND_A035_RESOLUTION.md]] — so the machine is adoptable by the people inside it, not only by the authority that signs it;
- **The symmetry clause:** no scope configuration exempts the operating authority's own projects from the instruments it aims at others; the adoption layer makes the machine survivable for its sponsor, never selective in its sponsor's favor.

## Macul example

A newly elected Macul mayor adopts the sports-scope pilot. Quarter one publishes: "Instrumentation begins 2027-03; verified-delivery baseline from this date; prior-period completion figures are municipal self-reports, shown for context without attribution." Her first two projects are small, fast, and finish with citizen-visible verified milestones — campaign material no rival possesses. Her public-works office appears in timeout resolutions as an office, under a declared capacity profile, while the habit forms. And the scope she configured cannot exclude the municipality's own gymnasium project from the same instruments — the symmetry clause is what lets her call the machine credible when it flatters her.

## Citizen simplicity

The citizen sees dated verification records and one framing line on pre-adoption figures. Nothing else changes on the citizen surface.

## Transparency and accountability effect

Nothing is blunted: every gap is still measured, every timeout still resolves publicly, every indicator still runs. What changes is time and credit — the gap becomes the adopter's chosen commitment device rather than a retroactive indictment, and verified delivery becomes the adopter's visible asset. Per [[P001-comparative-critique-rule|knowledge/principles/P001-comparative-critique-rule.md]], this is how exposing instruments were actually adopted in the real world: procurement platforms and participatory budgeting spread through challengers, mandates, and conditioned funds — never through incumbents volunteering for retroactive audits.

## Scope boundary and limitation

Core v0 designs the instruments and their adoption-time configuration; it does not manufacture political will, and the manuscript's claims stand independently of whether any authority ever adopts. The adoption-theory note is deployment engineering, not a prediction.

Limitation statement: the adoption layer selects — the architecture will be adopted first by relatively clean or newly arrived sponsors, in jurisdictions that need it least, and the corpus says so rather than promising otherwise; and no symmetry clause can fully prevent a sophisticated operator from timing scopes and project mixes to flatter itself — it can only make the flattery visible.

## Residual risks

- An incumbent with much to hide still rationally never adopts; deployment geography is bounded by sponsor supply, not by design quality.
- The prospective frame can be abused as amnesty theater — adopt, reset the clock, and point forward; the impersonal pre-adoption context figures are the mitigation, and their impersonality is a deliberate trade.
- Credit surfaces invite gaming toward photogenic quick wins; the verification layer prices delivery honestly, but portfolio composition remains the sponsor's choice, visible through the existing salience and territorial observability.

## Integration target
