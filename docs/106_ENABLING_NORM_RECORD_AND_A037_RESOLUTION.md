## Resolution decision

A037 is founded as a threshold gap: the corpus recorded authorization without establishing that an authorization of sufficient rank can exist, and the manuscript's "legally mandated share" language treated as premise what is in fact a conclusion. The author's review fixed the resolution's posture: that binding citizen allocation is not possible in the pilot jurisdiction today does not make the work useless — it makes the enabling norm a *stated requirement*, and the architecture's job is to specify, on the strength of the legal bases other countries have already built, exactly what the competent authority must enact. The accepted resolution is an enabling-norm record with a binding-mode gate on the existing Allocation Mandate, a two-readings clause across the manuscript surfaces, and a reference legal proposal as a required country-implementation deliverable before any binding pilot.

Under [[P007-integrate-or-bound-rule|knowledge/principles/P007-integrate-or-bound-rule.md]], this integrates a record and a gate through an existing object and bounds the doctrine: the platform states the legal requirement and records what law supplies; constructing the norm remains external legislative work.

## Rule added to Core v0

Every active Planning Scope's Allocation Mandate additionally records the legal foundation of the citizen allocation capacity it grants.

Minimum elements:

- an **enabling-norm record**: the instrument, its rank, the competence it confers, the organ to which allocations are imputed, and the review path for the acts it produces;
- a **binding-mode gate**: no scope operates in binding mode unless the record names a norm of sufficient rank for that jurisdiction; where none exists, the scope runs in consultative or tutored mode, and the citizen surface says so in plain language before first allocation;
- in tutored mode, the existing rule stands and is restated here as the doctrinal answer during pilots: citizen signals and the weight vector are informational inputs, and every material allocation decision is issued as a reasoned public Governance Resolution imputed to the competent authority ([[58_TUTORED_MODE_GOVERNANCE_RESOLUTIONS_AND_C020_RESOLUTION|docs/58_TUTORED_MODE_GOVERNANCE_RESOLUTIONS_AND_C020_RESOLUTION.md]]);
- a **reference legal proposal** as a required country-implementation deliverable before any binding-mode deployment: the draft enabling instrument for the target jurisdiction, built on the regional statutory precedents (Peru's participatory-budgeting statute, Brazil's city-statute framework) and specifying rank, conferred competence, imputation, and review;
- **legal-defection visibility**: repeal, adverse reinterpretation, or judicial suspension of the enabling norm surfaces as a recorded governance event with reason and affected scopes, parallel to the fiscal-reliability indicators of [[88_FISCAL_COMMITMENT_PROFILE_AND_A021_RESOLUTION|docs/88_FISCAL_COMMITMENT_PROFILE_AND_A021_RESOLUTION.md]];
- the **two-readings clause**, recorded here and reflected in the manuscript and the publishable model: under preference-input (consultative) status, the architecture's delivery, metering, and reputational-memory claims operate unchanged at pilot scale; binding allocation is required only by the mature open mode, whose deployment was already gated by [[91_OPEN_MODE_GATING_AND_A023_RESOLUTION|docs/91_OPEN_MODE_GATING_AND_A023_RESOLUTION.md]] — the enabling-norm requirement joins that gate.

## Macul example

The Macul sports scope's mandate records: current instrument — municipal ordinance under the national participation statute; rank — sufficient for consultative participation only; mode — tutored, with every funding decision issued as a reasoned municipal resolution; and, attached, the reference legal proposal stating what a future statute would need to contain for the same scope to run in binding mode. A citizen sees one sentence before allocating: "Your allocation guides the municipality's decision and is publicly traced; the final act is the municipality's, until the law referenced here changes that."

## Citizen simplicity

One mode-disclosure sentence at first use. The enabling-norm record, the gate status, and the reference proposal live in the scope's public record for lawyers, auditors, and legislators.

## Transparency and accountability effect

The architecture stops assuming its own legality and starts documenting it: every scope carries, visibly, the legal ground it stands on and the exact legislative distance to binding mode. What today is a diffuse objection — "this is probably illegal" — becomes an auditable record and a drafted requirement. The comparative gain stands per [[P001-comparative-critique-rule|knowledge/principles/P001-comparative-critique-rule.md]]: the current system never asks citizens at all; real participatory budgeting crossed this same threshold with statutes, and the corpus now cites and follows that path instead of ignoring it.

## Scope boundary and limitation

Core v0 records legal foundations and gates modes on them; it does not construct doctrine, draft statutes into force, or predict constitutional adjudication. The reference legal proposal is an input to legislative work, not a claim about its outcome.

Limitation statement: until a named instrument of sufficient rank exists in some jurisdiction, the architecture's binding mode is a designed capability awaiting law; its lawful deployments are consultative and tutored, and the manuscript must say so wherever it describes the allocation capacity.

## Residual risks

- A jurisdiction whose organic law forecloses even statutory delegation of allocation caps deployment at consultative mode indefinitely.
- The reference proposal may anchor legislative debate on the corpus's framing and inherit its blind spots; drafting it with local public-law counsel is the mitigation.
- Whoever controls the enabling statute controls the architecture's existence — a legal counterpart of fiscal strangulation that visibility records but cannot prevent.

## Integration target

This resolution should inform the Allocation Mandate record, Planning Scope and operating-mode configuration, the open-mode gate of docs/91, the manuscript's funding section and Section 9, the publishable model's legal boundaries, citizen-facing mode disclosure, and country-implementation guidance (the Chilean reference trajectory as the first worked annex).
