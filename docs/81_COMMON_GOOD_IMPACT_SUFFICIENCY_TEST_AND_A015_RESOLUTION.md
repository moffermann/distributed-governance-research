## Resolution decision

A015 is founded and does not distort the project when it strengthens project-level common-good disclosure without importing the full Extension v1+ charter system. The accepted resolution is a minimum common-good impact sufficiency test.

## Rule added to Core v0

A project that plausibly affects a common good should not proceed with a vague or unexplained impact declaration.

Minimum sufficiency test:

- identify affected assets and affected parties;
- declare no impact, uncertain impact, or impact with explanation;
- require evidence and fiscalization where impact is plausible;
- require active-charter relationship if a charter exists;
- prohibit unexplained overuse of `uncertain`;
- connect material omissions to fiscalization, complaint, mitigation, responsibility, and reputation.

## Macul example

A court project in a plaza may improve sports access but reduce green area, increase night noise, restrict informal use, or displace existing users. Even without a full common-good charter, those effects must be declared, visible, fiscalizable, and auditable.

## Citizen simplicity

Citizens should see simple common-good labels and material risks, not the full future charter machinery.

## Transparency and accountability effect

The rule avoids two failures: hiding common-good impact because no charter exists, and overloading Core v0 with full common-good governance.

## Residual risks

- Some common-good conflicts require legal, environmental, cultural, or scientific expertise.
- Strategic actors may overclaim common-good harm to block projects.
- Full registry and charter mechanics remain Extension v1+.

## Integration target

This resolution should inform project creation, ValueAntivalueProfile, common-good impact declarations, affected-party channels, evidence needs, fiscalization, Planning Scope review, and complaint paths.
