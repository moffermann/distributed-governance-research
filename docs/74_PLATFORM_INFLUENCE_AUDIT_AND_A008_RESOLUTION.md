## Resolution decision

A008 is founded and does not distort the project when it prevents hidden platform authority. The project rejects the distorted conclusions that algorithms should become sovereign deciders or that no platform/AI assistance should be used. The accepted resolution is a platform-influence audit layer.

## Rule added to Core v0

Material platform or AI influence should be recorded, explainable, correctable, and auditable when it may affect validation, publication, discovery, funding, evidence, complaints, disbursement, reputation, closure, or rule changes.

Minimum audit fields:

- AI assistance reference where material;
- recommendation or urgent-slot reason;
- ordering or discovery rule;
- exposure-to-funding influence metrics;
- correction history for misleading summaries;
- distinction between technical implementation change and governance change.

## Macul example

If an AI summary repeatedly frames accessibility objections as minor local resistance while highlighting sports benefits and funding urgency, the source basis, summary generation, correction path, and exposure impact should be reviewable.

## Citizen simplicity

Citizens should see why a project appears and be able to switch ordering modes. They should not need to inspect the full ranking audit unless they choose to.

## Transparency and accountability effect

The platform remains a coordinator and assistance layer, not a hidden allocator or hidden evaluator.

## Residual risks

- UI emphasis can bias attention even under visible rules.
- Model bias may be hard to detect.
- Exposure metrics may be technically complex.

## Integration target

This resolution should inform AssistedDeliberationContext, Home/Discovery, recommendation surfaces, AI assistance traces, SystemImplementationChange, and Layer 5 audit trail.
