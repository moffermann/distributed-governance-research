## Resolution decision

A031 is partially founded. Allocation can become an identity battleground: funding and defunding turn into tribal signaling, coordinated brigade-funding and boycott campaigns mobilize around projects, and value/antivalue declarations become culture-war terrain, while aggregate signals cannot by themselves distinguish a broadly legitimate project from a well-mobilized one. But expressive, identity-driven allocation is legitimate citizen choice, and the platform's job is not to police motive. This resolution fixes a motive-neutrality boundary and adds mobilization-pattern observability.

Under [[P007-integrate-or-bound-rule|knowledge/principles/P007-integrate-or-bound-rule.md]], this is a bounded resolution. Polarization threatens the social-sovereignty-over-value claim, so it cannot be ignored, but the control fits existing objects — antivalue management, affected-party evidence, threshold policies, funding-target closure, and administrative observability — and the residual is a deliberation-quality problem, not a missing mechanism. A platform that discounted "tribal" support would arrogate to itself exactly the authority over value that P002 denies it, so the fight is kept materially accountable rather than suppressed.

## Rule added to Core v0

Citizens' reasons for funding are not platform business, and material effects stay gated identically regardless of motive. The platform does not police motive, moderate viewpoint, or attach effects to any polarization score.

Minimum motive-neutrality boundary:

- reasons for funding are outside the platform's authority (P002), so no motive-policing and no viewpoint moderation of allocation;
- material effects stay gated identically regardless of motive — antivalue declarations route through measurement and fiscalization, affected-party evidence and threshold policies bind, and a contested antivalue supports a complaint only if someone files one;
- funding-bloc correlation observability — synchronized timing, shared origin, reciprocal brigading across paired projects — as research and administrative data only, without discounting either side's signals;
- funding-target closure (H035) bounds brigade amplitude on any single project, so a faction cannot pour unbounded funding into a favorite to overwhelm a rival.

## Macul example

A Macul migrant-run football school and a rival club's facility become proxies in an identity dispute; one faction brigades the migrant project with contested "noise" and "safety" antivalue declarations while the other counter-mobilizes brigade funding. Both projects keep their expressive support and objections. What the system adds is a mobilization-pattern read for observers and unchanged evidence gating: the antivalue claims still route through measurement and fiscalization, so tribal funding buys feasibility but not a waiver from the noise data or the closure rule.

## Citizen simplicity

Citizens should be free to fund for any reason they hold, and should see that a well-supported project is financially viable, not thereby certified as broadly legitimate, and that a flooded objection count does not by itself defund anyone.

## Transparency and accountability effect

A well-brigaded project is not read as broadly legitimate, because mobilization patterns are visible to observers, and a contested antivalue cannot defund a rival by assertion, because material effects run through evidence identically no matter who mobilized.

## Scope boundary and limitation

Core v0 does no motive policing, no viewpoint moderation of allocation, and attaches no platform effect to any polarization score; mobilization-pattern indicators are observability only. Deliberation-surface protections and contextual protected identity are supported but do not judge why citizens act.

Limitation statement: expressive allocation can still shift aggregate spending toward symbolic battlegrounds, and a hostile comment surface can drive out moderates; the architecture guarantees the fights stay materially accountable, not that they stop being fights.

## Residual risks

- Aggregate signals still cannot fully separate diffuse judgment from reciprocal mobilization, and mobilization-pattern indicators can themselves be gamed or contested.
- Deliberation quality degrades under polarization even when material effects are contained.
- Minority or out-group projects can lose funding to identity mobilization regardless of their declared value.

## Integration target

This resolution should inform social sovereignty over value (P002), value icons and antivalue-ceiling declarations (H012), support and justified-objection signals (H026), the common-good impact declaration (C022), the funding-target closure rule (H035), and the comment and deliberation surface.
