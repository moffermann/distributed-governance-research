# Participation Equity Indicators and A009 Resolution v0

## Status

Accepted Phase 3 resolution.

Paired review:

## Resolution decision

A009 is founded and does not distort the project when it improves participation capacity without demanding perfect equality before any distributed participation is allowed. The accepted resolution is a participation-equity indicator layer.

## Rule added to Core v0

Formal openness is insufficient. The system should expose participation concentration, affected-party gaps, and participation-support risks where material.

Minimum indicators:

- affected-party gap signal for high-impact projects;
- participation concentration by territory, beneficiary group, role, or delegate cluster;
- participation-support funding visibility;
- warnings when support providers also receive delegation or recommend projects;
- protected-identity usage and abuse monitoring;
- citizen-facing prompt when relevant affected voices appear missing.

## Macul example

If sports clubs mobilize quickly while older residents near the courts never saw the project, the project can show high funding support and still carry an `affected-party gap` signal. A help-desk project may assist older residents, but it must disclose relationships to delegates or project proponents.

## Citizen simplicity

Ordinary citizens see simple participation signals. Detailed demographic, protected-identity, or capacity data should be privacy-aware and visible only at the appropriate layer.

## Transparency and accountability effect

This makes participation inequality visible without turning Core v0 into a complete social-policy engine.

## Residual risks

- Participation gaps may be difficult to measure without sensitive data.
- Support providers may still steer users informally.
- Some low-participation groups require offline outreach beyond the platform.

## Integration target

This resolution should inform citizen interface layers, participation-support projects, delegation, protected identity, complaint/evidence flows, and administrative observability.
