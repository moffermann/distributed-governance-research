# Participation Equity Boundary and A009 Resolution v0

## Status

Accepted Phase 3 resolution.

Paired review:

## Resolution decision

A009 is founded as a comparative and observability problem, not as a reason to demand perfect participation before distributed participation is allowed.

Current institutional practice also suffers from unequal participation: physical neighborhood meetings, municipal consultations, emails, WhatsApp messages, working-hour sessions, winter evening meetings, family representation, and low attendance routinely exclude older adults, disabled people, workers, caregivers, digitally disconnected residents, and less organized affected parties.

Core v0 should improve that baseline through asynchronous discovery, nearby-project following, protected identity, simple citizen actions, delegation visibility, required affected-party evidence where threshold policy demands it, ordinary participation-support projects, and auditability. It should not pretend to solve all participation inequality in v0.

The accepted resolution is a participation-equity boundary, not a new Core v0 entity or mandatory indicator layer.

## Rule added to Core v0

Formal openness is insufficient, but Core v0 does not require full representativeness, universal demographic measurement, or perfect inclusion before projects can proceed.

Core v0 rule:

- participation inequality should be treated as a visible risk or observability concern where material, not as automatic veto or proof of illegitimacy;
- funding, support, delegation weight, discovery visibility, or meeting attendance must not be presented as full affected-party representation;
- where an active Threshold Policy requires affected-party mapping, consultation, observation windows, or readiness evidence, missing required participation evidence may block or condition publication, execution-ready status, disbursement, or closure under that policy;
- where no such requirement applies, low participation, missing voices, or concentration signals may inform warnings, assisted deliberation, administrative review, research, or future policy improvement, but they do not automatically block the project; ^r6f43bd5d
- participation-support projects remain ordinary transparent projects with budget, executor responsibility, fulfillment/control evidence, fiscalization, comments, complaints, and auditability;
- a support provider that also receives delegation, recommends projects, receives project funding, or has related-party ties should disclose those relationships where material;
- protected identity may support vulnerable participation under C024, but it must not become anonymous formal power;
- administrative, authority, research, or country implementations may add privacy-aware participation-equity indicators, but those are Extension v1+ or implementation observability, not a new Core v0 layer. ^rd61b0f81

## Macul example

If sports clubs mobilize quickly while older residents, disabled users, or nearby workers never attend a neighborhood meeting, that is not a new failure created by the platform; it is common in current practice.

Core v0 improves the baseline because a Macul resident can discover nearby projects asynchronously, follow the project, submit an observation, use protected identity where justified, delegate, fund complaint review, or use a participation-support project without attending one physical meeting at one time.

If the active Threshold Policy requires affected-party mapping, plan presentation, accessibility consultation, or neighborhood observations before execution-ready status, missing required evidence remains a real blocker. If the policy does not require it, low visible participation may still be shown as a warning, administrative observability signal, or future review input, but not as an automatic veto.

A help-desk project may assist older residents, but it must disclose relationships to delegates, project proponents, executors, or organizations it recommends where material.

## Citizen simplicity

Ordinary citizens see simple warnings where participation limits matter, such as `consultation evidence pending`, `affected-party issue pending`, or `participation-support relationship disclosed`. Detailed demographic, protected-identity, or capacity data should be privacy-aware and visible only at the appropriate layer.

## Transparency and accountability effect

This makes participation inequality visible where it matters while recognizing that Core v0 improves the current participation baseline rather than solving every social, digital, disability, age, time, mobility, and organizational barrier.

## Residual risks

- Participation gaps may be difficult to measure without sensitive data.
- Support providers may still steer users informally.
- Some low-participation groups require offline outreach beyond the platform.
- Authorities or researchers may need additional privacy-aware indicators to evaluate whether the implementation is improving participation over time.

## Integration target

This resolution should inform citizen interface layers, participation-support projects, delegation, protected identity, complaint/evidence flows, Project Legitimacy Profile handling where Threshold Policy requires participation evidence, and administrative observability.
