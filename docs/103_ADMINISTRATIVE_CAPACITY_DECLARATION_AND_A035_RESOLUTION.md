## Resolution decision

A035 is founded as a deployment precondition and does not distort the project when read comparatively: the review labor it prices already exists — an official approves budgets and projects today, unmeasured — and the model re-platforms that work under declared windows and public attribution rather than creating it. The accepted resolution is an Administrative Capacity Declaration per tutored scope, with capacity-calibrated timeouts and bounded authority-side assistance.

Under [[P007-integrate-or-bound-rule|knowledge/principles/P007-integrate-or-bound-rule.md]], this integrates through existing objects — the Planning Scope carries the declaration exactly as it carries the Allocation Mandate and Fiscal Commitment Profile, timeout policies are already configurable per operating mode, the AI-assistance boundary already exists — and bounds quantification: no staffing number is empirical until the first pilot measures one, and deployment economics remain a country-implementation matter, not an argument this corpus makes.

## Rule added to Core v0

Every tutored Planning Scope publishes an Administrative Capacity Declaration alongside its mandate and fiscal profile.

Minimum elements:

- expected review volume, staffing assignment, and per-resolution effort assumptions, published and versioned with the scope;
- timeout policies calibrated to the declared capacity and never copied between scopes with different declarations;
- authority-side AI assistance for resolution drafting, admissibility checklists, and window tracking, inside the existing boundary: AI assists, the responsible authority decides, every decision attributable;
- review-window performance (decisions in window, lapses, timeout outcomes) joined to the functional transition maturity metrics;
- hours-per-resolution measurement by resolution type committed as an explicit first-pilot deliverable — the corpus's first empirical staffing data.

## Macul example

Commune X publishes: one assigned reviewer, forty expected reviews per quarter, visibility-only timeouts. Reviews lapse in month two; the public record shows lapses against a declared one-reviewer capacity, and the corrective discussion is about the staffing line, not about the model. The neighboring commune declared two reviewers with escalation timeouts and shows clean windows — the difference is visible as capacity, not as ideology.

## Citizen simplicity

Citizens see review deadlines and whether decisions arrived on time; a lapse shows who owed the decision and what the timeout policy did. The staffing declaration itself is one public line on the scope, not citizen homework.

## Transparency and accountability effect

A lapsed deadline never produces an anonymous system action: it produces a public Review Timeout Resolution naming the responsible authority, dates, decision result, and applicable policy. The machinery attributes non-decision to who failed to decide — the software is never the responsible party — and the capacity declaration ensures an unfunded post reads as an unfunded post, not as model failure.

## Scope boundary and limitation

Core v0 does not model staffing economics, does not prescribe headcounts, and does not argue implementation costs: operating capacity is absorbed and budgeted by the installing authority as country implementation. The publishable model is an architecture paper, not a deployment prospectus.

Limitation statement: until a pilot measures hours per resolution type, every capacity declaration is an assumption, and the corpus contains no empirical basis for sizing one; the declaration's function is to make that assumption public and falsifiable, not to replace the measurement.

## Residual risks

- An authority may under-declare capacity deliberately as an incumbent-resistance throttle wearing an operational excuse (A016 pattern).
- Effort assumptions may be gamed optimistic to ease approval, then produce lapse cascades anyway.
- Authority-side AI drafting may drift toward de facto deciding if attribution discipline weakens.

## Integration target

This resolution should inform Planning Scope records, Governance Resolution and Review Timeout Resolution configuration, operating-mode timeout policies, the AI assistance boundary, functional transition maturity metrics, and first-pilot instrumentation.
