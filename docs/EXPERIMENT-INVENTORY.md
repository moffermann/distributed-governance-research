# Experiment inventory and forward plan — Core v0 computational program

**Purpose.** A single canonical map of every computational experiment: what it measures, which code
implements it, its current status, and the ordered plan for closing the program. Written to stop us
re-discovering this each session. Author-authoritative framing (author intent overrides stale corpus
text where they conflict). Last updated 2026-07-12.

Status legend: **CURRENT** = live evidence path, publication-ready or in progress · **CLOSED** =
finding validated and reported (engine may be legacy) · **LEGACY** = pre-v1.14 engine, reproduction-only
(behind `E4_ALLOW_LEGACY`) · **RETIRED** = the calibrated compound is withdrawn · **PENDING** = not yet
built.

---

## The two layer vocabularies (read this first)

The project uses "layer" in two senses; keeping them apart resolves most of the confusion.

1. **The three functional layers of Core v0** (the architecture): **planning** (constructing the
   eligibility frame / macro categorization) → **selection/allocation** (routing budget to projects) →
   **delivery/execution** (getting the value out the door). All three have been part of Core v0's design
   since the corpus origin.
2. **The value-model factors** an earlier version multiplied into a single compound
   (`macro × allocation × delivery ≈ 1.83`). That single calibrated compound is **RETIRED** — not the
   layers themselves, only the "×N" packaging (the objection was: report each layer's effect as a
   percentage, not as a multiplier). Provenance of the factors:
   `research/e4e5-analytical-propositions.md:103-106`; retirement banners at line 4 of the `e4*`/`e4e5*`
   research files; master statements `drafts/paper.md:543-576`, `:1059-1063`.

## The E5 disambiguation

Two different apparatuses have carried the name "E5":

- **(a) Original E5 — Selection × Delivery** (`research/e5-value-delivery-design.md`). A 2×2
  (selection regime × delivery regime, arms S/A1/A2/A3) measuring **delivered social value**
  `V = Σ θ·delivered_fraction·budget`, Okun's leaky-bucket frame, leakage band anchored to
  **87% Uganda capitation grants (Reinikka & Svensson 2004)** and **~24% Indonesian roads (Olken 2007)**.
  This is the real "aggregate value at equal budget" experiment. Its Finding-5 numbers are in the paper
  (delivery +43%, selection +53–54%, interaction +0.085) **but the single compound is RETIRED** and its
  engine (`e5-sp-model.mjs`, `e4e5-pipeline.mjs`) is now LEGACY.
- **(b) `e4-v5/e5-layers.mjs`** — a thin algebraic wrapper: it takes E4's selection efficiencies and
  multiplies two **default-off constants** (`κ`=admin cost, `leak`=diversion); it does **not** re-simulate
  delivery. In effect this is the scaffolding for the **future cost/leakage legs**, mislabeled "E5". It
  will be **re-labeled** as the cost-leg apparatus, not E5.

## The planning layer — author-authoritative note (corrects the ≤1× reading)

The recorded macro/planning factor (`≈1.05×`, `e4e5-analytical-propositions.md:106`; "≤1×, 0.76–0.99×",
`e4e5-value-model-v2.md:226`) is **contested by the author and must not be read as "the planning layer is
weak / a pruning candidate."** Reasons:

- Planning has **always** been a core Core v0 layer; it is in the corpus design from the start. It is not
  optional and not a parsimony candidate.
- The ≈1.05–1.08× number came from comparing **planning options within a shared frame** — distributed
  planning acting as the politician's *insurance against making a mistake* — **not** the correct contrast.
- The correct contrast has **never been run**: a **fully-modeled central planner** (harm-blind and
  politically biased — in the worst case destroying value across *entire categories*) vs **Core v0's
  distributed, fully-open planning**. Under that contrast the planning layer is expected to matter much
  more; whether it composes additively or multiplicatively is for the experiment (E9) to decide.
- The satellite carries the exploratory distributed-planning result; the **honest full measurement is the
  not-yet-built E9** (below). Until E9, the planning layer's value contribution is **UNRESOLVED**, not
  "≤1×."

## Forward plan (ordered)

1. **E5 — close it on the E4 base (NOW).** Rebuild **Selection × Delivery** on the clean v1.14 engine
   (like E4). Report each layer **separately and jointly**, as **percentages** (no compound multiplier),
   and test whether they compose **additively or multiplicatively** (literature suggests multiplicative;
   the experiment decides). **No costs.** Re-label `e5-layers.mjs` as the future cost-leg scaffolding.
2. **E9 — the full-stack comparison (NEXT, new).** Correctly modeled, end to end:
   **central planning + central selection + central delivery** vs **Core v0 full: distributed planning +
   distributed allocation + distributed delivery.** This is where the **planning layer** finally gets its
   honest measurement (see the note above). Depends on the E4 re-frame and E5 being closed.
3. **E10 — E9 + costs (new).** Add the administrative-cost dimension (the `κ` machinery cost that breaks
   the "equal budget" framing), and leakage as appropriate, on top of E9. This absorbs the current
   `e5-layers.mjs` cost scaffolding and the `ADMIN-COST-LEG.md` / `LEAKAGE-CORRUPTION-LEG.md` specs.
4. **Headlines + paper corrections (LAST).** Only after E5/E9/E10 are closed do we write the final
   headlines and propagate corrections into the master (and cite the satellite where appropriate).

**Roadmap implication:** this **re-sequences `docs/ROADMAP.md`**, which currently puts the
administrative-cost leg (`m_admin`) as the immediate next track under a "strengthen-first, deposit sooner"
strategy. Under this plan, costs move **after** E5 and E9 (they become E10). The ROADMAP should be updated
to match once this plan is confirmed.
