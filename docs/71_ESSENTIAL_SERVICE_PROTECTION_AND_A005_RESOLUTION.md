## Resolution decision

A005 is founded and does not distort the project when it preserves bounded distributed allocation. The project rejects the distorted conclusions that all public services must be centrally operated or that all public budgets should be citizen-assignable. The accepted resolution is an `Essential Service Protection` test for Planning Scopes and funding eligibility.

## Rule added to Core v0

Planning Scopes should identify whether a project or scope affects essential guarantees, continuity, emergency capacity, redistribution, vulnerable beneficiaries, universal access, or low-visibility groups.

The test should record:

- whether the function belongs partly or wholly to the non-assignable common pool;
- what layer is public/legal guarantee and what layer is service provision;
- whether citizen funding is assignable, default-assigned, reserve-backed, or excluded;
- underfunding indicators for low-visibility eligible needs;
- cream-skimming risks and beneficiary-protection conditions.

## Macul example

Sports infrastructure may be eligible for a sports pilot, but elder-care continuity, disability services, emergency reserves, or rights guarantees should not silently depend on sports-project popularity. The Planning Scope should show which services are protected outside monthly attention dynamics.

## Citizen simplicity

Citizens should see simple scope labels such as `eligible for civic allocation`, `protected common-pool function`, `reserve-backed continuity need`, or `underfunded protected scope`.

## Transparency and accountability effect

This prevents authorities or project promoters from moving hard obligations into citizen popularity dynamics without visibility.

## Residual risks

- The exact common-pool boundary is country implementation.
- Authorities may define protected scopes too narrowly or too broadly.
- Low-visibility needs may still require outreach and participation-capacity support.

## Integration target

This resolution should inform PlanningScope, ThresholdPolicy, civic wallet scope, common-pool design, default allocation profiles, essential-service pilots, and administrative observability.
