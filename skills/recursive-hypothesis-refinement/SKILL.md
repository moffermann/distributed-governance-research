# Recursive Hypothesis Refinement Skill

## Purpose

Use this workflow when developing the Distributed Governance Research project or any similar conceptual research project.

## Core rule

Do not convert an idea into theory until it has been attacked, refined, and classified.

## Workflow

1. **Capture the idea**
   - Restate it clearly.
   - Remove rhetorical excess.

2. **Convert to hypothesis**
   - Make it falsifiable or at least criticizable.
   - Identify the functional claim.

3. **Identify assumptions**
   - What must be true for this idea to work?

4. **Attack the hypothesis**
   - Look for edge cases.
   - Look for failure modes.
   - Look for cases where the opposite may be true.

5. **Refine**
   - Narrow the claim.
   - Add conditions.
   - Separate functions or categories.

6. **Classify**
   - hypothesis;
   - concept;
   - objection;
   - open question;
   - literature task;
   - draft text.

7. **Persist**
   - If the idea survived, store it in the repository.
   - If it did not survive, store the objection or failed hypothesis.

## Style

Be rigorous, not promotional. Prefer precise distinctions over persuasive language.

## Warning

Do not write a paper too early. First build the theory.
