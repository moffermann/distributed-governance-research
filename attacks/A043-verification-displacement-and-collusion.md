# A043 - The Verification Layer Under Machines and Rings: Displacement, Blind Trust, and Collusion

## Status

Reviewed in paired review. Resolved in [[113_VERIFICATION_PACKAGE_AND_A043_RESOLUTION|docs/113_VERIFICATION_PACKAGE_AND_A043_RESOLUTION.md]] (author verdict 2026-07-07). Paired defense: [[D043-verification-displacement-and-collusion|defenses/D043-verification-displacement-and-collusion.md]].

## Description

Cheap machine verification is the obvious next move — and it breaks three corpus assumptions at once. First, a near-zero-price verifier **displaces** the human fiscalizer market that H016 imagines and that supplies the machine's only audit; a fully automated first instance starves the profession that measures the machine. Second, an **imperfect** machine could wave fraud through — and worse, a single strict prompt makes some models reject nearly all honest work while others pass fraud, so the naive "just add a model" fails. Third, and deepest: a **colluding ring** that controls an executor, its verifiers, and its citizen-evidence contributors bypasses the entire verification layer — the machine clears its own, no contradiction is raised, and the per-milestone deterrence inequality is never even consulted.

## Problem

The panel measured that machine verification of judgment-requiring fraud is imperfect and correlated across model families (ensembles fail worse than independence predicts), that operating points are steerable by instruction and evidence richness, and that a healthy system's outcome audit is blind — so drift and capture are undetectable without seeded controls. Experiment G then measured that collusion is the *only* attack to move leakage by an order of magnitude, because it is not a per-milestone bet the cascade can deter. The verification layer, left naive, is displaceable, blindly trusting, and collusion-fragile.

## Recommended resolution path

- Machine triage with humans as the permanent, budget-floored second instance; the lax accuracy requirement conditioned on a positive docs/111 margin.
- Universal exposure with unpredictable selection; the mandatory set includes the shared-blind-spot flaw classes; coverage value-weighted.
- Seeded positive controls as the epistemic instrument, conditioned on their indistinguishability (real-project stings, secret rotated manifold, separated seeder).
- Contraposed citizen evidence as a control instrument, bounded by contributor independence and non-suppressive confirmation.
- Cross-layer collusion resistance as first-class: verified beneficial ownership, contributor Sybil-resistance, decentralized adversarially-reviewed assigner/seeder/floor-setter.

## Theoretical base and citations

- Milgrom & Roberts (1990), complementarities — the verification instruments fail jointly.
- Ostrom (1990), graduated sanctions monitored by the community that depends on them — contraposition, mechanized, and its capture failure mode.
- The audit profession's proficiency-testing / stings literature — seeded controls and their evasion (the Dieselgate class).

## Social reasons

If the machine displaces the human profession that measures it, the system loses the ability to know its own error rate; if collusion is unpriced, the architecture is safe on paper and captured in practice. Citizens are protected only if the watching stays distributed (contraposition) and the small professional corps is capture-resistant.

## Inconsistencies to test

- H016 assumes a living human control market; near-free machine verification erases the *paid routine-verification* form of it — the ecosystem must migrate, not survive as-is.
- The corpus's "reputation informs, never excludes" guarantees a colluding ring is never locked out; combined with nominal (uncollectable) penalties and shell-cycling, deterrence is defeated for the ring.

## Stress scenario

A ring builds clean reputation for three years, positions trusted executors and captured auditors, poisons the shared market-price reference, and defects en masse in the published post-model-change window — swamping the 15–25% human corps in a single period while every automated layer reports clean.

## Review checklist

- Are humans the permanent second instance, funded by an independently-set floor?
- Is the automatic lane gated on a positive published margin?
- Is selection unpredictable, coverage value-weighted, the blind-spot classes mandatory?
- Are seeded controls indistinguishable and separately operated?
- Is contributor independence established and confirmation non-suppressive?
- Is beneficial ownership verified and the assigner/seeder/floor-setter decentralized?

## Resolution output

Resolved in docs/113: machine triage + human second instance (six rules), with the machine's lax-accuracy safety net conditioned on the docs/111 margin, contraposition bounded by contributor independence, and cross-layer collusion resistance made first-class.
