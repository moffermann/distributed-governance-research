# Literature Map

This is an initial map of related research areas. It is not yet a formal literature review.

## Closest fields

- Polycentric governance
- Network governance
- Digital governance
- Blockchain governance
- Public choice theory
- Institutional economics
- Mechanism design
- Participatory democracy
- Liquid democracy
- Digital identity and e-government
- Decentralized autonomous organizations
- Arbitration and private dispute resolution
- Trust infrastructure

## Initial observation

The specific combination developed in the conversation does not appear to be captured by a single existing frame:

- functional decomposition of state institutions;
- distribution criteria based on the nature of the function;
- distributed resource allocation;
- institutional competition and auditability;
- distributed trust as a general architecture;
- AI as coordinator or personal guide, not central sovereign;
- selective centralization for defense and coercive security.

## Research task

Find papers that support, contradict, or partially anticipate each component.

## Phase 3 attack literature anchors

The Phase 3 attack queue uses the following literature families as initial theoretical anchors. This is still not a full literature review; each attack resolution should later create more precise source notes where needed.

### Legitimacy, participation, and public reason

- Jurgen Habermas, `Between Facts and Norms` (1996).
- John Rawls, `Political Liberalism` (1993).
- Robert Dahl, `Polyarchy` (1971) and `Who Governs?` (1961).
- Sherry Arnstein, "A Ladder of Citizen Participation" (1969).
- Archon Fung, "Varieties of Participation in Complex Governance" (2006).
- James Fishkin, `When the People Speak` (2009).

### Institutions, incentives, and capture

- Douglass North, `Institutions, Institutional Change and Economic Performance` (1990).
- Oliver Williamson, `The Economic Institutions of Capitalism` (1985).
- George Stigler, "The Theory of Economic Regulation" (1971).
- Jean-Jacques Laffont and Jean Tirole, "The Politics of Government Decision-Making" (1991).
- Mancur Olson, `The Logic of Collective Action` (1965) and `The Rise and Decline of Nations` (1982).
- Michael Jensen and William Meckling, "Theory of the Firm" (1976).

### Information, evidence, and audit

- Friedrich Hayek, "The Use of Knowledge in Society" (1945).
- George Akerlof, "The Market for Lemons" (1970).
- Mark Bovens, "Analysing and Assessing Accountability" (2007).
- Michael Power, `The Audit Society` (1997).
- National Research Council, `Strengthening Forensic Science in the United States` (2009).
- Daubert v. Merrell Dow Pharmaceuticals, 509 U.S. 579 (1993).
- ISO/IEC 17025.
- NIST SP 800-86, `Guide to Integrating Forensic Techniques into Incident Response` (2006).

### Metrics, agency, and disbursement incentives

- Charles Goodhart, "Problems of Monetary Management" (1975).
- Donald Campbell, "Assessing the Impact of Planned Social Change" (1976).
- Bengt Holmstrom, "Moral Hazard and Observability" (1979).
- Bengt Holmstrom and Paul Milgrom, "Multitask Principal-Agent Analyses" (1991).

### Algorithmic accountability and platform power

- Joshua Kroll et al., "Accountable Algorithms" (2017).

### Fiscal federalism, commons, and essential services

- Richard Musgrave, `The Theory of Public Finance` (1959).
- Wallace Oates, `Fiscal Federalism` (1972).
- Elinor Ostrom, `Governing the Commons` (1990).

### Delegation and concentration

- Robert Michels, `Political Parties` (1911).
- Christian Blum and Christina Isabel Zuber, "Liquid Democracy: Potentials, Problems, and Perspectives" (2016).
- Anson Kahng, Simon Mackenzie, and Ariel Procaccia, "Liquid Democracy: An Algorithmic Perspective" (2018).

## Phase 3 research caution

These references should be used to strengthen attacks, not to decorate the theory. Each source may also weaken the project by showing limits of participation, audit, delegation, metric governance, and institutional transition.
