> a later pre-registered **symmetry gate (NO-GO)** showed the distributed selection advantage is small
> (~0.026 of a full-information benchmark, below its 0.05 gate), not a ~2× headline. E8's behavioral
> adoption findings stand as an *earlier apparatus* result only. Controlling spec:

Registered 2026-07-06, before implementation. Author approvals on record: the

## Question

E7 established the headline V(A2)/V(S′) = 2.224 [2.103, 2.346] at Np=200 with
*imposed* participation: distributed arms at d=0.8 (default share) and
pi=0.3 (informed share), constant across cycles. The behavioral adoption
experiment now generates those quantities endogenously — adoption trajectories,
active shares, delegation, informed composition — from a Core v0-conformant
behavioral model calibrated by LLM-elicited priors.

```text
Does the manuscript's headline survive when the participation side of the
architecture is generated by behavior instead of assumed?
```

## What crosses the bridge, and what does not

**Crosses (participation side only):**

| E7 quantity | Behavioral source (per seed, per tick, resampled 104 ticks → 24 cycles) |
|---|---|
| d_t (default share) | 1 − active_user_share(t) — Core v0 semantics: every non-active share follows the default rule (docs/101) |
| pi_t (informed share among actives) | (direct_pop(t) + delegator_share(t)) ÷ active_user_share(t), capped at 0.95 — the approved delegation→informed fold; direct_pop = direct_participation_share × registered_user_share |
| piInformed (world signal carriers) | plateau mean (last third of the run) of direct_pop + delegator_share — preserves the engine's coupling between signal-holding and informed allocation |

**Does not cross (declared):** the executor side of E7 — CALIBRATED
(p=0.35, a=0.5, r=0.3, R=0) stays audit-anchored per docs/105; OPAQUE and
VERIFIED unchanged; planner bandwidths, SIGMA parameters, scarcity, discovery
surface unchanged; central arms (S, S′, A1′) consume no participation and must
reproduce E7's values exactly at equal seeds — a built-in audit check.

Profile-driven citizens map to the uninformed-active channel (salience
discovery surface), the closest mechanism the paper engine offers; declared
approximation.

## Design

- **Populations** (behavioral scenarios, 10 seeds each): `llm_calibrated`
  (primary — best-grounded priors), `baseline` (synthetic priors), and
  `high_friction_launch` (adoption stress).
- **Variants**: `trajectory` (per-cycle d_t, pi_t from the actual launch
  curve — adoption starts near zero and grows; the complete case) and
  `steady-state` (plateau means held constant — comparability with E7's
  imposed-constant design).
- **Arms and scales**: the full E7 set (S, S′, A1′, A3′, A2) at
  Np ∈ {10, 20, 40, 200}; 20 paper seeds × 10 behavioral seeds = 200 paired
  ratios per cell.
- **Engine**: a new self-contained `--exp e8` block in `allocation-sim.mjs`
  reading `research/e8-behavioral-inputs.json` (generated, committed, with
  provenance); E1–E7 outputs must remain byte-identical before and after the
  block is added.

## Contrast with what E7 imposed

The behavioral populations are more active and more informed than E7's
distributed-arm assumptions: plateau d ≈ 0.50–0.65 (E7: 0.8) and pi ≈ 0.6–0.8
(E7: 0.3), with high-friction approaching E7's passivity (d ≈ 0.80). E8
therefore tests the headline on both sides of E7's operating point.

## Pre-registered predictions

- **P1.** Steady-state, `llm_calibrated`, Np=200: V(A2)/V(S′) lands in
  [1.9, 2.8] — at or above E7's 2.22, because richer informed participation
  improves distributed selection while the calibrated status quo gains nothing.
- **P2.** The launch trajectory costs less than 25% of the steady-state ratio
  at Np=200 and never pushes it below 1.4: early thin participation is
  absorbed by the default layer (docs/101), which anchors selection while
  adoption grows.
- **P3.** Under `high_friction_launch`, the ratio stays ≥ 1.5 at every scale —
  the behavioral analog of E7's municipal band (1.4–1.6).
- **P4 (audit check, not a prediction).** Central arms reproduce E7's per-seed
  values exactly at equal (seed, Np); any deviation means the bridge touched
  something it declared untouched.

  *Implementation note (2026-07-06, before the run):* under a behavioral
  piInformed ≠ 0.3 the world's signal-carrier composition — and hence the RNG
  stream feeding the planner vectors — legitimately differs from E7's, so
  exactness is verified through a control cell that runs the E8 machinery with
  E7's exact constants (d=0.8, pi=0.3, piInformed=0.3, Np=200) and must
  reproduce E7's main table verbatim.

## Interpretation discipline

Behavioral populations carry llm-elicited synthetic priors (no human data);
signals are honest by construction on both sides of the bridge (the docs/87
elicitation problem stays open); a favorable E8 result strengthens the
robustness section of the manuscript, not its empirical standing.
