# Audit-Institution Evidence Base for the E7 Calibrated Baseline

## Status

Evidence base assembled 2026-07-04 under the rule of [[105_CALIBRATED_BASELINE_EVIDENCE_AND_A036_RESOLUTION|docs/105_CALIBRATED_BASELINE_EVIDENCE_AND_A036_RESOLUTION.md]]: the E7 institutional-baseline arm (S′) must be parameterized from published findings of supreme audit institutions and ex-post evaluation bodies, spanning several countries and political orientations, with per-item citations, category matching (construction/infrastructure findings calibrate construction arms; transfer/social-program findings calibrate transfer arms), a declared band, and the chosen operating point stated in the E7 write-up — not here. This document declares the band; the operating points are selected and justified when E7 is implemented, and the author reviews the mapping before any run.

Method: three parallel research sweeps (Andean: Chile, Peru, Colombia; Atlantic Latin America: Brazil, Mexico, Argentina; high-capacity comparators: United States, United Kingdom, European Union), each instructed to report only findings attributable to a named audit or evaluation institution, to tag category explicitly, and to label verification status honestly. Items are labeled **[P]** where the primary institutional source was read directly, and **[S]** where the figure is corroborated across multiple outlets citing the institution but the primary document was not opened; every [S] item carrying weight in the E7 parameterization enters the verification queue below before formal citation.

Declared bias, both directions, per docs/105: audit reports measure *detected* deficiencies — they may understate leakage audit never finds (the ECA reports that the cohesion control system itself underperforms; the GAO warns its improper-payment totals are likely understated), and may overstate typical performance by sampling programs already suspected of failure (Fiscobras selects works by risk). The band is a synthesis of published findings, not a measurement of any specific administration.

## High-capacity floor (United States, United Kingdom, European Union)

These jurisdictions anchor the *lower* end of the band: functioning, well-resourced administrations still exhibiting measurable error, leakage, and non-delivery — which is what makes the band a floor rather than an accusation.

### United States — GAO and related bodies

- **[P]** GAO, *Improper Payments: Fiscal Year 2024 government-wide estimate* (GAO-25-107753, 2025). Category: mixed/transfers (68 programs). ~US$162 billion improper payments, ≈2.4% of outlays; ~84% overpayments; 18 programs above 10%, six above 20%. Informs: aggregate execution-deficiency floor. <https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-25-107753>
- **[P]** TIGTA/IRS, *EITC improper payment rate FY2023* (TIGTA 2024-40-026). Category: transfer (refundable credit, weak eligibility verification). **33.5%** improper (US$21.9 B); the 23–34% range has held since FY2003. Informs: transfer-leakage ceiling *within* high capacity — leakage follows verifiability, not geography. Cite as FY2023. <https://www.tigta.gov/sites/default/files/reports/2024-05/2024400026fr.pdf>
- **[P]** CMS, *Medicaid improper payment rate FY2024*: 5.09% (US$31.1 B), down from 8.58%. Category: transfer (complex eligibility). Informs: mid-band transfer deficiency. <https://www.cms.gov/newsroom/fact-sheets/fiscal-year-2024-improper-payments-fact-sheet>
- **[P]** GAO, *Weapon Systems Annual Assessment* (GAO-25-107569, 2025). Category: large capital projects (analogue, not civil works — category-matched with caution). Portfolio cost growth ~US$484 B over initial estimates; average delay 31 months. Informs: capital-project overrun and reported-vs-real schedule.
- **[S]** GAO improper-payment series context: FY2025 ~US$186 B (GAO-26-108694); FY2023 ~US$236 B (GAO-24-106927); cumulative since FY2003 ~US$2.8 trillion, with GAO's own understatement warning. Informs: temporal stability of the floor plus the declared detection bias.

### United Kingdom — NAO and DWP audited statistics

- **[P]** DWP (NAO-audited), *Fraud and error in the benefit system FYE 2024*. Category: transfers. Overpayment rate by program: system total **3.7%** (£9.7 B); Universal Credit **12.4%**; Housing Benefit 6.3%; PIP 0.4%; State Pension **0.1%**. Informs: the verifiability gradient inside one administration — 0.1% to 12.4% by program design. <https://www.gov.uk/government/statistics/fraud-and-error-in-the-benefit-system-financial-year-2023-to-2024-estimates/fraud-and-error-in-the-benefit-system-financial-year-ending-fye-2024>
- **[P]** Same series FYE 2025: total 3.3% (£9.5 B); Universal Credit 9.7%. Informs: trend and operating point.
- **[P]** NAO, *Tackling benefit overpayments due to fraud and error* (VfM study, 2025). Informs: detection and recovery capacity at high capacity.
- **[P]** NAO, *HS2 update following cancellation of Phase 2* (HC 128, 2024) and 2025 follow-up. Category: construction/infrastructure. Over £2.7 B written off across transport projects 2023-25; £2.1 B from the Phase 2 cancellation alone; unresolved cost-range disagreement between department and delivery body. Informs: infrastructure non-delivery and reported-vs-real cost at high capacity.

### European Union — European Court of Auditors

- **[P]** ECA, *Annual Report 2023*: estimated level of error **5.6%** overall (up from 4.2% in 2022) — material and pervasive, adverse opinion. By chapter: **Cohesion 9.3%** (construction-intensive — the high-capacity infrastructure anchor); **Natural Resources 2.2%** (direct payments — the simple-transfer anchor). Informs: category-matched error rates across 27 politically diverse member states. <https://op.europa.eu/webpub/eca/audit-in-brief-2023/en/>
- **[P]** ECA, *Annual Report 2022*: 4.2% overall (range 3.1–5.3%).
- **[S]** ECA, special report on the cohesion control system (2024): the control layers themselves underperform — the documented reason measured error is a lower bound on real leakage. Informs: the declared detection bias.
- **[S]** ECA, special report on TEN-T core-network delivery: core network unlikely to be operational by 2030; five of nine corridors at risk; underused infrastructure financed. Report number to be fixed from eca.europa.eu before formal citation. Informs: reported-vs-real delivery and durability of works.

## Latin American band (upper range)

### Brazil — TCU and CGU (the strongest systematic series)

- **[S→P queue]** TCU, *Diagnóstico de Obras Paralisadas* (operational audit and continuing panel, 2018-19 onward). Category: construction. Of ~21,000 federally funded works that should be in execution, **more than 8,600 (~41%) paralyzed or unfinished**, with R$8.2+ billion already invested in them. Informs: the reported-vs-real completion analogue for works — the single most direct calibration item for the construction arm's non-delivery share. <https://portal.tcu.gov.br/biblioteca-digital/auditoria-operacional-sobre-obras-paralisadas.htm>
- **[P]** TCU, *Fiscobras 2023* (27th annual works-audit plan). Category: construction. 31 works audited, **21 (67%) with indications of grave irregularity**, R$17 B audited volume; risk-based selection declared (sampling bias toward suspect works — stated, not hidden). Informs: grave-deficiency detection rate in audited works; the annual series supports a multi-year band. <https://portal.tcu.gov.br/data/pages/8A81881F8A588DD8018B975855A6641E.htm>
- **[S→P queue]** CGU, *Programa de Fiscalização em Entes Federativos* (municipal audit lotteries since 2003; >2,200 audits). Category: transfers/social programs. Reported synthesis: ~73% of audited administrations with irregularities; **~8.2% of federal transfers not applied as intended** — the direct candidate for the transfer arm's leakage level. Level figures are secondary until confirmed against the primary descriptive statistics in Avis, Ferraz and Finan (2018). <https://www.gov.br/cgu/pt-br/assuntos/auditoria-e-fiscalizacao/programa-de-fiscalizacao-em-entes-federativos>
- **[P]** Ferraz and Finan (2011, AER); Avis, Ferraz and Finan (2018, JPE). Verified *effects*: reelection incentives reduce misappropriation by 27%; being audited reduces future corruption ~8% and raises subsequent legal action ~20%. Informs: the detection→deterrence elasticity (mechanism evidence, not a level).

### Mexico — ASF (the recovery-parameter anchor)

- **[S]** ASF, *Informe General Ejecutivo, Cuenta Pública 2024* (federalized spending). Category: mixed, decomposable. 1,846 audits over a ~2.5-trillion-peso sample; ~65.2 billion pesos flagged for clarification (≈2.6% of the audited sample — an order-of-magnitude derivation, not an official ratio). Informs: detection rate on audited spending.
- **[S]** ASF, *recuperaciones operadas* series. Category: cross-cutting. Historical (CP 2007-2010): **64.2% of determined-recoverable amounts actually recovered**; recovery capacity down >80% since 2018, with recent sector recoveries in the low single digits of observed amounts. Informs: the recovery parameter r — a wide band whose denominators differ (determined-recoverable vs observed), to be handled explicitly in the E7 write-up. <https://www.asf.gob.mx/Section/62_Recuperaciones>
- **[P]** ASF, *Línea 12 Metro CDMX* audits (CP 2013, 2019, 2020). Category: construction. Original project cost +71% (15,290→26,200 M pesos); extension +44%; >11,000 defects detected. Informs: works cost-overrun band, upper anchor.

### Argentina — AGN and SIGEN (cases, not series — declared weakness)

- **[S]** AGN, *Soterramiento del Tren Sarmiento* audit (2024). Category: construction. Certified progress of 2.8% in 2017 on the nationally controlled section, reaching ~30% before paralysis, versus **85-90% completion on the sections financed and controlled by the World Bank** — the same work, different institutional control, different delivery: a direct empirical analogue of the corpus's institution-effect claim. ~US$759 M paid; AGN found prices in line with international references (no confirmed overpricing). Informs: reported-vs-real completion and the control-regime effect.
- **[S]** SIGEN, state-enterprise review (2024): ~50% of non-financial public enterprises with observations for irregularities or control failures. Category: public enterprises — kept separate, not blended with works or transfers.
- Argentine peso-denominated overrun figures (e.g., Sistema Riachuelo) are excluded from calibration for inflation contamination; only real/USD-denominated items usable. AGN has been institutionally headless since April 2025, limiting recent series.

### Chile — CGR and DIPRES (the pilot jurisdiction)

- **[P]** CEA-CGR, *Estudio sobre observaciones y recomendaciones en la ejecución de obras públicas* (2016; inspections 2012-2015). Category: construction. **Roughly one in two audited contracts with at least one relevant observation** at the highest-ratio entities; **MOP 34%** as the floor; anomalies raise final works cost. Informs: works deficiency/observation band ≈34-50% for the pilot jurisdiction. <https://www.ceacgr.cl/CEA/pdf/publicaciones/Estudio-OOPP-CGR.pdf>
- **[P/S]** DIPRES, *Evaluación de Programas Gubernamentales* (EPG/ex-post series). Category: programs/social. Historical aggregate: **17.3% of evaluated programs rated "desempeño insuficiente"** (official DIPRES category); 37.7% not satisfactorily meeting their purpose (academic aggregation over DIPRES data — labeled as such). Recent cohorts: 2024 evaluations ~47% low/insufficient; 2017 cohort 44%, with one round at 17 of 22. Informs: share of programs with negative ex-post evaluation, band ≈17% (core) to ≈44-47% (recent cohorts). <https://www.dipres.gob.cl/598/w3-article-341721.html>
- **[S]** CGR Chile, *Reporte de Hallazgos 2024-2025*: on the order of CLP 1.5 trillion in mismanaged funds flagged (aggregate magnitude; not normalized over audited amounts — context, not calibration).

### Peru and Colombia — the works non-delivery stock

- **[S→P queue]** CGR Perú, *Obras Paralizadas* series (Infobras). December 2023: **2,298 paralyzed works**; ~S/13 billion already spent (≈48% of their cost executed with no delivered use value). December 2024: 2,476 works, S/43.1 billion (definition: 6+ months without physical progress); causes led by contract breach (25.4%) and lack of funds (21.2%). Category: construction. Informs: reported-vs-real completion and the sunk-spend share of the works arm; two consecutive years give temporal robustness.
- **[S→P queue]** CGR Colombia, *elefantes blancos / obras inconclusas*. 2023: 1,753 works flagged (COP 15.2 trillion), of which **1,054 strictly unfinished "elefantes blancos" (COP 7.02 trillion)** — the strict subset is the calibration item; 2024: 1,700+ works ≈COP 6 trillion. Category: construction.
- **[S→P queue]** CGR Colombia, *VISR rural housing subsidies audit* (execution 2000-2019). Category: **transfers/social** — the one robust Colombian transfer item: of 10,000+ subsidies granted, **only ~24% culminated** in completed homes; fiscal findings COP ~9.7 billion, six with presumed penal implication. Informs: reported-vs-real delivery in a transfer program — a 76-point delivery gap inside a formally controlled scheme.

## Synthesis: the declared band, by parameter and category

| Parameter (E7) | Construction/infrastructure | Transfers/social programs |
|---|---|---|
| Execution deficiency / audited-irregularity share | Chile contracts-observed 34-50%; Brazil Fiscobras grave-irregularity 67% (risk-sampled); EU Cohesion error 9.3% | CGU misapplied transfers ~8.2% [S]; US improper 2.4% aggregate, 5.1% Medicaid, 33.5% EITC; UK 0.1-12.4% by program; EU direct payments 2.2% |
| Reported-vs-real completion (visibility-gap analogue) | Brazil ~41% of works paralyzed/unfinished; Peru ~48% of paralyzed-works cost sunk; Colombia 1,054 strict elefantes blancos; Argentina Sarmiento 30% vs 85-90% by control regime; UK HS2 write-offs; EU TEN-T non-delivery | Colombia VISR: ~76% of subsidies never materialized |
| Recovery (r) | — | Mexico ASF: 64.2% of determined-recoverable (historical) vs low single digits of observed (recent) — denominator-sensitive band |
| Detection→deterrence elasticity (mechanism) | — | Ferraz-Finan 2011 (-27% with reelection incentives); Avis-Ferraz-Finan 2018 (-8% after audit) |

Reading the band: leakage and non-delivery follow **verifiability and control design, not geography or political color** — the same gradient appears inside single administrations (UK 0.1%→12.4%; US 2.4%→33.5%) and across regions (EU works 9.3% → Brazil works 41% unfinished). The Argentine Sarmiento contrast (30% vs 85-90% on the same project under different control regimes) is the closest single-work empirical analogue of E5's arm contrast. Political diversity is satisfied: the items span administrations of every major orientation in nine jurisdictions plus the EU-27, over two decades; no single government or color drives any cell.

## Use rule

E7's S′ parameters are chosen *inside* these bands, category-matched, with the chosen operating point and its per-item justification stated in the E7 write-up and reviewed by the author before any run. This document is the evidence base; it does not itself select the point.
