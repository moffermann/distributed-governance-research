## Theoretical foundation (author-provided; belongs to Paper 1)
- **Value** = subjective degree of appreciation/importance a person attributes to something. Properties: gradual,
  subjective, relational, time-variable, context-dependent, **latent/hidden**, indirectly measurable,
  declared≠demonstrated, marginal, scarcity/alternatives-dependent, multidimensional, belief-dependent, uncertain.
- **F** = individual latent valuation → **latent random utility** (McFadden 1974), latent-variable-estimable
  (Bollen 2002), on a defined scale (Stevens 1946); a random variable (uncertainty about a unique latent value, or
  genuine stochasticity — a stated modeling choice).
- **G** = planner's belief/estimate of others' aggregate valuation → estimate with **measurement error** (Bollen)
  + **systematic bias** (Charité–Fisman–Kuziemko 2015 reference points; Ross–Greene–House 1977 false consensus;
  Broockman–Skovron 2018 elite–mass gap) under **info asymmetry** (Stiglitz 2017).
- Can't prove F≠G by definition (could coincide); can't guarantee F=G without strong assumptions (full info,
  perfect measurement, zero bias). ← the analytical + empirical territory of Paper 1.
- **References:** Stevens (1946); Bollen (2002); McFadden (1974, 2001); Item Response Theory; Braziunas & Boutilier
  (2006); Stiglitz (2017); Charité, Fisman & Kuziemko (2015); Ross, Greene & House (1977); Broockman & Skovron (2018).
