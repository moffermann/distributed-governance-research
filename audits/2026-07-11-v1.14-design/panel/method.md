## (B) POSITION — the restructure is honest IFF this checklist is met

I SUPPORT the restructure. It is honest emphasis, not spin, **only if every item holds.** Any single failure turns it
into selective reporting and I withdraw support.

**The single-eyeful rule (governing principle):** a reader who reads ONLY the body — and never opens the appendix —
must come away with a *correct* four-part summary. If any of the four is absent from the body, the restructure has
hidden information and fails.

Checklist (all mandatory in the BODY, not only the appendix):

- [ ] **B1. Epistemic label.** The headline scenario is named the *probable/expectable* scenario — explicitly one of
  four declared scenarios — never "the result," "the finding," or an unqualified number.
- [ ] **B2. Counter-scenario in the same eyeful.** The fact that the **central wins at its declared favourable
  endpoint (~98% vs ~68%)** appears in the *same paragraph* as the probable headline — not merely "see appendix."
  The losing case for the thesis is co-located with the winning case.
- [ ] **B3. Confirmatory-test anchor.** The headline carries a one-clause reminder that the paper's *one
  pre-registered confirmatory test* returned near-parity / NO-GO (Δ≈0.025), and that this equals the four-scenario
  map's no-myopia regime — so the +46.6% does not overturn the NO-GO; they are the same phenomenon under opposite
  harm-sight assumptions.
- [ ] **B4. Calibration hedge co-located.** "Declared reference/stress points, not calibrated impact" sits at the
  headline, not only in the appendix.
- [ ] **B5. Provenance of "probable" is honest.** The body (or a one-clause footnote) discloses that "probable" is a
  *source-motivated* declared case in which only some knobs are empirically anchored (per `e4-plausible-anchors.md`,
  `a_V,b_V` and `p` are confirmed; the harm-myopia strength — the load-bearing knob — is theory-anchored, least
  pinned). "Probable" must not silently imply a jointly-calibrated probability region.
- [ ] **B6. Explicit, complete pointer.** A one-clause "full four-scenario map, frontier, and theorem in Appendix E4"
  pointer, and the appendix reproduces the FULL table with no row dropped.
- [ ] **B7. Abstract parity.** The abstract's E4 sentence carries B1+B2+B4 (probable label, central-wins case,
  calibration hedge). The abstract is the most-read surface; it may not headline +46.6% without the caveats. (The
  current L13 already does this — the restructure must not weaken it.)
- [ ] **B8. EN/ES mirror parity.** Every one of B1–B7 lands identically in `drafts/paper.es.md`.

**The honest-emphasis vs spin test (falsifiable, two probes):**

1. **Swap test.** Would the *same presentation structure* be acceptable if the winner were reversed — i.e., if we
   headlined the PRO-CENTRAL endpoint and relegated the probable case to the appendix? Honest emphasis headlines the
   probable case *because it is the declared modal/source-motivated case* (B5), AND still gives the central-win case
   body-level visibility (B2). If the only reason "probable" is on top is that it is the thesis-favourable number,
   the swap test fails and it is spin.
2. **Single-eyeful test.** Hand the body-only text to a skeptical reader. If their one-sentence takeaway omits any of
   {distributed likely ahead in the source case; central ahead under its favourable conditions; magnitudes
   uncalibrated; confirmatory test was near-parity/NO-GO}, the restructure has hidden something → spin.

If both probes pass, the restructure is honest emphasis and I endorse it.

## (C) RED LINES — non-negotiable; any of these is a NO from me

- **R1.** Headlining the probable number (91%/45%, or "+46.6%", or any single figure) in body or abstract *without*
  the co-located central-wins case (B2). This is the primary spin failure mode.
- **R2.** Presenting +46.6% in a way a reader could read as *calibrated impact*, a *forecast*, or a *probability* —
  drop of B4, or "probable" used as if jointly calibrated (violates B5).
- **R3.** Any appendix row deleted, or the four-scenario table trimmed to fewer than four scenarios + the diagnostic
  contrast. "Move to appendix" must mean *relocate in full*, never *shrink*.
- **R4.** Severing the +46.6% from the NO-GO reconciliation (drop of B3) — this is the exact result-shopping defect
  the consolidated panel flagged as C5. The body must not let the reader think the four-scenario map overturned the
  pre-registered gate.
- **R5.** EN and ES diverging on any headline caveat (drop of B8). A caveat present in one language and absent in the
  other is selective reporting by locale.
- **R6.** Reintroducing a `×` multiplier, a `D/C` ratio, or "N-fold"/"N times" phrasing at the headline to make it
  punchier. The retired multiplier stays retired; punch comes from the D/O–C/O levels and the sign, not a ratio.
- **R7.** "Honest emphasis" used to *cut* the hedges rather than *relocate* them. Condensation removes repetition;
  it never removes the load-bearing caveat from the surface where the number appears.
