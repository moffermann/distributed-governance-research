# Panel review — Skeptic (pro-central, methodological hawk) — E4 form & headline restructure

**Scope:** FORM ONLY. No substance/finding changes. The E4 result is blessed and NOT
relitigated: PROBABLE distributed ~91% vs central ~45% (+46.6% gap); central WINS its
declared favourable endpoint (~98% vs ~68%); harm-myopia alone ≈40% of the gap;
symmetry-gate Δ≈0.025 = the no-myopia near-parity anchor. My job is to stop the
*probable* headline from being read as a measured effect once the full comparative map
moves to the appendix.

**One-line stance:** SUPPORT ONLY IF THE READER CANNOT MISTAKE THE HEADLINE FOR A
MEASURED EFFECT. A probable-scenario headline in the body is defensible as honest
emphasis — but only if four caveats travel *in the same breath* as the number, not in
the appendix. Without them attached, this is the single most quotable overreach in the
paper, and a hostile reader will lift "distributed beats central by 46.6%" verbatim.

## (A) Specific FORM edits — condensation + clarity

I concur with the lib.md relocation plan (A1–A5: collapse the earlier-ratios note,
keep the gate *verdict* in the body and move the mechanics to one merged **Appendix
E4**, headline the probable row + point to the table). No need to duplicate that here.
My form edits are the ones a skeptic adds *on top* — they exist to keep the caveats
load-bearing, not decorative.

### Edit S1 — the abstract's E4 sentence (L13) is one ~7-line run-on; split it, and put the epistemic status in the *same* clause as the number
The clause "a central realistically myopic to harm … loses to coverage" currently
sits far downstream of "with all magnitudes reported as source-motivated declared
reference points." Bind them:

> **After (two sentences):** "A four-scenario robustness extension then maps where
> each institution wins: a competent, harm-aware central ties the coverage arm (the
> pre-registered near-parity regime), while a central *assumed* myopic to harm on the
> low-visibility long tail loses to it. Every magnitude in that map — including the
> probable gap — is a source-motivated *declared reference point*, not a calibrated
> impact; the central figures in particular are proxy-informed and carry an
> unpropagated transport gap (§6)."

The word "realistically" in the abstract (L13) does quiet rhetorical work — it asserts
the myopic scenario is the true one. Change to "*assumed* myopic" or "*posited*
myopic." Direction is source-motivated; the *strength* is not measured, and the abstract
must not imply it is.

### Edit S2 — §6 L622–656: the caveat currently lands last; move it adjacent to the first magnitude
As written, the four scenarios (L634–639) precede the honest-status sentence
(L651–655) by ~15 lines. A reader who stops at "distributed arm delivers ≈ 91% and the
central ≈ 45%" (L636–637) has already taken the number without its status. In the
condensed body version, the status clause ("source-motivated declared reference/stress
points, intervals cover inner Monte-Carlo variability only, harm-myopia strength is the
load-bearing, least-empirically-pinned assumption") must be in the *same sentence* as
the 91/45 figures — see (D).

### Edit S3 — quarantine the word "probable"
"Probable" (L622, L636, abstract) reads to a policy audience as "most likely outcome" /
"our best estimate" — i.e. a posterior. It is not: it is one declared configuration
whose knobs are individually source-motivated, with no joint likelihood over
configurations and no propagated transport gap. On first use in the body, bind it once:
*"the source-motivated reference scenario (labelled 'probable' — a justified-per-knob
reference point, not a calibrated posterior over outcomes)."* Thereafter "probable" may
appear in quotes. This is a form fix (one added parenthetical), not a substance change.

### Edit S4 — keep the frontier crossing (t ≈ 0.57) in the body, not only the appendix
`e4-plausible-anchors.md` L81–87: interpolating probable → central-favourable, the
winner *flips to the central at t ≈ 0.57* — roughly 57% of the way along the declared
segment. This is the single most important honesty anchor for a skeptic: it tells the
reader a real target setting could land on *either* side of parity, and that the answer
is not robust to moving partway toward the central-favourable end. It must not be
buried. One clause in the body: *"a ceteris-paribus path from the probable to the
central-favourable endpoint crosses parity partway along (t ≈ 0.57), so which side a
real setting falls on is not decided by any single measured knob."*

### Edit S5 — EN/ES parity
Every edit lands identically in `drafts/paper.es.md` (13/13 mirror rule). The Spanish
"probable" carries the same posterior-implying overtone — apply S3 there too.

## (B) POSITION + the exact caveats that MUST accompany the body headline

**SUPPORT WITH HARD CONDITIONS** (nearer to a conditional block than lib.md's).

I accept the author's premise — honest emphasis, not spin — but only because the
probable case is *inconvenient-to-central in a way the sources genuinely motivate*, and
because the central's winning endpoint is shown. What makes me a reluctant, not
enthusiastic, supporter: the +46.6% gap is carried by the **fragile side of the
ledger**. The distributed 91% is comparatively robust across the map (68–96%); the
central's 45% is the swing term (45%→85%→98%, or −104%), and it swings on exactly the
two least-defensible inputs — the harm-myopia strength (anchor type **T**, the
least-empirically-pinned knob) and the projection/bias slope `w,a,b` (anchor type **P**,
proxy-informed with an **unpropagated transport gap** from political-opinion data to
project-value). So the headline gap is manufactured by two unmeasured central-side
assumptions. That is legitimate as a *declared robustness scenario*; it is
indefensible if it reads as a measured central deficiency.

**The four caveats that MUST be in the body, attached to the headline (not the appendix):**

1. **Declared, not calibrated.** "A source-motivated declared reference point, not a
   calibrated effect." In the same sentence as +46.6% / 91% / 45%.
2. **Transport gap on the central figures.** The central magnitudes (the 45%
   especially) are proxy-informed and carry an *unpropagated* political-opinion →
   project-value transport gap. The most fragile number must wear its fragility.
3. **Harm-myopia is the load-bearing, least-pinned assumption.** The gap exists because
   the central is *assumed* blind to diffuse harm on the low-visibility long tail; that
   strength is posited, not measured, and ~40% of the gap is that channel alone.
4. **Intervals are inner-MC only.** The [46.0, 47.4] / CI bands cover inner Monte-Carlo
   variability of the simulated DGP only — they are NOT confidence intervals over the
   real-world quantity and must never be read as calibrated uncertainty.

Plus the two reconciliation anchors lib.md already names (central wins its endpoint;
Δ≈0.025 gate near-parity is the same phenomenon under opposite harm-sight assumptions).
I treat those as necessary too, but 1–4 are the ones a *skeptic* insists on because they
are the ones an advocate is tempted to let slide to the appendix.

## (C) RED LINES — what must NOT be lost, hidden, or softened

1. **No magnitude appears in the body naked.** +46.6%, 91%, 45% — each carries
   "declared reference point, not calibrated impact" in its own sentence. A number in
   the body without its epistemic status is a red-line breach.
2. **The transport gap on the central figures stays in the body**, adjacent to the 45%.
   It cannot migrate to the appendix calibration table alone. If the number that makes
   the gap big is the least-defended one, that fact is body material.
3. **Harm-myopia flagged as the load-bearing, least-empirically-pinned assumption in
   the body.** Not appendix fine print. This is the crux the whole result pivots on.
4. **"Probable" is never unqualified on first body use** (Edit S3). No "the most likely
   outcome," "we expect," "in practice," or any wording implying a posterior over
   configurations. It is a justified-per-knob reference point.
5. **Inner-MC-only interval labelling.** No ± band in the body may read as calibrated
   real-world uncertainty. Either label it inner-MC in place or drop the band from the
   body and keep it in the appendix.
6. **The central's winning endpoint (~98% vs ~68%) stays first-class in the body.**
   Removing it would let the headline read as a uniform distributed win — the exact
   overreach I am here to prevent. (Shared with lib.md; I hold it just as hard.)
7. **The Δ≈0.025 gate + reconciliation travels with the headline.** No +46.6% on a page
   that omits the near-parity anchor and the "same phenomenon, opposite assumptions"
   framing.
8. **No resurrection of the retired ×-multiplier** through the probable number. +46.6%
   is a gap between two benchmark fractions; it must not read as a comeback of the 2×.
9. **The frontier crossing (t ≈ 0.57) stays in the body** (Edit S4). The result's
   sensitivity to moving partway toward central-favourable is not optional context.

## (D) Proposed BODY headline sentence with its minimal honest caveat attached

Primary (completeness — every mandatory caveat bound in):

> **"Under the source-motivated reference scenario labelled *probable* — a central that
> reads visible, credit-bearing value but is *assumed* myopic to concentrated harm on
> the low-visibility long tail — coverage-routed distributed selection delivers about
> 91% of the full-information benchmark against the central's ~45% (a +46.6% gap). This
> is a declared reference point, not a calibrated effect: the central figures are
> proxy-informed and carry an unpropagated transport gap, the harm-myopia strength that
> drives most of the gap is the load-bearing, least-empirically-pinned assumption
> (≈40% of the gap is that channel alone), and the interval reflects inner Monte-Carlo
> variability only. It is one row of a four-scenario map (Appendix E4): the central
> *wins* its own favourable endpoint (~98% vs ~68%), a competent harm-aware central
> ties the coverage arm (the pre-registered Δ≈0.025 near-parity regime), and a
> ceteris-paribus path between the two crosses parity partway along (t ≈ 0.57) — the
> same mechanism read under opposite assumptions about what the planner can see."**

Minimal (if the editor wants punch — but caveats 1, 2, and the endpoint pointer are the
irreducible floor; do not cut below this):

> **"Where the planner is *assumed* blind to diffuse harm on the low-visibility long
> tail — the source-motivated 'probable' reference, not a calibrated case — coverage
> delivers ~91% of the full-information benchmark to the central's ~45% (+46.6% gap; a
> declared reference point on proxy-informed central figures with an unpropagated
> transport gap). The central wins its own favourable endpoint (~98% vs ~68%); full map
> in Appendix E4."**

The difference between my minimal variant and lib.md's is deliberate: mine keeps
"proxy-informed / transport gap" in the *minimal* form, because that is the caveat a
pro-Core-v0 advocate is most tempted to shed and the one that most protects the paper
from a hostile "you manufactured the central's 45%" reading.
