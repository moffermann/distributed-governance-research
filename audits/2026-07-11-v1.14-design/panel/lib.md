## (B) POSITION on the probable-headline / full-appendix restructure

**SUPPORT WITH CONDITIONS.**

Why a classical liberal supports headlining the probable case: the probable scenario
is not the analyst's thumb on the scale — each of its knobs is source-motivated
(heavy-tailed procurement for the visibility tail; PB turnout for participation;
agenda-setting for harm-myopia). A planner who reads visible, credit-bearing value
and is myopic to diffuse harm on the low-visibility long tail is precisely Hayek's
knowledge problem and Bastiat's unseen made operational. Headlining the *most
probable* configuration is what any honest empiricist does; the author's premise
holds — the same emphasis rule would publish a central-favourable probable case if
that were where the sources pointed. Leading with a four-way "on the one hand / on
the other" table when one row is the evidence-anchored reference would be false
even-handedness, not neutrality.

Why the conditions are non-negotiable: emphasis becomes spin the moment the
inconvenient rows get harder to reach than the convenient one. The central's winning
endpoint is the strongest single piece of evidence that this paper is not special
pleading — it must be one link away, honestly labelled, never softened. The
reconciliation with the symmetry-gate NO-GO must travel *with* the headline, because
the probable +46.6% and the gate's Δ≈0.025 are the same phenomenon under opposite
harm-sight assumptions; presenting either without the other misleads.

## (C) RED LINES — what must NOT be lost or hidden

1. **The central's winning endpoint stays first-class.** ~98% vs ~68% at the
   declared central-favourable endpoint must appear in the body headline paragraph's
   pointer sentence (not only deep in the appendix), stated as a real win, not a
   footnote. If the central wins where its assumptions hold, we say so plainly.
2. **The symmetry-gate NO-GO and its reconciliation stay attached to the headline.**
   Δ=0.025 < 0.05, multiplier retired, "same phenomenon under opposite assumptions."
   No headline of +46.6% may stand on a page that doesn't also carry the near-parity
   anchor.
3. **"Declared reference/stress points, not calibrated impact"** must sit in the same
   sentence as any magnitude in the body — including +46.6%, 91%, 45%. The number and
   its epistemic status are inseparable.
4. **Harm-myopia flagged as the load-bearing, least-pinned assumption.** The crux
   knob's fragility is the honest core of the result; it cannot migrate to appendix
   fine print alone.
5. **The full four-scenario table + diagnostic contrast + frontier + theorem +
   honest limits survive complete in Appendix E4.** Relocation, not attrition. A
   reader can reconstruct every scenario and its assumptions.
6. **No return of the retired ×-multiplier framing** as the de-facto headline via the
   probable number. +46.6% is a gap between two benchmark fractions, not a resurrected
   2×. The body must not let it read as one.
7. **EN/ES parity.** Every edit lands identically in `drafts/paper.es.md` (13/13
   mirror rule).
