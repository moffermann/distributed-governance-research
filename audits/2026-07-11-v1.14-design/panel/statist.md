## (B) POSITION on the probable-headline / full-appendix restructure

**SUPPORT WITH CONDITIONS.**

Where I agree with the author's premise: the probable case is source-anchored, not
analyst-chosen, and honest empiricism headlines the most probable configuration. I do
not dispute that.

Where I push back, and why it is a FORM (not substance) matter: the paper's own
evidence ledger records harm-myopia (`s_exp`, `b_H_C`) as a **T (theory) anchor** and
names it the "load-bearing, least-empirically-pinned assumption," while the central's
*tie* under harm-awareness (NO-MYOPIA) is the continuity anchor to the pre-registered
gate that actually cleared adversarial design. In plain terms: the single most
audited, most defensible number in the whole E4 program is the **near-parity** result,
and the +46.6% headline exists only because one weakly-pinned knob is set to
"myopic." A restructure that headlines +46.6% in the body while routing the tie and
the central win to an appendix would make the *least*-anchored result the most
prominent and the *most*-anchored result the least prominent. That is an inversion of
evidential weight — spin by layout, even if every individual number is true.

The fix is not to suppress the probable headline. It is to require the body headline
to carry, in the same breath, the tie, the central win, and the harm-myopia
dependency — so a scanning reader (table → headline → appendix) cannot come away
thinking "distributed beats central ~2×" when the honest reading is "distributed wins
*if* the state is blind to diffuse harm; a competent state ties or wins." With that
condition met, I support the restructure.

## (C) RED LINES — what the BODY must retain for this to be honest

1. **The central's TIE is a body-level result, stated before or beside the probable
   gap.** The competent, harm-aware central at near-parity (NO-MYOPIA ~91% vs ~85%;
   symmetry-gate Δ≈0.025) is the honest reference for a capable modern state. It must
   not be reachable only via an appendix link. This is my hardest red line and the one
   the liberal panelist does not fully carry.
2. **The central's WIN appears in the body headline paragraph, as a real win.**
   ~98% vs ~68% at the declared favourable endpoint, stated plainly — not softened,
   not buried, not called merely "a favourable corner."
3. **The harm-myopia dependency rides in the same breath as the +46.6% headline.**
   The body must state that the ordering hinges on the assumed myopia, and that this
   knob is the load-bearing, least-empirically-pinned one. A magnitude without its
   crux caveat in the adjacent clause is not permitted.
4. **"Declared reference/stress points, not calibrated impact"** sits in the same
   sentence as every body magnitude (+46.6%, 91%, 45%, 98%, 68%).
5. **~40% of the gap is harm-myopia alone** stays in the body, not appendix-only — it
   is the quantitative statement of red line 3.
6. **No resurrection of the retired ×-multiplier via the probable number.** +46.6% is
   a gap between two benchmark fractions under one adverse assumption; the body must
   not let it read as a restored 2×/2.1× effect. The near-parity and central-win rows
   are the antidote and must be visible for it to work.
7. **Full four-scenario map (all rows, CIs, diagnostic contrast, frontier, theorem,
   honest limits) survives complete in Appendix E4.** Relocation, not attrition.
8. **EN/ES parity** — every edit lands identically in `drafts/paper.es.md`.
