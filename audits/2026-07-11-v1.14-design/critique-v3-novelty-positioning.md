# Dimension 7 — novelty and positioning

## Verdict

**PARTIAL / NEEDS CHANGES. Blocker 7 is not fully cleared.** V3's positioning is much more honest than v2: it no longer calls transported political-opinion coefficients project-value anchors, it makes an unidentified sign a legitimate result, and it expressly conditions standalone novelty on a theorem and/or target-domain bridge data (`audits/2026-07-11-v1.14-design/DESIGN_SKETCH_v3.md:66-81,121-125`). That is the right direction.

The sharper search, however, finds substantial published prior art on the object v2 had not searched. In particular, Böttcher and Klingebiel already compare expert delegation with aggregation of noisy evaluations in budget-constrained multi-project selection and derive conditions under which either can perform better. Rey and Endriss already formulate costly participatory selection as truth-tracking from noisy citizen ballots. Boehmer et al. already produce a simulation-based robustness analysis of participatory-budgeting outcomes under noisy votes. Robust Portfolio Modeling has optimized project portfolios over uncertainty sets in scores, preferences, costs, and constraints since at least 2007. Mollick and Nanda empirically compare crowd and expert funding decisions and downstream project outcomes. Thus **“noisy/biased expert versus crowd budgeted selection,” “a robustness atlas,” “sometimes the central wins,” and “truth-tracking costly citizen selection” are not new categories of result.**

I did not find an exact prior paper that combines all of E4's remaining particulars: a source-to-target transport-discrepancy set that includes null transport and sign reversal; a sign-dependent MNAR coverage channel; a central proxy/credit channel; and sign classification of the same oracle-normalized parity estimand over heterogeneous-cost portfolios. That combination is a plausible narrow synthesis or application contribution. It is not yet a delivered theorem, identified empirical result, or demonstrated methodological innovation. V3 itself says the analytic derivation remains “to be written and checked” (`DESIGN_SKETCH_v3.md:83-90`), so calling the package “theorem-backed” in the present tense four sections later is premature (`DESIGN_SKETCH_v3.md:121-125`).

The defensible present claim is therefore:

> E4 proposes a preregistered, transport-sensitivity comparison of two specific budgeted project-selection channels. Its potential novelty is the transport-robust **institutional contrast and bridge-study design**, not noisy portfolio selection, expert-versus-crowd comparison, participatory-budgeting robustness, or uncertainty-set portfolio analysis as such. A theory claim requires a proved result beyond the standard signal-ranking benchmark; an empirical claim requires same-project target-domain data.

## Specific issues and required fixes

### 1. “Theorem-backed” is not true yet, and the proposed benchmark is unlikely to be the novel theorem

Section 5 calls `Cov(S,X_k)/sd(X_k)` an analytic benchmark but explicitly says its derivation remains to be written and checked (`DESIGN_SKETCH_v3.md:83-90`). Section 10 nevertheless says “This is a theorem-backed benchmark” (`DESIGN_SKETCH_v3.md:121-125`). Moreover, ranking the top fixed share under elliptical/Gaussian signals by their covariance with truth is a standard truncated-moment/signal-ranking result; Böttcher and Klingebiel already use order statistics and expected true portfolio quality to benchmark noisy selection. A restatement for two E4 signals would be valuable validation, but not obviously a publishable theorem.

**Fix:** until the proof exists, write “a proposed analytic benchmark.” For publication novelty, state and prove a result that is specific to E4—for example, sharp sufficient/necessary conditions for sign robustness over a declared transport set, or a theorem showing how sign-dependent missingness and transported directional bias move the parity boundary. Prove sharpness or explicitly call the result an outer-bound/sensitivity theorem. Cite the standard selection and order-statistic lineage rather than marketing the covariance ratio itself as new.

### 2. The closest paper already owns much of the claimed institutional-comparison space

Böttcher and Klingebiel is not a loose wisdom-of-crowds citation. Its outcome is the true quality of the **selected portfolio**, its constraint is a tight project-selection budget, its inputs are noisy project evaluations, and its alternatives include expert delegation and several collective aggregation rules. Its central substantive result is already conditional: known, well-matched expertise can make delegation best; aggregation is often better elsewhere. That is strikingly close to v3's stated object—conditions where one institution delivers more true value, less, or cannot be signed (`DESIGN_SKETCH_v3.md:8-14`).

**Fix:** make this the first theoretical comparator, reproduce its equal-cost/unbiased-noise special case as an external regression benchmark, and state E4's delta in one sentence: “We add signed MNAR citizen coverage and source-to-target biased central estimation, then characterize which expert/delegation-versus-aggregation conclusions survive transport uncertainty.” Compare E4's two rules to at least Böttcher–Klingebiel-style rank aggregation, score averaging, voting, and correctly assigned expert delegation. Otherwise a reviewer can reasonably say E4 compares two dominated or selectively parameterized rules.

### 3. Noisy participatory selection and its robustness are established, and rule choice is load-bearing

The distributed channel is not being introduced into an empty literature. Rey and Endriss treat costly citizen ballots as noisy signals of ground truth and show that epistemic justification depends sharply on the rule and noise model. Boehmer et al. perturb real PB votes and show that greedy/proportional rules and completion methods differ materially in robustness. V3 currently fixes one HT estimate and a greedy selection rule (`DESIGN_SKETCH_v3.md:44-64`) and treats the result as a comparison of institutions, but does not position that choice against these established alternatives.

**Fix:** add a prior-art subsection called “Epistemic and noise-robust participatory budgeting.” Explain why signed cardinal reports and social-value delivery require departing from approval-ballot MLE results. Pre-register rule variants as named model-form robustness checks, not additional headline estimands: greedy approval/cost, an MES/Phragmén-style proportional rule where meaningful, rank aggregation, and optimal knapsack versus greedy fill. If conclusions depend on the completion rule, say the institutional sign is rule-indeterminate rather than attributing the change to central-versus-coverage governance.

### 4. The uncertainty-set operation is established; the potentially new object is transport-specific

V3 computes `m_lo=min_T m` and `m_hi=max_T m` and labels a sign robust only when the entire interval clears the materiality band (`DESIGN_SKETCH_v3.md:66-81`). This is a sensible honesty device. But RPM already evaluates budget-feasible project portfolios across feasible sets of scores, preferences, costs, and constraints and identifies conclusions robust to all realizations. V3 should not imply novelty from using a set rather than a probability law (`DESIGN_SKETCH_v3.md:100-106`).

**Fix:** position the method as an application of robust/partial-set reasoning to **external-validity uncertainty in an institutional performance contrast**. Cite RPM and the transportability/sensitivity literature. State whether `T` is an identified set from observables or a preregistered sensitivity set supplied by the analyst; if it is the latter, call `[m_lo,m_hi]` “transport-sensitivity bounds under `T`,” not an empirically identified region without qualification. The novel candidate is the same-project bridge design that can shrink `T`, not the min/max operation.

### 5. The companion documents still contradict v3's honest positioning

V3 says source coefficients are not target structural coefficients and target `a,w,b` are “PROXY-INFORMED via T,” not anchored (`DESIGN_SKETCH_v3.md:66-81,92-98`). But the still-live foundation calls systematic intercept bias “the novel force,” labels target ranges “PROXY-ANCHORED,” claims `w≈21–50%`, and says the new boundary extends the retired `beta=1-eta` law (`research/e4-value-estimation-foundation.md:18-35,48-63`). The empirical-anchor document likewise upgrades target `a` and `w` to “PROXY-ANCHORED (range)” even while admitting transport is unverified (`audits/2026-07-11-v1.14-design/E4-empirical-anchors.md:57-71`). These are not harmless historical notes: they are the files v3 cites as its empirical foundation, and they directly recreate the novelty and anchoring overclaims that v3 says it retired.

**Fix:** before preregistration, either rewrite both documents to the v3 vocabulary or add an unmistakable superseded banner and a normative pointer to v3. Delete “novel force,” “proxy-anchored range,” “plausible target range,” “irreducible,” and “extends `beta=1-eta`.” Preserve the source-domain findings, exact outcome/scale, and their role only in motivating the form and constructing explicitly assumed transport scenarios.

### 6. A measurement *plan* is not yet a scientific contribution

V3 names a same-project bridge study as the only way to shrink `T` (`DESIGN_SKETCH_v3.md:74-81`) and calls the package a “measurement plan” (`DESIGN_SKETCH_v3.md:121-125`). Mollick and Nanda already compare crowd and expert judgments on the same project proposals and examine realized outcomes, although their design cannot identify a pure crowd-versus-expert policy contrast because budgets and proposal universes differ. E4's proposed bridge can improve on that, but only if it concretely measures the objects its transport map needs.

**Fix:** attach a minimum bridge protocol: same project set and response scale; citizen value distribution; nonresponse/negative-voice validation; planner own value, point forecast, and distribution forecast; central team aggregation; costs and budget; held-out projects/jurisdictions; and a preregistered mapping from bridge moments to `T`. Until data are collected, call it a future empirical contribution. When collected, compare predictive rank loss and selected-portfolio value against Mollick–Nanda-style expert/crowd agreement and against competent central/team and hybrid rules.

### 7. Combination novelty must be claimed narrowly and tested against stronger baselines

No exact match located in this targeted search jointly has E4's transport set, signed MNAR reports, proxy/credit split, and oracle-normalized institution contrast. But combining established modules is not automatically a sufficient standalone contribution. The package becomes useful if it changes a decision: it identifies regions robust to extremely permissive transport, shows that commonly proposed rankings fail robustly, or demonstrates which bridge measurement most contracts institutional ambiguity.

**Fix:** make the contribution hierarchy explicit before preregistration:

1. **Not claimed:** expert/crowd divergence, noisy budgeted selection, PB robustness, robust portfolio uncertainty sets, or the existence of central-win regions.
2. **Delivered only after proof/code:** a formally correct transport-sensitivity map for two precisely defined institutions, with sharp or explicitly conservative bounds and external regression benchmarks.
3. **Potential empirical contribution:** same-project bridge data that bound the central projection and citizen coverage mechanisms on the target scale.
4. **Potential theory contribution:** a nonstandard theorem specific to signed MNAR coverage plus transported central bias—not merely the covariance-to-noise ranking formula.

## Is the proposed contribution honest and sufficient?

- **Honest as a preregistration description after wording fixes:** yes. “Proposed analytic benchmark + preregistered transport-sensitivity atlas + bridge measurement protocol” accurately describes a worthwhile model-criticism exercise even if most cells are transport-indeterminate or central-winning.
- **Sufficient as a present standalone novelty claim:** no. The benchmark is not yet proved, the engine and bounds are not delivered in v3, the uncertainty-set technique is established, and the measurement component is only a plan.
- **Plausibly sufficient after execution:** yes, but narrowly—if the paper demonstrates a transport-specific result not implied by Böttcher–Klingebiel/Rey–Endriss/RPM, validates the distributed rule against PB alternatives, and either proves sharp robust-sign conditions or supplies target-domain bridge data.

## Bottom line

V3 closes the worst rhetorical defect of v2: it no longer needs planner ugliness or a distributed win to tell a story. It does **not** yet close the novelty blocker. The sharper literature shows that nearly every broad ingredient is published already. The surviving contribution is the **transport-robust synthesis for one precisely defined central-proxy-versus-coverage institutional contrast, plus a bridge design capable of turning sensitivity bounds into target evidence**. State that narrow claim, prove or implement it, compare it against the nearest rules, and remove the contradictory “anchored/new force/extends beta” language from the companion files.
