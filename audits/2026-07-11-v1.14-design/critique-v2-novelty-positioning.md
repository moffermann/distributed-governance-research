# Dimension 7 — Novelty and positioning

## Verdict: partial fix; the positioning is more honest, but the claimed contribution is not yet earned

v2 correctly abandons the untenable claim that planner–citizen value divergence, projection, or the “rate it, then guess others” instrument is new. Its own prior-art review says every component of that story is established and calls Dias–Lucas–Sheffer “nearly our exact thesis” (`audits/2026-07-11-v1.14-design/E4-prior-art-review.md:8-23,25-31`). That clears the most important honesty test.

But §7 turns three proposed work products into present-tense contributions too early (`audits/2026-07-11-v1.14-design/DESIGN_SKETCH_v2.md:74-79`). No shipped engine implements the stated central model; no new boundary theorem has been derived for it; and no identified set has been constructed. At present the defensible output is a **proposed preregistered robustness atlas plus a project-value measurement plan**, not yet a standalone novel comparative-institutions result. The stronger positioning becomes earned only with a new theorem and/or genuinely new project-level planner/citizen data.

## Specific issues

### 1. Contribution (a) is phrased as novelty when it is currently only imported structure

The design calls the literature-anchored equation “new inside” a mechanism-design/institutional-comparison simulation (`audits/2026-07-11-v1.14-design/DESIGN_SKETCH_v2.md:74-77`). “New inside our simulator” is implementation novelty, not a scientific contribution by itself. The prior-art review is unusually clear that E4 should **cite rather than re-prove** the F≠G/projection premise (`audits/2026-07-11-v1.14-design/E4-prior-art-review.md:33-45`). The foundation nevertheless calls systematic intercept bias “the novel force” (`research/e4-value-estimation-foundation.md:26-35`), which is directly at odds with that review.

There is also not yet an implementation to support even the narrower application claim. The v4 engine's central score is cell-level noisy valuation with attenuated harm (`scripts/simulation/e4-v4-symmetric-frontier.mjs:36-50`); the E5 gate's central score is sampled mean times reach, combined with political credit (`scripts/simulation/e5-sp-symmetry-gate.mjs:77-90`). Neither contains `a`, `b`, `w`, planner position, or the reducible/irreducible format decomposition.

**Fix.** Replace “contribution is a literature-anchored central-arm estimation model” with: “We import established elite-misperception and projection findings as a structured, explicitly transported central-arm specification.” Claim a substantive contribution only after the equation is implemented, its targets are extracted from exact source estimates, and its out-of-domain predictions are tested on project-value data.

### 2. Contribution (b) conflates an old frontier with an unproved new theorem

A parity frontier and central-win region already exist in this project. The old engine reports `<1 = central wins` and states the closed form `beta* = 1-gamma` (`scripts/simulation/e4-v4-symmetric-frontier.mjs:73-85`); the prior v1 critique counted 11 of 25 displayed central-win cells (`audits/2026-07-11-v1.14-design/CRITIQUE_SUMMARY.md:9-14`). Therefore “we map where each institution wins” is not new, and a denser surface adds resolution rather than a mechanism.

The possible new object is much narrower: a theorem for the **joint selection problem** induced by biased/projected central estimates, adverse distributed participation, costs, eligibility, budget-constrained greedy choice, and credit pressure. That result does not follow by relabeling `gamma` as `(a,b,w)`. The old proof compares two scalar harm weights (`research/e4-analytical-backbone.md:43-60`); the new model changes scores, ranks, and eligibility. v2 simultaneously asserts analytic characterization and admits that derivability is unresolved (`audits/2026-07-11-v1.14-design/DESIGN_SKETCH_v2.md:74-78,86-90`).

The equation is not yet sufficiently indexed for the claimed boundary either. As written, `a` and `w(v_p-S_bar)` are common shifts across projects (`research/e4-value-estimation-foundation.md:23-36`). Under `z(M_C/cost)` and a positive-eligibility gate, such a common shift primarily changes cost tilt and threshold crossings, not project-specific projection toward planner preferences. Until the model defines project/issue-specific `a_j`, `v_pj`, and population targets, a purported projection frontier can be an artefact of cost and gating conventions.

**Fix.** State contribution (b) conditionally: “We seek sharp or sufficient dominance conditions; if no tractable closed form exists, we will report a preregistered numerical frontier and make no theorem claim.” Before computation, define project-indexed signals and prove which terms affect ordering versus only eligibility. Derive the cleanest special case, state every restriction, then show by simulation exactly which departures move the boundary. Do not describe `beta=1-gamma` as evidence for the new boundary; the analytical backbone itself labels it a superseded special case (`research/e4-analytical-backbone.md:12-24`).

### 3. “Anchored simulation” is neither a novelty nor currently anchored in code

The empirical documents are candid that political-opinion magnitudes do not identify public-project-value parameters (`audits/2026-07-11-v1.14-design/E4-empirical-anchors.md:66-71`; `research/e4-value-estimation-foundation.md:71-76`). Consequently, a simulator that samples those ranges would be a transparent transport sensitivity exercise, not an empirically anchored estimate of institutional performance. The current gate cannot validate the proposed model because it uses a different central estimator and a frozen world (`scripts/simulation/e5-sp-symmetry-gate.mjs:29-49,60-99`). Its NO-GO output is relevant history, not validation of the new a/b/w mechanism.

**Fix.** Position simulation as verification and robustness analysis. For a publishable substantive claim, add a held-out project-value exercise: estimate/elicitate planner and citizen project valuations, fit the central and distributed observation equations without looking at parity outcomes, and test predicted rank errors and boundary crossings on held-out projects or jurisdictions. Compare against competent adaptive central appraisal and hybrid/stratified designs, not only the two stylized arms—the prior critique already identified these comparisons as necessary (`audits/2026-07-11-v1.14-design/CRITIQUE_SUMMARY.md:93-97`).

### 4. “Partial identification” is currently a category error

Partial identification is not the fact that some parameters have wide ranges or different provenance labels. It requires a stated observable distribution/moment set, assumptions that map observables to a parameter set, and a reported identified set for the target. v2 has a status table but no observation equations, moment inequalities, transport set, or identified set (`audits/2026-07-11-v1.14-design/DESIGN_SKETCH_v2.md:48-61`). Calling the unfinished registry “partial identification” overstates the result.

**Fix.** Define an admissible set `Theta_I(P)` from exact observed moments plus explicit transport bounds. For `g(theta)=E_W[Delta_O|theta]`, report the set `G_I={g(theta): theta in Theta_I(P)}` and, where possible, the projection of `{theta in Theta_I(P): g(theta)=0}` onto policy-relevant axes. A win is sign-identified only if the entire set lies above `+delta` or below `-delta`; otherwise the honest result is “institutional ranking not identified.” Derive sharpness or label bounds outer/conservative. Until this exists, say **uncertainty classification and measurement map**, not partial identification.

### 5. The novelty search does not cover the object now being claimed

The supplied prior-art review is expressly a first-pass web search whose closest papers were not read in full (`audits/2026-07-11-v1.14-design/E4-prior-art-review.md:1-6,50-53`). More importantly, it searches and rejects novelty for F↔G, projection, meta-prediction, and valuation elicitation. It does not establish novelty of biased-expert-versus-crowd project selection, budgeted ranking under noisy signals, comparative institutional information acquisition, robust decision boundaries, or partial identification of policy choice. Thus it can retire the old foundational claim, but it cannot validate the new parity-theorem claim.

**Fix.** Before preregistration or publication positioning, run a documented search centered on the actual proposed theorem and estimand across mechanism design, social choice/information aggregation, operations research/knapsack selection under noisy or biased scores, expert-versus-crowd forecasting, participatory budgeting, robust decision-making, and partial identification. Record inclusion criteria and a claim-by-claim nearest-neighbor table. If the closest result already gives the boundary, E4's contribution must be empirical transport/measurement rather than theory.

## Positioning that is earned now

The honest present-tense claim is:

> Established research shows that planners can track public opinion while exhibiting systematic intercept shifts and directional projection. E4 proposes to import that structure—without claiming its political-opinion magnitudes transport—into a preregistered comparison of central and coverage-routed project appraisal. Its immediate product is a robustness atlas and measurement map. A new theoretical contribution would require a sharp or sufficient parity result for the fully specified selection problem; a new empirical contribution would require project-value data that identify or bound where the parity boundary lies.

This is consistent with the v1 critique's publication standard—“theorem plus data”—and its fallback of a preregistered robustness atlas/pilot-measurement appendix (`audits/2026-07-11-v1.14-design/CRITIQUE_SUMMARY.md:93-97`). It also remains valuable if the central arm wins broadly or the sign remains unidentified. What v2 may not yet claim is that the equation, boundary, simulation, or partial identification is already a novel delivered result.
