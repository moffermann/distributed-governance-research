# Dimension 7 — novelty, positioning, and companion-document alignment

Its summaries of Böttcher–Klingebiel, Rey–Endriss, Boehmer et al., Liesiö–Mild–Salo, and Mollick–Nanda are consistent with the prior primary-paper review (`audits/2026-07-11-v1.14-design/critique-v3-novelty-positioning.md:19-29`).

But the live companion documents still make stronger transport and measurement claims than v4 permits, and v4 still presents an uncomputed atlas and an ordinary Gaussian selection identity too close to present-tense contribution language. The defensible contribution remains a **proposed transport-sensitivity synthesis and bridge-study design**, not a delivered atlas, new generic theorem, or source-anchored target model.

## Remaining issues

### 1. The companions contradict their own corrections and v4's model

The foundation still opens by saying both model form **and parameter ranges are anchored** to published evidence (`research/e4-value-estimation-foundation.md:3-7`), then later says target coefficients are not anchored (`research/e4-value-estimation-foundation.md:50-54`). It also says the sign and “plausible ranges” transfer “with confidence” (`research/e4-value-estimation-foundation.md:80-83`). The empirical document similarly promises a “measured, citable anchor instead of assumed parameters,” retains an “anchored ranges” heading, calls target bias materially non-zero and large, and again says plausible ranges are anchored (`E4-empirical-anchors.md:3-8,18-26,71-75`). Those claims are not rescued by the correction at lines 57-68: source political-opinion effects do not establish the sign, nonzero magnitude, or plausible range of project-value coefficients in the target domain.

More seriously, the foundation still specifies a project-invariant projection term `w(v_p−S̄)` (`research/e4-value-estimation-foundation.md:18-37`), while v4's claimed identification depends on project-varying `v_{p,j}` and `w(v_{p,j}−S_j)` (`DESIGN_SKETCH_v4.md:61-74`). This is not merely notation: it is the difference between the v3 collapse and v4's proposed rank condition.

**Fix:** rewrite the opening/section headings and lines 80-83 to say “source evidence motivates candidate mechanisms, forms, and sensitivity scenarios; no target sign or range is identified.” Replace the foundation equation and parameter descriptions with v4's project-varying contract, or mark the file superseded. Correct the broken prior-art pointers: the cited paths do not exist; the live file is `audits/2026-07-11-v1.14-design/E4-prior-art-review.md` (`research/e4-value-estimation-foundation.md:6-7`; `E4-empirical-anchors.md:3-4`).

### 2. The present contribution is still one tense too strong

V4 calls the atlas a present contribution even though the sketch specifies future engine/schema work and contains no atlas artifact (`DESIGN_SKETCH_v4.md:154-170,184-187`). The fixed-threshold covariance formula is a useful E4 regression benchmark, but the prior review already identified truncated-normal signal ranking as standard and Böttcher–Klingebiel as the closest budgeted noisy-selection neighbor (`critique-v3-novelty-positioning.md:33-43`). “Our narrow, not-yet-matched synthesis” also sounds like an exhaustive novelty finding although the search was expressly targeted, not systematic (`critique-v3-novelty-positioning.md:15-17`; `DESIGN_SKETCH_v4.md:188-194`).

**Fix:** say “a proposed theorem/benchmark, an atlas specification, and a proposed bridge protocol.” State that the Gaussian identity is validation, not claimed novelty. Replace “not-yet-matched” with “no exact match was located in the targeted review.”

### 3. Prior-art differentiation is accurate but incomplete

The five one-phrase parentheticals name the right literatures but do not state the operative deltas. V4 omits the close democratic-versus-elite theorem of Manouchehrabadi–Letizia–Hendrikse and the knapsack decision-bias comparator, both already identified in the prior review (`critique-v3-novelty-positioning.md:28-29`). It also does not acknowledge that Böttcher–Klingebiel compare several collective rules, or that Boehmer et al. make rule/completion choice load-bearing (`critique-v3-novelty-positioning.md:23-25`). Without those distinctions, a result from one greedy rule can be marketed too easily as an institutional effect.

**Fix:** add a compact nearest-neighbor table: prior object, E4 delta, and required external benchmark. Name the surviving delta as signed-MNAR coverage plus transported central projection/credit on the same oracle-normalized institutional contrast—not generic robust min/max, crowd/expert reversal, or Gaussian ranking.

### 4. The Prelec claim still exceeds the bridge protocol

V4 correctly limits the convenience app to existence/direction, not population magnitudes (`DESIGN_SKETCH_v4.md:175-181`). The foundation instead calls Surprisingly Popular an established instrument for **eliciting `w`** and the F↔G gap (`research/e4-value-estimation-foundation.md:74-78`). Recovering a categorical truth under that method does not by itself identify v4's planner-specific project projection coefficient or its target-population transport map.

**Fix:** call it a candidate elicitation component. Require jointly observed planner forecasts, citizen values, category signals, response/nonresponse information, and an explicit mapping from bridge moments into `R_α`; do not say it elicits `w` absent that identification argument.

## Bottom line

The broad positioning is now candid, and the retired β law is no longer a live overclaim. Blocker 7 remains **PARTIAL** because the companions still imply target anchoring, encode the superseded projection model, and overstate what the app identifies. Align those files and narrow “present contribution” to artifacts that actually exist before preregistration.
