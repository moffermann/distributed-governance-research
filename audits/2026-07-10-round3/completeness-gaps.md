# Completeness & gaps

**Verdict:** **No** — not publication-ready on this dimension. The architecture is unusually well specified, but the manuscript does not yet supply a self-contained account or literature basis for its new load-bearing mechanism; it openly leaves the novelty review unfinished; and it continues to rely on an audit evidence base whose own mandatory verification step is incomplete. The controlling welfare boundary is also missing from the paper where it matters most.

## Findings

### 1. Major — The one confirmatory result has no self-contained methods subsection

- **Exact locator:** `drafts/paper.md:537`
- **What is wrong:** The governing paragraph gives symmetry conditions and headline results, then sends the reader to the contract, code, and audit files. It never defines the estimand in the paper, explains the coverage estimator, defines the central credit-weighted score, lists the primary factor grid, states the 100-world sampling plan, or reports the negative control and matched-budget low-information stress result. Section 5 instead announces three other models (`drafts/paper.md:391`), none of which is the credit-versus-coverage gate. The missing design is material: the actual preregistration defines different reporting probabilities for positive and negative stakeholders, an even central appraisal allocation, `Delta=(D-C)/O`, and a frozen `lambda × rho × h` grid (`audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-preregistration.md:26`, `audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-preregistration.md:29`, `audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-preregistration.md:41`, `audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-preregistration.md:45`).
- **Why it matters:** The title, abstract, and conclusion make this the paper's only confirmatory computational evidence, but a reviewer cannot understand, evaluate, or reproduce the test from the article itself. A repository link is supplementary material, not a substitute for the methods required to interpret the estimand and the 18-cell claim.
- **Concrete fix:** Add a dedicated “Symmetric credit-versus-coverage gate” methods-and-results subsection. Include the estimand equation and definitions of `D`, `C`, and `O`; the two estimators and eligibility/ranking rules; all frozen parameters and primary cells; the negative control; the matched-budget low-information stress regime and its non-resilience interpretation; the preregistered decision statistic/rule; and a compact table separating the preregistered median from the post-hoc ratio-of-sums and clustered interval.

### 2. Major — The load-bearing credit-versus-coverage mechanism is asserted without a literature bridge

- **Exact locator:** `drafts/paper.md:13`
- **What is wrong:** The abstract says credit-seeking central ranking “can systematically underweight” diffuse, low-visibility benefits, but supplies no citation. The paper contains no focused treatment of political credit claiming and no Mayhew or Arnold entry in the reference list, even though the repository's mechanism note identifies those as the intended theoretical anchors (`research/e4e5-value-model-v2.md:73`). Nor does the paper explain what evidence, if any, supports a positive bounded `lambda`; its own note correctly admits that `corr(S,P)` must be measured rather than posited (`research/e4e5-value-model-v2.md:124`).
- **Why it matters:** After retirement of the multiplier, this qualitative mechanism is half of the publication's claimed contribution. Without a source-to-construct bridge, `P` and `lambda` read as convenient simulator inputs that encode the conclusion rather than as a mechanism grounded in political-economy evidence. The simulation can show consequences conditional on credit pressure; it cannot establish that pressure's prevalence or magnitude.
- **Concrete fix:** Add a short related-work/mechanism subsection and full bibliography entries for the credit-claiming and traceability literature. State exactly which proposition each source supports, its jurisdictional/institutional scope, and what it does **not** estimate. Define `P` and `lambda` operationally, label positive `lambda` as a posited frontier pending project-level measurement, and describe what data would identify it.

### 3. Major — The paper explicitly publishes a novelty claim before doing the prior-art review needed to sustain it

- **Exact locator:** `drafts/paper.md:270`
- **What is wrong:** “What is new” is qualified as “pending a systematic prior-art review,” and the underlying literature map says it is “not yet a formal literature review” (`research/literature-map.md:3`) and still lists “Find papers that support, contradict, or partially anticipate each component” as a research task (`research/literature-map.md:33`). The manuscript nevertheless enumerates seven novelty claims, including an early symmetric-knowledge comparison and an end-to-end institutional comparison.
- **Why it matters:** A publication-readiness audit cannot treat an expressly unfinished search for antecedents as complete. The risk is not cosmetic: the contribution spans public finance, participatory budgeting, computational social choice, audit design, digital governance, and mechanism design, where combination and priority claims require systematic coverage of adjacent work.
- **Concrete fix:** Complete and document a reproducible prior-art search (databases, search strings, dates, screening criteria, and nearest comparators), then rewrite the novelty section as a comparison table of the closest systems/papers against the claimed components. If that work will remain future research, remove priority language and claim only a documented synthesis and object-level specification.

### 4. Major — E7 is described as audit-parameterized and item-by-item justified before its required source verification has cleared

- **Exact locator:** `drafts/paper.md:929`
- **What is wrong:** The manuscript says the status-quo arm is parameterized “exclusively from published audit-institution findings,” and later says its parameters were “justified item-by-item” (`drafts/paper.md:1173`). But the E7 preregistration says the evidence base's verification queue “must clear” before secondary-sourced figures are formally cited (`research/e7-calibrated-baseline-design.md:52`), while that queue is still present and contains six unresolved groups (`research/audit-evidence-base.md:81`), including the candidate CGU operating point (`research/audit-evidence-base.md:83`) and missing report identifiers (`research/audit-evidence-base.md:88`). The paper nevertheless retains the conclusion-level statement that audit at “real, documented intensity” deters no diversion in the model (`drafts/paper.md:1284`).
- **Why it matters:** Calling a baseline calibrated or exclusively evidence-parameterized implies a completed source-to-parameter chain. An open primary-source queue means the chain is not publication-auditable, even if the resulting multiplier is now demoted. The surviving “documented intensity” claim still depends on it.
- **Concrete fix:** Clear the queue and publish a parameter crosswalk giving each model parameter, exact primary source/page/table, construct and denominator, category match, transformation, declared band, and chosen operating point. Until then, relabel E7 throughout as a partially verified, documented-practice-informed scenario; remove “exclusively,” “calibrated,” and “real, documented intensity”; and keep its conclusions out of the abstract and conclusion.

### 5. Major — The paper omits the controlling outcome-measure limitation and instead makes an unsupported universal welfare claim

- **Exact locator:** `drafts/paper.md:31`
- **What is wrong:** The introduction says, “States do not tax to redistribute,” and reframes the purpose of public money as effective delivered value (`drafts/paper.md:35`). The conclusion universalizes the criterion to “any allocation institution” and cites Musgrave and Okun (`drafts/paper.md:1262`, `drafts/paper.md:1264`). The controlling contract says the modeled value is a utilitarian-style cardinal sum, not distributionally weighted, says nothing about redistribution or equity, and applies only to a bounded infrastructure-like public-investment slice (`research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:47`, `research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:52`). Section 8 never imports that distributional boundary. Okun's leaky-bucket argument does not support the categorical denial of redistribution as a tax purpose, and Musgrave's public-finance framework includes distribution rather than licensing its omission.
- **Why it matters:** “Delivered social value” is the paper's normative yardstick, yet the manuscript does not tell readers that its measure can rank a portfolio highly while ignoring who receives benefits and harms. That omission is especially serious for a citizen-allocation architecture whose defaults have acknowledged distributional effects. It also breaches the controlling contract and invites application to domains the estimand expressly excludes.
- **Concrete fix:** Delete the categorical sentence about why states tax and narrow the conclusion to the bounded public-investment selection problem studied. Add a limitations paragraph defining the cardinal aggregate, stating that redistribution/equity and distributional weights are not evaluated, naming the omitted partial-equilibrium channels (strategic reporting, endogenous proposals, complementarities, tax incidence, and general-equilibrium effects), and explaining that a separate social-welfare and incidence specification is required before cross-group policy comparison.
