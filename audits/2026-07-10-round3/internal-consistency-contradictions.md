# Internal consistency & contradictions

## Verdict

**No.** The Path-B decision itself is consistently stated, and the retired multiplier is not reasserted as a current calibrated claim in the scoped publication surfaces. However, two major contradictions remain: summary surfaces conflate the post-hoc ratio-of-sums with the pre-registered decision statistic, and the live roadmap gives mutually incompatible publication/deposit status. Two smaller consistency defects also remain.

## Findings

### 1. Major — The introduction, conclusion, and public companion attribute the post-hoc 0.026 estimate to the pre-registered gate

- **Locators:** `drafts/paper.md:120`, `drafts/paper.md:121`, `drafts/paper.md:122`, `drafts/paper.md:1277`, `drafts/paper.md:1278`, `drafts/paper.md:1279`, `drafts/paper.md:1280`, `docs/101_CORE_V0_MINIMAL_PUBLISHABLE_MODEL.md:5`, `docs/101_CORE_V0_MINIMAL_PUBLISHABLE_MODEL.md:6`, `docs/101_CORE_V0_MINIMAL_PUBLISHABLE_MODEL.md:7`, `docs/101_CORE_V0_MINIMAL_PUBLISHABLE_MODEL.md:8`, `docs/101_CORE_V0_MINIMAL_PUBLISHABLE_MODEL.md:785`, `docs/101_CORE_V0_MINIMAL_PUBLISHABLE_MODEL.md:788`, `docs/101_CORE_V0_MINIMAL_PUBLISHABLE_MODEL.md:789`, `docs/101_CORE_V0_MINIMAL_PUBLISHABLE_MODEL.md:790`.
- **Contradicting side:** the governing Section 6 account says, “the **pre-registered decision statistic** is the **pooled median Δ = 0.025**” (`drafts/paper.md:542`) and then separately says “a **post-hoc** ratio-of-sums estimate is **Δ = 0.026**” (`drafts/paper.md:543`–`drafts/paper.md:544`). The controlling contract likewise distinguishes “pre-registered pooled **median Δ ≈ 0.025**” (`research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:63`) from “the pooled ratio-of-sums ... **Δ = 0.026**” (`research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:64`) and requires the latter to be “reported separately from the median” (`research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:66`).
- **What is wrong:** the introduction says that “a pre-registered, symmetric, selection-only stress test shrinks” the advantage “to a pooled ~0.026” (`drafts/paper.md:120`–`drafts/paper.md:122`). The conclusion is more explicit: “a pre-registered, symmetric, selection-only gate finds” (`drafts/paper.md:1277`–`drafts/paper.md:1278`) “a pooled ~0.026” (`drafts/paper.md:1279`). Both companion summaries repeat the same attribution: the “pre-registered symmetry gate” showed “a pooled ~0.026” (`docs/101_CORE_V0_MINIMAL_PUBLISHABLE_MODEL.md:7`–`docs/101_CORE_V0_MINIMAL_PUBLISHABLE_MODEL.md:8`), and the later “pre-registered ... stress test” returned a “pooled ~0.026” (`docs/101_CORE_V0_MINIMAL_PUBLISHABLE_MODEL.md:785`–`docs/101_CORE_V0_MINIMAL_PUBLISHABLE_MODEL.md:790`). The number is not numerically false; its inferential label is false. It is the post-hoc ratio-of-sums, not the pre-registered decision statistic.
- **Why it matters:** this recreates the exact median-versus-ratio conflation the controlling contract was written to prohibit, including in the conclusion and the Zenodo-promoted accessible companion. A reader can reasonably conclude that 0.026 was the frozen gate statistic and miss that its interval is post-hoc Monte Carlo uncertainty conditional on the simulated DGP.
- **Concrete fix:** in every short summary of the gate, report “pre-registered pooled median Δ = 0.025, below the 0.05 research-program rebuild gate.” If 0.026 is retained outside Section 6, add a separate sentence: “A post-hoc ratio-of-sums was Δ = 0.026 with a 95% world-cluster Monte-Carlo interval [0.023, 0.029].” Apply this to the introduction, conclusion, and both companion summaries.

### 2. Major — The controlling roadmap simultaneously says publication is blocked, round-2 remediation is still active, and re-deposit is the only remaining step

- **Locators:** `docs/03_ROADMAP.md:3`, `docs/03_ROADMAP.md:4`, `docs/03_ROADMAP.md:37`, `docs/03_ROADMAP.md:38`, `docs/03_ROADMAP.md:39`, `audits/2026-07-10/remediation-roadmap.md:3`, `audits/2026-07-10/remediation-roadmap.md:4`, `audits/2026-07-10/remediation-roadmap.md:129`, `audits/2026-07-10/remediation-roadmap.md:130`, `audits/2026-07-10/remediation-roadmap.md:131`, `audits/2026-07-10/remediation-roadmap.md:353`.
- **Contradicting sides:** `docs/03_ROADMAP.md` designates the remediation roadmap as “authoritative” (`docs/03_ROADMAP.md:3`–`docs/03_ROADMAP.md:5`) and says the current phase is “clearing the **round-2 audit** findings” (`docs/03_ROADMAP.md:37`–`docs/03_ROADMAP.md:38`), yet immediately declares “the **only remaining** post-remediation step is the mechanical v1.13 Zenodo re-deposit” (`docs/03_ROADMAP.md:39`). The designated control document still says “publication **RE-BLOCKED; remediation in progress**” (`audits/2026-07-10/remediation-roadmap.md:3`) while also saying “Tiers 0–7 ... are done” (`audits/2026-07-10/remediation-roadmap.md:4`). It additionally records a stale Spanish-summary PDF that “**Must be regenerated ... or removed before any dissemination**” (`audits/2026-07-10/remediation-roadmap.md:129`–`audits/2026-07-10/remediation-roadmap.md:131`) and directs “Re-run the **publication-readiness gate** ... only then submit/deposit” (`audits/2026-07-10/remediation-roadmap.md:353`).
- **What is wrong:** there is no single current project state. The roadmap says remediation is active, says its main tiers are complete, says a dissemination artifact still requires disposition, says a publication gate must still run, and says re-deposit is the only remaining step. Round 3 itself confirms that the gate, not merely mechanical deposit, is the current step (`audits/2026-07-10-round3/AUDIT_PROMPT.md:1`–`audits/2026-07-10-round3/AUDIT_PROMPT.md:6`).
- **Why it matters:** the roadmap is explicitly a publication control surface. Its present wording can authorize a premature deposit even while the same control document says publication is blocked and names a pre-dissemination task. This independently fails the no-notes/roadmap-contradiction gate.
- **Concrete fix:** replace the status blocks in both roadmap files with one dated state: “Round-2 critical/major remediation complete; round-3 publication-readiness gate in progress; do not deposit until its blockers are cleared and the stale PDF is regenerated or removed.” Move the superseded “RE-BLOCKED” narrative into a dated history subsection. After round 3, update the single state block from the synthesized verdict rather than leaving both files to infer status separately.

### 4. Minor — Public summaries still call 0.05 a “materiality” gate without the controlling rebuild-only qualification

- **Locators:** `README.md:31`, `docs/101_CORE_V0_MINIMAL_PUBLISHABLE_MODEL.md:8`, `docs/101_CORE_V0_MINIMAL_PUBLISHABLE_MODEL.md:9`, `docs/101_CORE_V0_MINIMAL_PUBLISHABLE_MODEL.md:789`, `docs/101_CORE_V0_MINIMAL_PUBLISHABLE_MODEL.md:790`, `research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:63`, `research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:64`.
- **Contradicting side:** the manuscript's governing explanation says 0.05 is a “research-program **rebuild threshold** on this uncalibrated scale” (`drafts/paper.md:549`–`drafts/paper.md:550`) and “**not** a calibrated policy-materiality threshold” (`drafts/paper.md:551`–`drafts/paper.md:552`).
- **What is wrong:** README and both companion summaries stop at “0.05 materiality gate,” and even the controlling contract calls it a “materiality bar” without naming the research-rebuild purpose at that point. These statements are not numerically inconsistent, but their semantics are inconsistent across the controlling and public surfaces.
- **Why it matters:** “materiality” naturally reads as policy or welfare materiality. The units are uncalibrated, so only the internal research-program go/no-go interpretation is defensible. The omission is especially risky in the README and accessible companion, where readers may never reach manuscript lines 549–552.
- **Concrete fix:** replace each short-form label with “0.05 research-program rebuild gate on the uncalibrated simulation scale (not a policy-materiality threshold),” including in the contract's interpretation clause.
