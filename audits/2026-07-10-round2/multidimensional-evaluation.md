# Multi-dimensional evaluation — independent audit

**Date:** 2026-07-10 — **Round 2**

## Executive summary

The Path-B correction is substantively real: the controlling contract limits the surviving quantitative claim to a small, conditional selection effect, and the deterministic gate reproduces the reported NO-GO. The current manuscript, however, has not completed that pivot: its abstract and conclusion still use retired or superseded apparatus outputs as headline evidence, including +43% delivery and +53–54% selection, although the contract says a delivery-inclusive effect is future work and “not claimed here.” Several fresh defects weaken rigor further: earlier experiments are still called pre-registered contrary to the controlling contract, the E4 parity discussion reverses the meaning of a 0.89 distributed/central ratio, and the prominent ~10× capture claim is largely generated by stipulated detection and acquisition-cost asymmetries. This is a potentially useful and unusually specified architecture paper, but the current v1.13 draft remains a **major-revisions working paper**, not yet a clean architecture-plus-mechanism contribution.

## Audit basis and calibration

I read `drafts/paper.md` in full, the controlling contract, the frozen gate specification, archived gate results and diagnostics, the formal-model, literature, calibration, audit-evidence, and E4/E5 research notes. I reran `node scripts/simulation/e5-sp-symmetry-gate.mjs` and the diagnostics under Node v24.11.1: the run reproduced the archived 18/18-positive, median-0.025 NO-GO (`audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-results.txt:82-90`) and the corrected pooled Δ = 0.0260, world-cluster 95% interval [0.0233, 0.0288] (`audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-diagnostics.txt:1-3`). Scores use the requested scale: 10 = top-venue seminal; 7 = solid publishable; 5 = workshop/major revisions; 3 = fundamentally weak.

## Ranked findings

### Critical

None. The project no longer makes the Round-1 compound multiplier claim as an estimated effect; the paper explicitly says it is retired (`drafts/paper.md:69-82`) and the contract prohibits it (`research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:70-80`).

### Major

1. **The Path-B retirement is half-applied in the paper.** The contract says a delivery-inclusive `Δ_total` requires a separately estimated delivery model and “is not claimed here,” and says the delivery effect was “not run here” (`research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:21-22,38-44`). Nevertheless, the abstract presents ~1.8× selection, ~10× capture resistance, and ~43%/1.4× delivery as its three results (`drafts/paper.md:29-67`); the conclusion answers its two core questions with +43% and +53–54% (`drafts/paper.md:1318-1335`); and the implementation section says the paper answers “and by how much” (`drafts/paper.md:1231-1237`). “Model-internal” labels prevent these from being mistaken for empirical estimates, but do not reconcile their continued headline and inferential role with the controlling contract.

2. **The manuscript contradicts the controlling provenance rule.** The contract retires the “pre-registered 7/7” framing and states that “the symmetry gate is the one genuinely pre-registered test” (`research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:79-80`). The paper still calls E4, E5, E6, E7, and E8 pre-registered (`drafts/paper.md:725-730,899-902,948-951,979-985,1021-1023`), while README and Zenodo still advertise “eight experiments (six pre-registered)” (`README.md:29-33`; `.zenodo.json:2-5`). The gate file self-describes as frozen before execution (`audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-preregistration.md:1-8`), but prospective freeze-before-run timing is **unverified** independently.

3. **The E4 frontier contains a direction error and a denominator error.** The manuscript says the lower-variance distributed estimator wins at η = 1, β = 0 while reporting “0.89×” (`drafts/paper.md:829-840`). The source defines the ratio as distributed/central and explicitly says values below one mean the central arm wins; its η = 1, β = 0 cell is 0.89 (`research/e4-v4-symmetric-frontier-results.txt:1-16`). The code also gives the distributed arm a participation sample while the central arm observes every interested cell with noise (`scripts/simulation/e4-v4-symmetric-frontier.mjs:8-14,36-50`). Separately, “~1.8× of a full-information oracle” (`drafts/paper.md:785-793`) is malformed: 1.82 is distributed/central, while the same source reports distributed at 71% of the benchmark (`research/e4-v4-symmetric-frontier-results.txt:19-25`).

4. **The abstract-level ~10× capture claim is assumption-driven, not calibrated robustness evidence.** The source fixes central acquisition cost at 0.3 and detection at 0.10, but distributed acquisition at `0.3 × project cost` and detection near one (`research/e4-v5-capture-results.txt:1-5`); its own design states that exact magnitudes are model-internal (`research/e4-v5-capture-design.md:14-19`). Sensitivity is decisive: lowering distributed detection to ~0.3 makes its delivered value negative, and cheaper persuasion materially weakens it (`research/e4-v5-capture-results.txt:27-39`). The abstract instead says critics must assume “near-total civic apathy” to defeat the result (`drafts/paper.md:46-57`). That burden-of-proof language is not supported by estimated detection or acquisition parameters.

5. **The paper does not import the contract's welfare and scope limits.** The contract says the outcome is an uncalibrated utilitarian-style cardinal sum, says nothing about redistribution or equity, is partial-equilibrium, and applies only to a bounded infrastructure-like public-investment slice—not the whole budget or “the purpose of the state” (`research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:46-56`). The manuscript opens with “States do not levy taxes in order to redistribute” (`drafts/paper.md:9-13`), later says the paper answers whether the architecture “delivers more value” (`drafts/paper.md:1207-1209`), and closes by calling delivered value the criterion for any allocation institution (`drafts/paper.md:1311-1317`). Those universal claims exceed the controlling estimand even after the numerical correction.

6. **The paper elevates a mechanism its own current manifest classifies as superseded.** The abstract makes the harm-blindness/voice-inequality frontier its first result (`drafts/paper.md:29-44`), but the underlying backbone marks every magnitude retired and harm-blindness “superseded as primary mechanism” (`research/e4-analytical-backbone.md:3-23`); the current research manifest classifies that E4 line as historical/superseded (`research/README.md:23-29`). The controlling positive claim is instead the narrower credit-pressured-central versus coverage-routed-distributed selection mechanism (`research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:82-90`). This unresolved hierarchy is a clarity and completeness defect, not merely historical traceability.

7. **Novelty is plausible but unverified.** The related-work section usefully distinguishes the proposal from polycentric governance, participatory budgeting, fiscal federalism, liquid democracy, and mechanism design (`drafts/paper.md:260-343`). But its own novelty claim is expressly “pending a systematic prior-art review” (`drafts/paper.md:345-377`), and the literature map calls itself initial and leaves “find papers that support, contradict, or partially anticipate each component” as a research task (`research/literature-map.md:1-4,33-39`). The defensible novelty claim is therefore integration and object-level specification; field-level priority is **unverified**.

### Minor

1. **“Materiality” risks a policy reading it cannot carry.** The 0.05 cutoff is a pre-registered research-program rebuild rule (`audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-preregistration.md:52-62`), but the value units and data-generating distribution are uncalibrated (`research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:46-51`). It is an internal go/no-go threshold, not a calibrated policy-materiality threshold.

2. **The matched-budget description is slightly too exact.** The contract says the total appraisal budget is the same (`research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:24-29`), but the central arm uses `K × round(expectedReports/K)`, so equality holds only up to integer rounding (`scripts/simulation/e5-sp-symmetry-gate.mjs:68-79`). This is unlikely to change the result but should be described as “matched expected budgets, up to rounding.”

3. **The archived verdict misstates what failed.** It says the “advantage does not survive,” although 18/18 cells were positive and the interval excluded zero; only the 0.05 gate failed (`audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-results.txt:82-90`). The paper's “positive but small” wording is correct.

4. **A current paper link points to a superseded calibration note.** The appendix directs readers to `research/e4-calibration-targets.md` for details (`drafts/paper.md:1372-1381`), but that note calls itself superseded and still says the central benchmark is “calibratable now” by a simple overlay (`research/e4-calibration-targets.md:3-6,18-34`), which conflicts with the paper's corrected requirement for an explicit construct bridge.

5. **The formal note retains exclusion language the architecture later rejected.** It defines reputation as future “protocol eligibility,” uses “exclusion” as a penalty, and treats credible exclusion as load-bearing (`research/formal-models.md:45-51,149-153,189-195`); the paper says reputation informs choice and “never excludes” (`drafts/paper.md:939-942,973-977`). The algebra can use continuation loss without exclusion, but the supporting note must state that mechanism consistently.

6. **The remediation roadmap reintroduced a license/deposit contradiction.** Its banner still says the author chose dual licensing, a protected manuscript, separate records, and two v1.13 deposits (`audits/2026-07-10/remediation-roadmap.md:3-11`), while the same file later records the final single-record CC-BY decision (`audits/2026-07-10/remediation-roadmap.md:117-133`), consistent with `LICENSE.md:5-11` and `RELEASING.md:17-23`.

### Nit

The gate output still labels the low-information case “ADVERSARIAL” (`audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-results.txt:43-44`), although diagnostics correctly require “matched-budget low-information stress regime” to prevent a participation-resilience misreading (`audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-diagnostics-notes.md:24-35`).

No material EN/ES divergence was found in the governing Path-B passages: the Spanish mirror carries the same v1.13/deposit state and retirement statement (`drafts/paper.es.md:1-5`), the same 0.026/[0.023,0.029]/0.05 result (`drafts/paper.es.md:79-90,668-682`), and the same retained +43%/+53–54% claims (`drafts/paper.es.md:987-1004,1405-1422`). This is consistency, not validation: the same substantive defects are mirrored.

## Dimension scores

### 1. Clarity — 5/10

The intended architecture and honest scope can be followed: the paper gives a concrete Core v0 summary with funding, role separation, conditional disbursement, attention infrastructure, and transition modes (`drafts/paper.md:384-461`), and the governing gate paragraph is unusually explicit (`drafts/paper.md:611-626`). But the Path-B thesis is buried beneath a very long abstract and roughly 460 lines of computational findings, and older 1.8×, 10×, 1.4×, 2.19×, 2.22×, and 2.26× figures remain prominent (`drafts/paper.md:29-82,604-626`). A reviewer can reconstruct the honest claim, but should not have to arbitrate between the contract, the retired E4 mechanism, and the manuscript's headline hierarchy.

### 2. Completeness — 5/10

The manuscript covers the intended chain—functional decomposition, object-level architecture, elementary formal mechanisms, simulations, adversarial method, limits, and transition (`drafts/paper.md:219-258,384-571,1037-1307`). Load-bearing pieces remain missing: the delivery/capture magnitudes are not identified by the controlling estimand (`research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:21-22,38-56`), novelty review is unfinished (`drafts/paper.md:345-346`), and independent expert review, empirical PB calibration, and a bounded pilot are explicitly future work (`drafts/paper.md:1220-1229`). The paper is end-to-end as a design dossier, not end-to-end as evidence that the institution improves real delivered value.

### 3. Quality (rigor & soundness) — 5/10

The strongest act of rigor is the committed gate: it removes the oracle eligibility advantage, pure-credit central objective, and stipulated delivery superiority (`audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-preregistration.md:10-39`), then obeys the failed magnitude rule (`audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-results.txt:82-90`). Against that, the current manuscript contains the E4 direction/denominator errors, continues provenance language the contract disallows, and treats a heavily stipulated capture comparison as a substantive result. The formal models are transparent and hand-checkable in intent (`research/formal-models.md:3-18`), but transparency does not offset incorrect interpretation or unsupported generalization.

### 4. Innovation / novelty — 6/10

Functional rather than territorial decomposition, joined to an object-level allocation/execution/evidence/verification architecture, is a potentially useful synthesis; the paper clearly distinguishes this combination from PB and fiscal federalism (`drafts/paper.md:271-303`) and maps formal parameters to implementable objects (`drafts/paper.md:321-343`). That is more than a generic decentralization proposal. It is not yet a demonstrated field novelty because the systematic prior-art review is expressly pending (`drafts/paper.md:345-377`) and the literature map remains preliminary (`research/literature-map.md:1-4`). Score 6 reflects credible integrative novelty, not verified priority or a new deep mechanism-design theorem.

### 5. Impact / relevance — 6/10

The project addresses a consequential problem and names plausible users: municipalities or authorities testing a bounded tutored allocation layer, with explicit maturity metrics and exit conditions (`drafts/paper.md:1231-1254`). Its object model and transition ladder could organize pilots and interdisciplinary debate even without a large effect. Impact is capped because binding citizen allocation lacks an enabling norm, verification supply is assumed, mature meta-governance is deferred, and adoption likely selects relatively clean sponsors (`drafts/paper.md:1149-1218`); no pilot has run (`drafts/paper.md:1220-1229`). The small, uncalibrated selection sign supports a research agenda, not a current policy performance claim.

### 6. Evidence & calibration — 5/10

The defensible result is narrow and correctly stated in the contract: 18/18 simulated cells are positive, pooled median ≈0.025, ratio-of-sums 0.026 with a conditional Monte Carlo interval, below the rebuild gate (`research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:58-68`). The project also corrected the naive interval with world clustering (`audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-diagnostics-notes.md:7-17`). But values, appraisal errors, sponsor/public-value alignment, and the project distribution are unmeasured (`research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:46-61`); the audit evidence parameterizes a baseline but includes an explicit primary-source verification queue (`research/audit-evidence-base.md:81-92`); and no delivery effect is identified under the controlling estimand. “Small conditional effect, not a calibrated multiplier” is defensible; stronger institutional-performance language is not.

### 7. Reproducibility — 7/10

The gate is dependency-free in practice, fixes the world count, seed base, and full sweep (`audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-preregistration.md:44-50`), and the code hard-codes those values (`scripts/simulation/e5-sp-symmetry-gate.mjs:102-117`). My rerun exactly matched both archived gate and diagnostic outputs at printed precision (`audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-results.txt:82-90`; `audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-diagnostics.txt:1-3`). It falls short of stronger reproducibility because `package.json` exposes no gate command, only anchor checks (`package.json:6-9`), no Node engine/version is pinned, results are plain text rather than a machine-readable world-level artifact, and the file's self-asserted freeze-before-run timing is unverified independently (`audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-preregistration.md:1-8`).

### 8. Honesty / self-critique — 7/10

The project did the important honest thing: it reported NO-GO, retired the compound effect, distinguishes Monte Carlo from empirical uncertainty, and states that the gate is a stylized selection mechanism rather than a validated Core v0 implementation (`research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:46-68`; `drafts/paper.md:611-626`). It also records failed predictions (`drafts/paper.md:706-723,926-942,961-969`) and admits the review loop is self-critique with AI assistance, not external validation (`drafts/paper.md:1090-1095`). The score is not higher because the paper still overstates prior preregistration, universalizes beyond its contract, and gives retired/superseded figures headline space. This is genuine correction with incomplete propagation, not yet fully disciplined self-representation.

## Overall

**Overall score: 5/10 — Promising, unusually specified architecture research with a credible narrow mechanism demonstration, but major revisions are required before the current draft is internally sound and honestly Path B.**

## Three greatest strengths

1. The functional decomposition is instantiated as a concrete, traceable architecture rather than left as a slogan (`drafts/paper.md:384-461`).
2. The project obeyed a failed go/no-go test and now reports the small conditional selection result with the correct clustered interval (`audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-results.txt:82-90`; `audits/2026-07-10/symmetry-gate-diagnostics.txt:1-3`).
3. The public adversarial record and explicit failed predictions expose genuine residual risks rather than hiding them (`drafts/paper.md:1037-1095,1097-1229`).

## Three greatest weaknesses

1. The manuscript still uses retired or superseded magnitudes as abstract/conclusion evidence, so the Path-B correction is not yet coherent (`drafts/paper.md:29-82,1318-1335`).
2. There is no identified real-world treatment effect, delivery estimate, or pilot, and the gate's welfare units and data-generating process are uncalibrated (`research/claim-and-estimand-contract.md:46-68`; `drafts/paper.md:1220-1229`).
3. Systematic prior-art review is unfinished and the surviving old-apparatus discussion contains concrete interpretation errors (`drafts/paper.md:345-377,829-840`).

## Publication recommendation

**Would I recommend publishing the working paper now, as an honest architecture+mechanism contribution? No.** First remove or demote the old apparatus magnitudes from the abstract and conclusion, reconcile all preregistration labels with the contract, correct the E4 ratio/parity errors, and import the contract's welfare/scope limits; after those bounded revisions, an honest architecture-plus-qualitative-mechanism working paper would be defensible.
