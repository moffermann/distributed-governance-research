# Review Packet — Intelligent Non-Expert Reader

## Why I am sending this to you

I am developing an independent research project on making some public projects more transparent, measurable, and accountable.

I am not asking you for a technical, legal, or academic review.

I am asking you to tell me whether the idea is understandable and what sounds unclear, suspicious, unrealistic, confusing, or too complicated.

If this does not make sense to an intelligent non-expert, the project is not ready to be communicated publicly.

## Compact paper summary

The project asks whether modern technology can help improve citizen participation, reduce corruption opportunities, increase accountability, and help manage some public projects without destroying existing institutions.

The basic idea is this:

A public project should not just ask for money and then disappear into a black box.

It should clearly say:

```text
what value it promises
who benefits
how success will be measured
what evidence will prove it
who will execute it
who will check it
when money can be released
what happens if people complain
how it closes
how the executor's reputation changes afterward
```

So instead of trusting only a ministry, contractor, platform, or crowd, the project must go through a visible lifecycle:

```text
promise
→ funding
→ evidence
→ fiscalization
→ milestone payment
→ complaints/corrections
→ closure
→ reputation
```

The model is not trying to replace the whole state.

It is trying to design a better way to manage some public projects so citizens can understand what is promised, what is paid, what was proven, what failed, and who should be trusted next time.

## What I need from you

Please focus on clarity.

Do not worry about whether you understand every technical term.

I need to know:

- whether the basic idea makes sense;
- where the language becomes too abstract;
- what sounds suspicious;
- what sounds unrealistic;
- what example would help;
- whether the project feels useful or too complicated.

## Key questions

```text
1. Can you explain the idea in your own words?
2. Where did you get lost?
3. What sounded unrealistic?
4. What sounded politically suspicious?
5. What sounded technically confusing?
6. What example would help you understand it better?
7. What sentence made the idea click?
8. What sentence felt like jargon?
9. Would you trust a project governed this way more than a normal public project? Why or why not?
10. What would you need to see in practice to believe it works?
```
