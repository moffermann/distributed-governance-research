# D043 - Defense Against A043: The Verification Layer Under Machines and Rings

## Integration status

Paired review completed. Accepted resolution: [[113_VERIFICATION_PACKAGE_AND_A043_RESOLUTION|docs/113_VERIFICATION_PACKAGE_AND_A043_RESOLUTION.md]] (author verdict 2026-07-07, with the collusion scope made explicit). Corpus propagation done with the resolution.

## Attack reference

- Attack file: [[A043-verification-displacement-and-collusion|attacks/A043-verification-displacement-and-collusion.md]]
- Attack title: `A043 - The Verification Layer Under Machines and Rings: Displacement, Blind Trust, and Collusion`
- Source: satellite Experiments E-1c/E-1d, F, G, doi:10.5281/zenodo.21246089.

## Attack summary

Cheap machine verification displaces the human fiscalizer market that audits it, can blindly pass correlated fraud (the panel found ensembles fail worse than independence predicts and a healthy system's outcome audit is blind), and — deepest — is bypassed entirely by a colluding ring that controls executor, verifiers, and contributors, which Experiment G found the only attack to move leakage by an order of magnitude.

## Objective evaluation

- Classification: founded; the collusion component partially reframes rather than defeats the architecture.
- Why it is founded: all three risks are measured, not hypothetical; the displacement breaks H016's paid-market assumption, the blind-trust risk is real under a naive single-model design, and collusion bypasses the per-milestone deterrence the whole verification story rests on.
- Why it is not fatal: the verified-value advantage survives collusion in the satellite (the honest majority carries delivery); what collusion moves is *leakage*, and the remedies (second instance, seeded controls, contributor independence, beneficial-ownership verification, root decentralization) are bounded integrations over existing objects — no new mechanism defeats the architecture's thesis, they harden its deployment.
- Difference of judgment: medium. The collusion scope is a genuine limitation that must be stated, not engineered away.
- Editorial distortion risk: high. "AI replaces auditors" and "collusion breaks it" are both quotable overshoots the resolution must bound: humans remain the second instance, and collusion raises leakage while the delivered-value advantage holds.

## Response

The resolution turns the machine into triage and keeps humans as the permanent, budget-floored second instance whose job is auditing the machine — displacement is prevented by a *rule* (a published control-budget floor set by an independent party), not by hoping the market survives. The lax machine-accuracy requirement is conditioned on a positive docs/111 margin (the slack that absorbs verifier error). Coverage is universal in *exposure* and unpredictable in *selection*, with the shared-blind-spot flaw classes mandatory and coverage value-weighted. Seeded positive controls are the epistemic instrument, valid only if indistinguishable — real-project stings, secret rotated manifold, separated seeder. Contraposition is a control instrument bounded by contributor independence: it holds against a non-colluding adversary and is halved under collusion, so independence is established (Sybil-resistance), confirmation is non-suppressive, and filing is bonded. Finally, cross-layer collusion resistance is first-class — verified beneficial ownership defeats shell-cycling, and the assigner/seeder/floor-setter are decentralized and adversarially reviewed so a ring cannot route its fraud to its own captured reviewer.

## Proposed improvements

- machine triage + permanent human second instance on an independently-set published floor;
- automatic lane gated on a positive docs/111 margin; evidence contracts carry comparison references;
- universal exposure, unpredictable selection, value-weighted coverage, blind-spot classes mandatory;
- indistinguishable seeded controls, separately operated;
- contributor independence established, confirmation non-suppressive, filing bonded;
- verified beneficial ownership; decentralized, adversarially-reviewed assigner/seeder/floor-setter.

## Applies to

- evidence contracts and ingestion validation;
- control budget and its floor-setting authority;
- fiscalizer roles and the complaint path (docs/26);
- operating-mode configuration (the coverage plan);
- semi-open envelope preconditions (docs/110).

## Defense strength and residual risk

Defense strength: strong on displacement and blind-trust (bounded integrations, measured); partial on collusion — the resolution *hardens* against it and states the scope honestly, but cannot claim to defeat a determined ring.

Residual risk: seeded-control indistinguishability against a registry-reading captured operator; contributor-layer capture; and root capture of the assigner/seeder/floor-setter — the single largest residual, and the reason the human corps is sized as a security parameter. These are named as the standing open problems the deployment must engineer against, not solved here.

## Decision

Founded; accepted as docs/113 — the verification package with the collusion scope explicit. The verified-value advantage holds; the honest statement is that machine + citizen verification covers document-legible delivery-phase fraud under a non-colluding adversary, and cross-layer collusion is the frontier.
