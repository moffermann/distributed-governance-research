# D011 - Defense Against A011: Moderation Abuse in Transition Pilots

## Attack reference

- Attack file: `attacks/A011-moderation-abuse-in-transition-pilots.md`
- Attack title: `A011 - Moderation Abuse in Transition Pilots`
- Roadmap source: Phase 3 priority objection, moderation abuse in transition pilots.

## Attack summary

The attack argues that tutored and semi-open modes can become discretionary control. Authorities may reject competitors, delay uncomfortable projects, favor controlled operators, or define the scope so narrowly that distribution becomes symbolic. In Macul, a municipality could reject independent sports projects as "duplicates" while protecting its own operator or ordinary budget.

## Objective evaluation

- Classification: founded, especially during transition.
- Why it is founded: tutored review creates real gatekeeping power.
- Why it is not fatal to the architecture: C020 does not pretend to eliminate tutored authority; it requires material tutored decisions and tutored silence to become public civic objects. C007 separates public authority from internal project participation in scopes it controls.
- Difference of judgment: medium. A country may legitimately keep a public function tutored; the project cannot force open mode.
- Editorial distortion risk: high if the attack is used either to prohibit all tutored modes or to normalize opaque tutored discretion. The project needs transition realism with public traceability.

## Response

The defense is that tutored mode may exist, but tutored opacity is the contradiction.

An authority may review eligibility, duplication, scope, and compatibility where the operating mode grants that external authority. But material decisions must become Governance Resolutions or Review Timeout Resolutions with reason, authority, scope, rule version, timeout policy, and civic audit path.

For Macul, rejecting a private multi-court project as duplicate may be valid if the reason is public, comparable, tied to an active scope, and not used to privilege the authority's controlled operator.

## Proposed improvements

Add a tutored-moderation abuse test:

- rejection rate by reason, scope, actor type, and authority-linked operator;
- review delay and timeout rate;
- duplicate or outside-scope reason comparability;
- operator concentration inside tutored scopes;
- authority-controlled or privileged operator flag;
- citizen-facing moderation pattern dashboard.

## Applies to

- OperatingMode;
- PlanningScope;
- GovernanceResolution;
- ReviewTimeoutResolution;
- tutored eligibility review;
- state-owned or authority-controlled operator eligibility;
- administrative observability.

## Defense strength and residual risk

Defense strength: strong for transparency, limited for legal power.

Residual risk: visibility may not stop an authority that legally controls the scope. The platform cannot force political transition, but it can prevent hidden gatekeeping from being presented as distributed governance.

## Decision

The attack is founded. The architecture has the correct boundary, but Phase 3 should add moderation-abuse metrics and operator-concentration observability for transition pilots.
