# Concept — Initial Institutional Taxonomy

## Defense

Highly centralized. Strong case for hierarchy and unified command.

## Security

Partially distributable by function, not necessarily by territory. Core coercive authority remains carefully bounded. Specialized complementary institutions may emerge.

## Justice

Distributable through arbitration, mediation, specialized tribunals, and agreed jurisdiction under a common legal framework.

## Legislative function

Highly distributable in proposal, deliberation, review, expert input, and citizen participation. Final legitimacy mechanisms require further design.

## Executive resource allocation

Strongly distributable where ministries act mainly as centralized budget allocators. Schools, hospitals, foundations, research centers, and cultural organizations can receive support through distributed resource assignment.
