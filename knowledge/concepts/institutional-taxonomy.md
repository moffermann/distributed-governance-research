# Concept — Initial Institutional Taxonomy

## Defense

Highly centralized for operational command, doctrine, and organized force. Oversight, auditability, procurement transparency, veteran support, and public accountability may still have hybrid or distributed layers where security constraints allow.

## Security

Partially distributable by functional layer, not by unbounded territorial fragmentation. Core coercive authority remains centralized or tightly bounded under law. Prevention, victim support, non-coercive community observation, evidence production, and oversight may be distributed through specialized complementary institutions.

## Justice

Hybrid by layer. Enforceable legal authority, due process, rights protection, and legal recognition require common frameworks. Mediation, arbitration-like mechanisms, specialized review, legal aid, evidence support, transparency, and case accountability can be more distributed under those frameworks.

## Legislative function

Highly distributable in proposal, deliberation, review, expert input, contradiction detection, simulation, and citizen participation. Final legitimacy, enactment, constitutional compatibility, and enforceability mechanisms require common rules and further design.

## Executive resource allocation

Strongly distributable at the bounded allocation, project execution, evidence, fiscalization, and accountability layers where ministries act mainly as centralized budget allocators. The aggregate fiscal envelope, non-assignable common pool, legal custody, and macro fiscal stability remain central or country-implemented coordination layers.

Schools, hospitals, foundations, research centers, cultural organizations, care providers, utilities, concessionaires, and other eligible operators can receive support through bounded distributed resource assignment only where active scopes, eligibility rules, thresholds, evidence, fiscalization, and accountability conditions are satisfied.
