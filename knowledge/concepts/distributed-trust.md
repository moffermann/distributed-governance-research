# Concept — Distributed Trust

Distributed trust is the idea that confidence in social systems should emerge from verifiability, transparency, auditability, reputation, and multiple independent actors rather than from blind dependence on a single authority.

## Key phrase

> We do not need to trust whoever publishes the information. We need to be able to verify it.

## Applications

- information integrity;
- public spending;
- institutional performance;
- arbitration;
- private agreements;
- governance processes.

## Related product idea

A general trust platform for human agreements: AI structures an agreement, money or guarantees are protected, evidence is preserved, and disputes are resolved through predefined arbitration.
