# Concept — Functional State Architecture

Functional State Architecture means analyzing the state by function rather than by inherited institutional form.

## Core idea

Instead of asking whether the state should be larger or smaller, ask:

- What function must be performed?
- What is the nature of the function?
- Does it depend on force, information, deliberation, trust, coordination, or resource allocation?
- What degree of centralization minimizes risk and maximizes value?

## Why it matters

A single ideological answer cannot fit all state functions. Defense, policing, courts, legislation, education funding, and cultural support are not the same kind of problem.

## Architectural principle

Centralize where fragmentation creates unacceptable risks. Distribute where centralization creates information loss, weak accountability, or poor incentives.
