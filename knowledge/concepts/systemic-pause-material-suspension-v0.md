# Systemic Pause, Material Suspension, and Final Resolution v0

## Purpose

This concept separates three effects that are easy to confuse when a complaint, antivalue discovery, or serious project failure appears:

```text
system effect inside the platform
material or legal effect outside the platform
final resolution that can close, revoke, compensate, or update reputation
```

The distinction keeps project control strong without turning the platform or the fiscalizer into a court, regulator, or physical enforcement authority.

## Systemic Pause

A `Systemic Pause` is a scoped platform state that temporarily blocks or restricts project actions inside the system.

It may affect:

- execution funding collection for the affected scope;
- release of funds;
- milestone advancement;
- phase gate acceptance;
- closure;
- related disbursement;
- related evidence or fiscalization status;
- audit preparation and referral packaging.

A systemic pause must identify its affected scope:

- whole project;
- project phase;
- milestone;
- disbursement;
- budget line;
- contextualized evidence item;
- actor relationship;
- legal or safety condition.

Rule:

> A systemic pause is an internal protocol effect. It can stop funds or system advancement, but it does not by itself physically halt construction, revoke a permit, suspend a legal right, or impose a legal sanction.

## Material or Legal Suspension

A `Material or Legal Suspension` is an external-world effect such as a construction halt, permit suspension, operational prohibition, legal sanction, or binding physical stop.

It requires one of the following:

- court order;
- regulator order;
- competent authority resolution;
- enforceable legal rule;
- enforceable contract or protocol obligation accepted by the relevant actor.

The fiscalizer may recommend or request this effect through a referral package, but the platform should not pretend that fiscalizer admissibility alone creates the external legal effect.

## Complaint Admissibility / Referral Record

A `Complaint Admissibility / Referral Record` is the record created when the competent reviewer or fiscalizer evaluates whether a complaint can proceed after the configured support, quote, funding, and evidence requirements are met.

Minimum fields:

- complaint reference;
- reviewer or fiscalizer;
- admissibility result;
- affected scope;
- blocking or non-blocking classification;
- systemic pause effects, if any;
- funding, disbursement, milestone, or phase effects;
- complaint evidence index;
- actor responses considered;
- referral package reference, if sent to a court, regulator, or competent authority;
- requested material or legal measure, if any;
- rule for lifting or maintaining the systemic pause;
- reputation status: pending signal, reviewed input, or no reputation effect.

Rule:

> Admissibility can create a scoped system effect. It does not prove final responsibility by itself.

## Final Resolution

A `Final Resolution` is the decision or reviewed outcome that can support final closure, revocation, compensation, recovery, or role-specific reputation effects.

It may come from:

- court or competent authority decision;
- regulator order;
- final fiscalization conclusion where protocol gives that effect;
- project closure or revocation rule after review;
- confirmed non-compliance or confirmed absence of non-compliance;
- executor abandonment or refusal to correct when classified as breach under the applicable rules.

Rule:

> Formal negative reputation effects should arise from final resolution, founded responsibility, verified non-compliance, or a reviewed Responsibility Event, not from mere complaint filing or admissibility.

## Funding Boundary

A systemic pause may stop new execution funding or release of funds for the affected scope.

It should not necessarily stop:

- complaint review funding;
- control funding;
- mitigation funding;
- correction funding;
- authority-referral preparation;
- funds for unaffected project scopes where separable.

## Example

In the Macul multi-court project, citizens denounce that the proposed courts lack bathrooms or have incorrect dimensions.

If the complaint reaches support, quote, funding, and admissibility requirements, the fiscalizer may create an admissibility/referral record. The platform may pause the construction phase, block release of construction funds, and preserve unreleased funds for that affected scope.

That does not yet mean the executor has a final negative reputation input. It is a pending public procedural signal. If a court or competent authority later confirms the defect, or if the project review establishes role-specific responsibility, the system may create a `Reputation Input` for the designer, executor, fiscalizer, or another responsible actor according to the role actually breached.

## Status

Core v0 concept aligned with H013, H014, H024, and C004.
