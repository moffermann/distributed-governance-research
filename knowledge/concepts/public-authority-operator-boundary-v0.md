# Public Authority / State-Owned Operator Boundary v0

## Status

Core v0 concept aligned with [[../../docs/43_PUBLIC_INSTITUTION_EXCLUSION_AND_C007_RESOLUTION|C007]] and [[../hypotheses/H004-institutional-monopolies|H004]].

## Definition

The public-authority / state-owned-operator boundary separates two concepts that should not be merged:

```text
Public authority:
  an actor exercising legal, budgetary, regulatory, eligibility,
  admissibility, tutored, fiscal, adjudicative, or coercive power.

State-owned operator:
  a publicly owned or publicly controlled enterprise acting as a service
  provider, utility, concessionaire, supplier, or executor under ordinary
  project-accountability rules.
```

## Core rule

> Authority power creates the exclusion. Public ownership alone does not always create the exclusion.

A public authority cannot be an internal project actor in a scope where it controls admission, scope, regulation, fiscalization, disbursement, funding, or tutored review.

A state-owned operator may participate only when it acts as an ordinary eligible organization under equal rules and does not carry privileged approval, regulatory, fiscal, funding, or review power over the same scope.

## Why this matters

The system weakens the ordinary public/private distinction because:

- private firms may deliver public services with public funding;
- public enterprises may compete with private firms as operators;
- civic organizations may execute public-value projects;
- public resources may move through accountable project funding rather than traditional institutional budgets.

The important question is therefore not simply:

```text
Is this actor public or private?
```

The important questions are:

```text
Is this actor exercising authority?
Is this actor competing as an operator?
Does it have privileged access, immunity, subsidy, or control?
Does the same authority family review, admit, regulate, or fund its project?
```

## Operating-mode implications

In tutored mode, a state-owned or publicly controlled operator is excluded by default when it is controlled by, directed by, materially privileged by, or materially dependent on the same authority that defines scope, reviews admissibility, approves publication, controls relevant budget, or regulates the same distributed-financing scope.

In non-tutored or open mode, a state-owned operator may be eligible if it declares ownership/control relationships, follows the same value, evidence, fiscalization, assurance, complaint, disbursement, reputation, and revocation rules as other operators, and has no privileged path over selection or review.

## Example

Allowed pattern:

```text
A state-owned sanitary company competes in an open mode against private
concessionaires under equal project, evidence, guarantee, fiscalization,
reputation, and revocation rules.
```

Rejected pattern:

```text
A ministry controls a tutored pilot and also allows its controlled public
enterprise to compete as executor inside that same pilot.
```

## Design rule

> The model should classify actors by function, mode, and control relationship before classifying them by public/private label.
