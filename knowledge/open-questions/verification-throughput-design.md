# Verification Throughput Design

## Question

How does Core v0 raise and regulate the throughput of milestone verification — the resource Experiment E-1a identified as the ceiling of the entire delivery pipeline — without corrupting verification's anti-fraud function?

## Origin

The satellite Experiment E-1a (longitudinal engine v0.6, 2026-07-06) found that verification capacity is the pipeline's ceiling before it is the anti-fraud instrument: with scarce fiscalizer capacity no budget-release policy recovers delivered value, and over-release actively destroys it (queue, then less delivery). The author responded with two improvement proposals, extended in a recorded brainstorm to a six-instrument package. This note preserves the design direction; the quantified evaluation is pre-registered as satellite Experiment E-1c, and the package travels to the corpus through the attack → defense → resolution pipeline with evidence attached (the A041 pattern), at the author's verdict.

## Author design direction (recorded 2026-07-06)

**Instrument 1 — verification windows (author's proposal, corrected in discussion).** Fiscalization is the only pipeline actor without a clock (docs/58 gives the authority declared windows with silence consequences; [[104_CLOSURE_DEADLINE_EXPIRY_AND_C040_RESOLUTION|docs/104_CLOSURE_DEADLINE_EXPIRY_AND_C040_RESOLUTION.md]] expires closure deadlines) — an asymmetry E-1a made visible. Design rules fixed in the discussion:

- Fines apply **only to contracted fiscalizers** paid from the control budget (docs/40, docs/52) — a contractual SLA. Fining volunteers would collapse the already-thin control market (A022).
- **Volunteers get timeout → automatic reassignment** of the milestone plus a reputational record of the timeout ([[107_REPUTATION_INFORMS_NEVER_EXCLUDES_AND_A038_RESOLUTION|docs/107_REPUTATION_INFORMS_NEVER_EXCLUDES_AND_A038_RESOLUTION.md]]: informs, never excludes).
- **The rubber-stamp counterweight is mandatory**: deadline pressure with no accuracy consequence produces fast wrong approvals — corrupting the anti-fraud instrument to save latency. Every window regime ships with random re-verification sampling and symmetric consequences: lateness costs, negligence costs more.
- Queueing honesty: timeouts bound the *tail* (stragglers), they do not add servers. Capacity instruments raise the mean.

**Instrument 2 — AI fiscalizer as triage, not replacement (author's proposal, architecture fixed in discussion).** Evidence contracts already require evidence that is measured and quantifiable against the value thesis, so a large class of verification is machine-checkable by construction (geolocation, timestamps, document completeness, invoice-price cross-checks, computer vision on physical progress, signed sensor data). The AI tutor already lowered the citizen side's participation cost; this is the same thesis move on the control side. Three lanes:

1. **Auto-release**: the AI verifies every milestone instantly; high-confidence results in *protocolizable evidence classes* release the tranche automatically — the same demarcation rule as [[110_OPERATING_REGIME_LADDER_AND_SEMI_OPEN_MODE|docs/110_OPERATING_REGIME_LADDER_AND_SEMI_OPEN_MODE.md]]: what can be protocolized enters the automatic lane; what cannot stays human.
2. **Referral with dossier**: unresolved or anomalous cases route to a human fiscalizer with the AI's analysis attached — multiplying human productivity per verification, not merely offloading.
3. **Unconditional human sampling**: a random share of AI-approved milestones is always re-verified by humans — measuring the AI's error rate continuously, catching drift and gaming, and keeping every approval contestable.

Guardrails carried from the corpus: the verifier model is **published, versioned, and contestable** — a model change is a material rule change under the docs/57 discipline; the AI's operator must not be judge and party ([[43_PUBLIC_INSTITUTION_EXCLUSION_AND_C007_RESOLUTION|docs/43_PUBLIC_INSTITUTION_EXCLUSION_AND_C007_RESOLUTION.md]]); and the declared boundary: an AI verifier is a **new attack surface** (adversarial evidence, staged imagery) — lane 3 and evidence-producer diversity mitigate, never eliminate.

**Instruments 3–6 (from the brainstorm).**

3. *Published, release-coupled verification capacity*: each scope publishes its capacity and current queue (the docs/111 pattern applied to throughput), and the E-1a pull release rule couples to it mechanically — freezing becomes visible before it happens.
4. *Verification demand smoothing*: the platform staggers milestone due dates across projects to flatten the fiscalization demand curve — pure scheduling, near-zero cost.
5. *Dynamic verification pricing and cross-scope pools*: queue growth raises the per-verification fee (paid from the control budget), pulling in elastic supply; remote evidence lets fiscalizers verify across scope boundaries, federating a thin market.
6. *Self-verifying evidence at the source*: evidence contracts privilege machine-verifiable formats (signed, geotagged, structured); unstructurable evidence pays a higher control budget — lowering cost per milestone at the source.

**Package logic**: instruments 2 and 6 raise the ceiling (multiply capacity); 1 and 4 fix the queue (variance and peaks); 3 and 5 make the system self-regulating (visibility and elasticity).

## Scope classification

Deployment-layer and control-market design over existing objects (evidence contracts, control budgets, fiscalizer roles, operating configuration). No new powers, no exclusion mechanisms, no change to who may verify. The AI verifier's certification authority is bounded by the protocolizability demarcation and lane 3's sampling.

## The verifier-displacement problem and the second-instance design (author, recorded 2026-07-06)

After E-1c/E-1b confirmed the AI triage design, the author identified its institutional side effect: **an AI verifier is a powerful competitor to human fiscalizers — it collapses both the cost of fiscalization and the incentive to fiscalize**, and that breaks several Core v0 assumptions at once: the distributed fiscalization ecosystem (H016) assumes a living human control market; the fiscalizer offer flow (docs/23) assumes the role is worth offering into; the thin-control-market analysis (A022) assumed scarcity, not displacement; control-budget pricing (docs/40, docs/52) assumed human fees as the price signal; the distributed accountability loop (H008) treats citizens-watching as *constitutive*, not merely instrumental; and — the sharpest edge, from E-1c's own finding — lane 3's human sampling is the **epistemic instrument that measures the AI**: if the human profession exits, the system loses the only tool that knows the machine's real error rate. The auditors of the AI must exist for the AI to be auditable.

**Author's solution direction:** human fiscalization always enters as a **second instance** — auditor of the AI and of the verification system — rather than as a first-pass competitor.

**Recorded design (proposed in discussion, pending quantified evaluation and pipeline formalization):**

1. **Role reframe.** The human fiscalizer's job becomes second-instance auditing: re-verifying sampled AI verdicts, investigating flags and anomalies, handling complaint-triggered reviews (docs/26, docs/79), and calibrating the verifier — a profession whose object is the verification *system*, not the milestone queue.
2. **The incentive fix is a budget rule, not a price.** The displacement collapses incentives only if human fiscalization competes with the machine's near-zero price. It must not: the control budget carries a **published floor share for second-instance human auditing** — AI savings re-allocate toward the human audit layer, never below the floor. Human audit demand becomes a mandated institutional complement, not a substitutable service. This is the complements-not-substitutes discipline of [[111_DETERRENCE_STACK_INTEGRITY_AND_A041_RESOLUTION|docs/111_DETERRENCE_STACK_INTEGRITY_AND_A041_RESOLUTION.md]] promoted to the control labor market.
3. **The frontier: universal exposure, selective inspection.** The author's open question — must the human second instance cover every project? — resolves the way audit institutions already resolve it: deterrence requires universal *exposure*, not universal *inspection*. Every disbursement faces a nonzero, published probability of human second-instance review (the random floor `s_min`); the **mandatory set** (probability 1) is defined by: (a) non-protocolizable evidence classes (the docs/110 demarcation — already human-first); (b) stakes above a scope-published threshold; (c) executors with thin or absent track record; (d) AI-flagged anomalies; (e) complaint-triggered reviews — any formal complaint forces a human instance; (f) **post-model-change windows** — after any verifier model update (a material rule change under the docs/57 discipline), elevated sampling until recalibration. The sampling plan is a published object in the scope configuration, the docs/111 pattern applied to audit coverage.
4. **The floor is set by epistemics, not by leak-plugging.** E-1c measured that deterrence held at a 5% sample under an intact stack; the binding constraint on `s_min` is **drift-detection latency** — time-to-detect a degrading or gamed AI scales inversely with (sample rate × audited exposure), so the floor is chosen for how fast the deployment must notice a bad verifier, not for how much leak it catches.
5. **Citizen voluntary fiscalization is not displaced — it is upgraded.** It never competed on price (it was never paid); with AI dossiers attached, the cost of citizen watching falls (the AI-tutor move on the control side), and the citizen's role sharpens into auditing the auditors. The offer flow (docs/23) stays open.

Declared consequence for the E-1c finding: the AI lane's lax accuracy requirement was already conditional on an intact deterrence stack (E-1b: verification is downstream of deterrence); this section adds the second condition — **an intact human audit layer**, which is what keeps the accuracy measured at all.

**Evaluation attached (satellite E-1d, 2026-07-06).** The design's own epistemics were tested and corrected: with an intact stack, a drifting verifier (5% → ~95% false-pass over thirty cycles) costs almost nothing in-model *and passive lane-3 sampling never detects it* — deterrence keeps true positives near zero, so no affordable sampling rate can estimate a false-pass rate from outcomes alone. The healthier the deployment, the blinder its outcome audit; the silent risk is sequencing (a later stack degradation meets a long-drifted, unmeasured verifier). The frontier therefore gains a sixth component: **seeded positive controls** — known-violation probes injected into the machine lane at rate q (the audit profession's proficiency-testing pattern), making drift-detection latency ∝ 1/(q·π), independent of the deterred real diversion rate. `s_min` remains for universal exposure, appeal capacity, and deterrence optics; **q is the epistemic knob**. Operational seeding design (who seeds, blinding, control secrecy) is part of the pending pipeline formalization. Source: `distributed-governance-experiments/adversarial-abm/RUN_2026_07_06_EXPERIMENT_E1D_RESULTS.md`.

## Layered heterogeneous machine verification (author direction, recorded 2026-07-07)

The author's next question: what if first-instance fiscalization is replaced **entirely** by layers of specialized machine agents — one engine as the first layer, a different engine as the second, and so on? Three sub-questions, with the analysis fixed in discussion:

1. **How many layers for sufficient confidence?** The binding variable is not the layer count but the **error correlation between engines**: independent failures would give multiplicative gains (p^k), but model families share training data and blind spots, so the fraud that passes one layer tends to pass the others through the *shared* blind spot — and no stack of models sees what is absent from the evidence (all layers see the evidence; none sees the world; ground-truth anchoring is a channel question, not a layer question). Expected shape: 2–3 heterogeneous layers capture most of the available benefit; beyond that, returns collapse into the correlated-failure mass. Confidence is therefore *measured, not assumed*: the E-1d **seeded positive controls generalize into the ensemble's independence meter** — a known-bad probe that passes two layers together more often than the product of their individual error rates has measured the correlation. Correction fixed in discussion: the layers replace the **first instance totally**; the E-1d human floor (seeding, ensemble audit, the mandatory set, the court-facing certification interface) is irreducible — total replacement would remove the instrument that measures the ensemble.
2. **How does the budget-to-value ratio change?** Verification capacity — the E-program's pipeline ceiling — becomes effectively unbounded and near-free; the ceiling relocates to **evidence production** (the control cost reduces to fulfillment evidences of machine-processable, court-grade quality) plus the small human audit floor. Honest modeling note: the current engines treat capacity K as a constraint, not a budget line; the quantification requires explicit control-cost accounting (real-world supervision runs ~3–10% of project cost).
3. **Can AI assess probative quality on technical grounds alone (legal frame adaptable per jurisdiction)?** Yes — and the stronger move is *constructive*: technical-probative properties (integrity, attested provenance à la C2PA, trusted timestamps, geolocation consistency, recorded custody chain, internal and cross-source consistency) are machine-verifiable and can be **required by the evidence contract at capture time** — court-grade-by-construction evidence, the natural extension of instrument 6 (self-verifying evidence at the source). Two boundaries: content authenticity (deepfakes) is a probabilistic arms race where attested provenance, not detection, is the durable defense; and the machine certifies the *properties* while courts decide the *weight*.

Quantified evaluation pre-registered as satellite **Experiment F** (F-0: empirical cross-engine error correlation via evidence-judgment panels — the models are the component under test, not synthetic priors; F-1: layers-vs-confidence in the longitudinal engine under measured correlation and ensemble gaming; F-2: the control-cost frontier). The author's model-layer refresh (latest Qwen, latest Gemma, ideally DeepSeek as a third family) precedes the F-0 runs. Candidate second satellite paper; results decide.

**Executed 2026-07-07, then corrected after a second review round.** The first subtle-fraud instrument carried a construct bug — every "clean" bundle published an above-threshold invoice item with no approval record, so models correctly flagging it scored as false alarms, inverting the false-flag result. On the de-leaked instrument (Wilson-CI'd, `evidence-panel/F0_V3_RESULTS.md`): the fraud side is saturated on mechanical flaws (layer-count questions not testable — subtler instrument is the named follow-up); on judgment-requiring flaws the corrected picture is **capability-tiered** — frontier models (gpt-5.5, Claude) converge on good specificity *and* fraud detection (≈0.03–0.08 false-flag, ≈0.08 false-pass), small local models are weaker (Gemma/DeepSeek ≈0.20 false-pass, 0.27–0.57 false-flag), Qwen degenerate (deny-all). The v2 "17–100% temperament spread" and the "evidence-richness rescue" were leak artifacts and are withdrawn. The one real correlation signal: the two frontier models share a blind spot (gpt–Claude φ=0.78, p<0.001), so "just add a second model" over-promises. Design consequences that survive: (a) **layer composition is specificity-first** with strict families as referees; (b) **seeded positive controls double as the operating-point calibrator**, conditional on indistinguishability; (c) additional layers pay only against independent-error fraud (benefit dead by ρ=0.3); (d) the economic step is human→machine (open-weights machine cost ≈0), with the binding line relocating to court-grade evidence production — restated as *tamper-evident, provenance-attested* capture, since the machine layer reaches only document-legible delivery-phase fraud (physical quality and pre-contract theft remain human). And Experiment G added the deepest scope: **cross-layer collusion** bypasses the per-milestone deterrence and moves leakage ~25× (the only attack to do so) while the verified-value advantage survives, so the whole package is scoped to a *non-colluding* adversary and collusion resistance is first-class (docs/113). Sources: `RUN_2026_07_07_EXPERIMENT_F0V2_RESULTS.md` (v2 + the bug), `evidence-panel/F0_V3_RESULTS.md` (corrected), `RUN_2026_07_07_EXPERIMENT_G_RESULTS.md`, `drafts/REVIEW_ROUND_2.md`.

## Contraposition-derived human review (author direction, recorded 2026-07-07)

Two additions from the author, on top of the second-instance design:

1. **Complaints already carry a double control**: probatory quality enforced at *entry* (court-grade ingestion validation of the complainant's evidence) plus admissibility review by the machine fiscalizer with human backup. Nothing new to build — the observation is that the complaint path (docs/26) composes with court-grade-by-construction ingestion into a two-gate channel.
2. **Citizen-contributed evidence as organic counter-verification.** Evidence contributed by ordinary citizens — validated at ingestion like any evidence — serves as a *control over approved work*, because contributors hold **opposed interests** to the executor (the neighbor wants the plaza actually finished; the executor wants the tranche released). The design rule that follows: **derive the human review from evidence contraposition** — the scarce human instance is summoned where citizen-contributed evidence *contradicts* executor-submitted evidence, machine-detected. This closes the deepest boundary Experiment F declared: no stack of models sees what is absent from the evidence — but citizen counter-evidence *is the world entering the record*, a continuous, organic, reality-anchored counterpart to the seeded controls. Routing human attention by measured contradiction converts the citizens' opposed interests into free adversarial verification, exactly the distributed-accountability loop (H008) given a mechanical trigger.

Recorded cautions: (a) opposed interests cut both ways — rival contractors or harassment campaigns can fabricate counter-evidence, so counter-evidence passes the same court-grade ingestion and its contributors carry the same reputational accountability as complainants (docs/26 discipline); (b) citizen watching is salience-biased (visible projects get watched, remote ones do not), so the random floor `s_min` remains for the unwatched — contraposition *replaces part of*, never all of, the sampling budget; (c) contribution incentives ride existing machinery (followed-projects feed, fiscalizer offers) plus the natural motive of opposed interests. Candidate quantification: a contradiction-triggered-review cell in the satellite engine — what fraction of the human review budget does contraposition replace at equal leak, as a function of citizen-coverage share.

**Machine-cost correction (author, 2026-07-07).** The F-2 machine-verification cost (0.1% of tranche per milestone) was a declared stipulation, not a modeled cost; the author's correction stands — with open-weights local models the effective marginal cost tends to zero (energy and hardware are sunk or marginal relative to an expert fiscalizer's salary). F-2 was re-run sweeping machine cost {0.1%, 0}: totals move ≤0.001 — the stipulation was already negligible, and the frontier's real content is unchanged: the machine regimes' remaining control cost is the residual human floor plus the court-grade evidence premium.

## Minimal human fiscalization and the H016 revision (author-approved 2026-07-07)

The consequence the measurements force, recorded at the author's instruction: with layered machine verification, contraposed citizen evidence, and the intact deterrence cascade, **humans touch roughly 15–25% of projects, lightly** (dossier-assisted flag resolution, low-confidence referrals, the mandatory set — now including the flaw classes where model families fail together, such as compressed timelines — a small random floor, complaints, and seeded-control operations), roughly 70–85% less routine verification labor. The author's centralization concern is real and answered structurally: what shrinks is the *paid routine-verification market*, not the citizenship of watching — H016's distributed-fiscalization vision **migrates** from "many paid humans verifying" to "many citizens watching (contraposition, with AI dossiers lowering the cost of watching) + a small professional audit corps". That corps requires explicit anti-capture design: cross-scope random assignment (which also aggregates the labor market across jurisdictions), rotation, separation of seeding and review functions, a plurality of contractable audit organizations per scope (never an audit monopoly), and auditors' verdicts logged and contestable — contraposition applies to the auditors too. F-3/F-4 evidence: the intact cascade covers the measured failures of today's models (a 21.7%-false-pass verifier leaks no more than pure-human verification), and contraposed evidence at half coverage rescues even a degraded stack (leak −72%) — anticipated contradiction is deterrence by itself.
