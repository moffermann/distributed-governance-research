# Agenda-Setting Study — Pre-Registered Design (candidate second manuscript)

Registered 2026-07-07. The operating-regime ladder ([[110_OPERATING_REGIME_LADDER_AND_SEMI_OPEN_MODE|docs/110_OPERATING_REGIME_LADDER_AND_SEMI_OPEN_MODE.md]]) formalized the four regimes' *definitions* but explicitly deferred the *mechanism design* of the two configurations the corpus had never treated: the tutored regime with a distributed agenda, and the semi-open regime's envelope objects. This is that study — the corpus's largest remaining intellectual object and the leading candidate for a dedicated second manuscript, distinct from the computational satellite (Offermann 2026b) and from the master.

## The question

Whoever constructs the Planning Scope's weight vector exercises the second face of power (docs/87, A020). docs/110 fixed *where authority discretion lives* per regime; this study designs the *mechanism* by which a distributed agenda is actually constructed and governed, and by which it coexists with an incumbent authority's agenda without the authority recapturing it through the back door.

## The three design objects

1. **The parallel-agenda construction mechanism** (the author's recorded design direction): the authority keeps its centralized agenda; the system constructs a distributed agenda alongside it from attentive + delegated citizen signals; in tutored mode the authority's power over the distributed agenda narrows to *admissibility of conflicts between the two agendas*, not general approval. Design: how the distributed vector is aggregated (elicitation mechanism), how conflicts are defined and detected, and what "admissibility review of a conflict" may and may not do (a closed causal set, per the docs/110 anti-covert-tutelage discipline).

2. **The semi-open envelope objects**: the bounded mandated envelope over named ontology subtrees. Design: the envelope object (which subtrees, what share, what period), the automatic-approval protocol (objective preconditions, not enumerated objection grounds), the ontological-frontier classification act (protocol-checkable and contestable, or discretion relocates there), and the collision-prevention-by-partition rule.

3. **The elicitation mechanism under gaming** (docs/87/91, the standing open problem): the study's hardest object — how dispersed citizen signals stay honest, unbiased, and representative under gaming, clientelism, and expressive-allocation pressure. E7b and the satellite's coordinated-signal-bias results bound the risk (near-linear degradation, ~30% coordination crossover); the mechanism design is the object.

## Method (mirrors the program's discipline)

- Pre-registered predictions before any run; honest prediction accounting.
- A planning-vector-construction extension (the satellite engine already models representative vs distributed vs Core-v0 channels) becomes the testbed for the parallel-agenda and envelope mechanisms.
- Adversarial: coordinated capture of the elicitation, frontier-classification gaming, and — post Experiment G — collusive capture of the aggregation institution.
- Anchored to the corpus (docs/58, docs/87, docs/110) with the traceability contract.

## Pre-registered predictions (initial)

- **PA1.** A parallel distributed agenda with conflict-only admissibility review delivers planning-vector quality within the Core-v0 channel band (0.815–0.939) while keeping the authority's per-item veto out of general scope construction — i.e., the narrow admissibility power costs little vector quality.
- **PA2.** Frontier classification is the discretion's new home: if the ontology-subtree classification act is not protocol-checkable, an authority recaptures agenda control through misclassification at a rate that erases the distributed advantage.
- **PA3.** The elicitation mechanism's honesty is the binding constraint (as the whole program has found): no aggregation design beats coordinated capture above the ~30% coordination share; the deliverable is the mechanism that raises that threshold.
- **PA4.** Collusive capture of the aggregation institution (Experiment G's lesson applied to agenda-setting) is more damaging than signal-level gaming, because it bypasses the elicitation entirely — the agenda analog of the verification collusion result.

## Scope and status

Recorded design, not executed. It is the corpus's next major research object; whether it becomes a standalone manuscript or a section of the master is an author decision deferred until the mechanism results exist. It is explicitly *larger* than any single experiment run so far — the reason it is registered as a program, not an experiment.
