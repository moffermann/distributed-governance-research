# Session Distillation — Ricardo Review Round

Date: 2026-06-25 / 2026-06-26

## Context

Ricardo acted as an external reviewer of the emerging distributed governance theory. His questions forced clarification around value, representation, information integrity, deliberation, and accountability.

## Main additions from this round

### 1. Assisted deliberation

A distributed system should not merely aggregate preferences. It should improve the quality of the decision environment by exposing pros, cons, costs, externalities, evidence, risks, alternatives, and affected groups.

The algorithm should not decide for people. It should help people decide better.

### 2. Performance dashboards

Before allocating resources, citizens should be able to inspect historical performance. Institutions should not compete only through promises, narratives, or popularity. They should show previous commitments, KPI fulfillment, audits, beneficiary evaluations, complaints, and comparisons with similar projects.

### 3. Distributed accountability

Accountability must not depend only on the institution receiving resources. Self-reporting creates incentives for manipulation.

The platform should combine:

- commitments declared by the institution;
- evidence of execution;
- financial traceability;
- beneficiaries;
- families or users;
- independent auditors;
- experts;
- public complaints;
- AI-assisted inconsistency detection.

### 4. Full resource allocation cycle

The emerging cycle is:

> proposal → financing → execution → evidence → evaluation → reputation → new financing

This cycle is stronger than a simple voting or donation model because it links future support to verifiable past performance.

### 5. Democracy augmented, not weakened

A criticism emerged that distributed allocation may become a popularity contest. The counterargument is that current democracy also contains popularity dynamics and imperfect preference aggregation. The proposed model may become a form of augmented democracy because it is more direct, more informed, more traceable, and more continuously evaluated than periodic indirect representation.

## New hypotheses created

- H006 — Assisted Deliberation
- H007 — Continuous Performance Evaluation

## Research significance

This round strengthened the theory by showing that distributed governance cannot rely only on participation. It requires an information and accountability architecture that makes decisions better informed and makes institutional performance continuously visible.
