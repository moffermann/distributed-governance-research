# H005 — Personal AI Guides for Participation

## Hypothesis

Personalized AI guides could reduce the cognitive cost of citizen participation by summarizing proposals, comparing them with individual preferences, detecting contradictions, and recommending informed actions.

## Problem addressed

A common objection to participatory governance is that people lack time, information, or technical knowledge to participate in many decisions.

## Proposed role of AI

Not a central AI that decides for everyone, but many personal AI guides aligned with each individual.

## Risks

- model bias;
- provider capture;
- manipulation;
- over-delegation;
- privacy concerns.

## Status

Useful supporting hypothesis. Not the foundation of the theory because distributed governance can be explored even without advanced AI.
