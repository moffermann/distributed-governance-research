# H003 — Distributed Trust

## Hypothesis

Trust can be distributed through architecture: multiple sources, multiple verifiers, transparent evidence, audit trails, reputation, and arbitration can reduce dependence on a single institutional authority.

## Origin

This emerged from two lines of discussion:

1. state-level governance and information integrity;
2. a product idea for agreements/contracts where AI structures commitments, escrow protects payment, and arbitration resolves ambiguity.

## Principle

Do not ask people to trust a single authority when the system can be designed so that claims are verifiable.

## Blockchain analogy

Blockchain is not necessarily the product or solution. It is a useful analogy because it distributes the registry and reduces dependence on a central trusted party.

## Broader application

Distributed trust can apply to:

- public information;
- resource allocation;
- arbitration;
- contracts;
- institutions;
- governance processes.
