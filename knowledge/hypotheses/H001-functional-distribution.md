# H001 — Functional Distribution Principle

## Hypothesis

The optimal degree of institutional distribution depends on the functional nature of the state function, not on a general ideological preference for more or less state.

## Working formulation

Functions close to the legitimate use of force tend to require stronger central coordination. Functions centered on information, deliberation, knowledge generation, resource allocation, or service evaluation tend to have greater potential for distribution.

## Rationale

Not all state functions share the same risk profile. Fragmenting defense may create catastrophic coordination and civil conflict risks. Distributing deliberation, auditing, research, arbitration, or resource allocation may improve information quality and accountability.

## Status

Core hypothesis. Needs literature review and formal typology.
