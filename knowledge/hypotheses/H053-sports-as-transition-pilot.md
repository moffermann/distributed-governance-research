# H053 — Sports as Transition Pilot

## Hypothesis

Sports is a strong candidate function for the first institutional-transition pilot toward distributed governance.

## Rationale

A realistic implementation should not begin by attempting to replace the entire state. It should start with a function that is visible, socially attractive, relatively low-risk, and politically less central than defense, justice, taxation, security, or macroeconomic management.

Sports may satisfy these conditions better than many other state functions.

## Reasons

Sports may be a good pilot area because:

- it is popular and visible;
- it can motivate citizen participation;
- it is less strategically sensitive than core state functions;
- many sports initiatives can be structured as projects;
- athletes, clubs, communities, schools, municipalities, and sponsors already operate in a distributed ecosystem;
- results can often be made visible through participation, infrastructure use, competition support, training access, equipment delivery, and athlete development;
- it may face fewer legal and regulatory complexities than environmental or heritage-related functions.

## Institutional critique to test

A sports-transition pilot can test whether distributed allocation improves on bureaucratic resource allocation, especially where citizens perceive that traditional institutional spending produces limited visible benefit for athletes, clubs, or communities.

This should be treated as an empirical question, not as an assumption.

## Possible project types

Examples:

- equipment for local clubs;
- athlete travel support;
- training infrastructure;
- coaching programs;
- community tournaments;
- parasport initiatives;
- school sports programs;
- regional talent development;
- sports facility maintenance;
- independent fiscalization of sports spending.

## Transition role

Sports can serve as an early test of:

- civic tax wallet allocation;
- project discovery;
- delegation;
- reputation of executors;
- fiscalization;
- evidence contracts;
- milestone disbursement;
- transition KPIs;
- institutional maturity measurement.

## Principle

> The first transition pilot should be socially visible, low enough risk to experiment, and concrete enough to measure. Sports is a strong candidate.

## Status

Transition-pilot hypothesis. Needs later empirical validation and country-specific design.
