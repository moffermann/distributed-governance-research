# H018 — Project Value Thesis and Measurement

## Hypothesis

Every project should explicitly declare its thesis of value before receiving resources, along with a proportionate method for measuring whether that value was delivered.

## Rationale

A distributed architecture does not discover social value as an objective truth and does not impose one universal theory of value. Instead, it allows projects to propose value theses that society can support, evaluate, compare, and revise.

## Value thesis

A project should declare:

- what value it claims to generate;
- for whom;
- why that value matters;
- in what time horizon;
- what evidence would show progress;
- who can legitimately evaluate it;
- what antivalue it may produce;
- what happens if it fails.

## No universal KPI

Different projects require different metrics. A show of puppets, a highway, a hospital, a basic science project, and a heritage preservation project should not be evaluated with the same metrics.

The KPI follows the thesis of value.

## Example: puppet show

If the thesis is recreation and cultural access for children, the indicators may be simple:

- functions performed;
- approximate attendance;
- evidence of execution;
- comments from parents or teachers;
- satisfaction signals;
- absence of serious incidents;
- willingness to fund future performances.

If the thesis is educational, the indicators may include:

- message comprehension;
- teacher evaluation;
- before/after learning signals if proportionate.

If the thesis is heritage or cultural preservation, the indicators change again.

## Formal evaluation and social signals

The model distinguishes:

1. **Formal evaluation of fulfillment:** whether the project did what it promised.
2. **Distributed signals of perceived value:** comments, recommendations, likes, repeated support, word of mouth, community response, and willingness to finance similar projects.

Not all value should be converted into rigid KPIs.

## Principle

> The system should not force every human experience into hard metrics. It should combine proportionate evidence of fulfillment with distributed social signals of perceived value.

## Status

Core hypothesis for Ricardo's second major question on social value.
