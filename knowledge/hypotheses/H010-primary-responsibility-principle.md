# H010 — Primary Responsibility Principle

## Hypothesis

Every project in a distributed planning and resource allocation system should declare exactly one primary goal within the roadmap hierarchy.

## Rationale

A project that claims to pursue many equally important goals becomes difficult to fund, evaluate, audit, compare, and hold accountable.

The system needs to know what the project is primarily responsible for delivering.

## Rule

A project must declare:

- exactly one primary goal;
- zero or more secondary goals;
- the level at which it operates: national, regional, communal, or local.

## Secondary goals

Secondary goals may represent additional benefits or externalities, but they should not replace the accountability attached to the primary goal.

Example:

Primary goal:

- reduce emissions from urban transport.

Secondary goals:

- improve air quality;
- reduce congestion;
- increase bicycle usage;
- improve public health.

## Splitting rule

If a project has two goals that are both truly primary, it may actually be two projects and should be split.

## Benefits

This principle improves:

- funding clarity;
- KPI definition;
- accountability;
- auditability;
- comparison with similar projects;
- reputation scoring;
- future funding decisions.

## Status

Core design principle for the project layer of the planning architecture.
