# H055 — Universal Institutional Observability Panel

## Hypothesis

The distributed governance platform should implement a universal observability panel that captures internal system metrics useful for any country or jurisdiction evaluating institutional transition.

The system should not define political thresholds for adoption, budget expansion, or institutional replacement. It should provide transparent, auditable metrics generated by the platform itself.

## Rationale

The implementing country decides how to interpret metrics and how to compare them with its own institutional baselines. The platform should not impose those decisions.

The platform should instead record all useful internal metrics that help public authorities, citizens, researchers, and auditors evaluate whether a distributed function is operating well.

## Design rule

Only metrics produced by the platform itself should be part of the universal observability panel.

The platform should not depend on external datasets to compute its core transition metrics.

Examples of out-of-scope external metrics:

- national GDP impact;
- obesity rates;
- public health outcomes;
- national crime rates;
- school test scores;
- broad quality-of-life indicators.

Those can be compared externally by the implementing country, but they are not intrinsic platform metrics.

## Platform versus country decision

```text
Platform:
  measures and displays internal evidence.

Country / jurisdiction:
  interprets the evidence and decides policy, budget, and transition pace.
```

## 1. Citizen adoption metrics

These measure whether citizens actually use the system.

| Metric | Measures | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Verified active users | Number of identified users who performed at least one action in the period | Measures real adoption, not just registration |
| Direct participation rate | Share of users assigning resources, voting, commenting, or evaluating directly | Measures active non-delegated participation |
| Delegated participation rate | Share of users participating through delegation | Measures whether delegation expands participation |
| Inactive users using automatic rule | Share of users whose funds are allocated through default or configured autopilot | Measures reliance on automatic civic allocation |
| Territorial participation | Distribution of participation by declared commune, region, or territory | Detects geographic concentration |
| Participation by category | Activity distribution across project categories | Shows which areas mobilize social value |

## 2. Resource allocation metrics

These show how money flows inside the platform.

| Metric | Measures | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Civic budget assigned | Total resources assigned to projects in the period | Measures volume mobilized |
| Directly assigned budget | Resources assigned by direct user decision | Measures active citizen allocation |
| Delegated budget | Resources assigned by delegates | Measures delegated influence |
| Automatic profile budget | Resources assigned by profiles or automatic rules | Measures reliance on automation |
| Committed budget | Resources supporting projects not yet fully funded | Measures latent demand |
| Milestone-released budget | Resources actually disbursed after milestone compliance | Measures real execution, not just intention |
| Retained budget | Funded resources still held pending milestones | Measures pending exposure and discipline |
| Returned or reassigned budget | Eligible resources returned or reassigned through closure, expiration, rejected or expired reformulation, revocation, unused balances, retentions, guarantees, or recovery | Measures recycling of eligible unexecuted or recovered commitments without implying voluntary withdrawal |

## 3. Project lifecycle metrics

These show whether projects move through the system.

| Metric | Measures | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Projects proposed | Number of projects submitted | Measures initiative supply |
| Projects published | Projects passing minimum publication checks | Measures publication throughput |
| Projects funded | Projects reaching funding target | Measures social coordination capacity |
| Funding success rate | Funded projects divided by published projects | Measures project-market effectiveness |
| Time to funding | Days from publication to funding closure | Measures coordination speed |
| Projects expired without funding | Projects that missed their funding deadline | Detects low demand or poor formulation |
| Projects reformulated | Projects changed after not reaching funding or after feedback | Measures learning and adaptation |
| Projects closed | Projects closed, expired, revoked, or discarded | Measures ecosystem cleanup |
| Projects in execution | Funded projects that started execution | Measures conversion from funding to action |

## 4. Execution metrics

These measure whether funded projects actually execute.

| Metric | Measures | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Milestones completed | Completed milestones per project | Measures objective progress |
| Milestone compliance rate | Completed milestones divided by committed milestones | Measures execution discipline |
| Delayed milestones | Milestones not completed on time | Detects early risk |
| Average delay | Difference between committed and actual completion dates | Measures severity of delay |
| Projects completed | Projects completing all milestones | Measures effective closure |
| Completion rate | Completed projects divided by initiated projects | Measures execution success |
| Projects paused | Temporarily halted projects | Measures conflict or execution friction |
| Projects revoked | Projects terminated early by rule or governance process | Measures severe failure |
| Partially executed projects | Projects executing some but not all commitments | Measures partial value and loss risk |

| Metric | Measures | Use |
|---|---|---|

| KPIs declared | Number and type of indicators committed | Measures evaluation structure |
| KPIs fulfilled | Indicators reaching declared target | Measures actual performance |
| KPI fulfillment rate | Fulfilled KPIs divided by committed KPIs | Measures effectiveness |
| Evidence submitted | Documents, photos, videos, measurements, reports, or data uploaded | Measures evidential activity |
| Evidence accepted by fiscalization | Evidence considered sufficient by fiscalizers | Measures evidence quality |
| Evidence observed or rejected | Evidence questioned or insufficient | Detects weak proof |
| Projects with incomplete evidence | Projects missing required evidence | Measures compliance risk |
| Antivalue declared | Externalities or risks declared in project design | Measures preventive transparency |
| Undeclared antivalue detected | Externalities discovered during execution that were not declared | Measures omission, error, or bad faith |

| Metric | Measures | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Active fiscalizers | Fiscalizers with activity in the period | Measures available oversight capacity |
| Projects fiscalized | Projects reviewed by fiscalizers | Measures coverage |
| Fiscalization rate | Fiscalized projects divided by executed projects | Measures oversight depth |
| Time to fiscalization | Days between evidence/hit and review | Measures control speed |

| Fiscalizations challenged | Reports disputed by system actors | Measures controversy over oversight |
| Fiscalizations confirmed | Reports surviving review or appeal | Measures fiscalizer reliability |

## 7. Complaint, incident, and conflict metrics

These measure accountability health.

| Metric | Measures | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Complaints filed | Formal complaints entered | Measures citizen control activation |
| Complaints admitted | Complaints meeting threshold for review | Separates signal from noise |
| Complaints rejected | Complaints lacking sufficient merit | Measures weak or unproductive use |
| Complaints resolved | Complaints reaching final decision | Measures closure capacity |
| Complaint resolution time | Days from filing to resolution | Measures dispute efficiency |
| Malicious complaints confirmed | Complaints found false or in bad faith | Measures abuse |
| Projects with incidents | Projects activating complaint, pause, extraordinary review, or mitigation | Measures conflict level |
| Projects paused by complaint | Projects temporarily halted by review | Measures impact of complaints |
| Projects with mitigation required | Projects required to correct externalities | Measures adaptive capacity |
| Guarantees executed | Bonds or guarantees enforced | Measures real consequences |

## 8. Concentration and capture metrics

These measure whether power is being distributed or merely transferred.

| Metric | Measures | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Funding concentration | Share of funding from large contributors | Detects economic influence concentration |
| Social support concentration | Projects supported by few versus many citizens | Separates broad legitimacy from narrow funding |
| Executor concentration | Share of projects or resources executed by top executors | Detects execution oligopolies |
| Modeler concentration | Share of projects designed by top modelers | Detects design capture |
| Fiscalizer concentration | Share of reviews by top fiscalizers | Detects control dependency |
| Delegation concentration | Share of citizens or resources represented by top delegates | Detects delegated power accumulation |
| Territorial concentration | Resources and projects concentrated in a few territories | Detects geographic inequity |
| Related-party projects | Projects where funder, executor, operator, or beneficiary are related | Detects conflicts of interest |
| Resources in conflict-declared projects | Share of budget in transparent conflict-of-interest projects | Measures exposure to capture risk |

| Metric | Measures | Use |
|---|---|---|

## 15. Urgency and emergency metrics

These apply to projects marked as urgent or emergency.

| Metric | Measures | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Urgent projects | Projects marked urgent/emergency | Measures demand for rapid response |
| Time to fund urgent projects | Speed from publication to funding | Measures response capacity |
| Time to execute urgent projects | Speed from funding to action | Measures operational responsiveness |
| Urgent projects completed | Urgent projects completed successfully | Measures crisis effectiveness |
| Urgent projects failed or revoked | Urgent projects that failed | Detects abuse or poor execution |
| Urgent projects fiscalized | Oversight coverage for urgent projects | Prevents urgency from eliminating accountability |

## No single success score

The system should not hide institutional performance behind a single aggregate score such as:

```text
System success index: 87/100
```

A single score would reintroduce hidden value judgments.

Instead, the platform should show many internal metrics transparently. The implementing country decides how to interpret them.

## Principle

> The platform provides observability. The country interprets. Society audits both.
