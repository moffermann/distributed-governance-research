# H054 — Functional Transition Maturity Metrics

## Status

Country-implementation and Extension v1+ transition-evaluation hypothesis aligned with [[../../docs/58_TUTORED_MODE_GOVERNANCE_RESOLUTIONS_AND_C020_RESOLUTION|C020]], [[../../docs/59_CORE_ADMINISTRATIVE_OBSERVABILITY_BASELINE_AND_C021_RESOLUTION|C021]], [[H055-universal-institutional-observability-panel|H055]], and [[H058-operating-modes-for-transition|H058]].

H054 defines maturity metrics that can help a country, jurisdiction, public authority, citizens, auditors, and researchers evaluate whether a distributed public function is working well. It does not define mandatory political thresholds, automatic budget migration, automatic exit from tutored mode, or automatic institutional replacement.

## Hypothesis

A distributed governance model should be evaluated through functional, measurable, and progressive transition metrics rather than treated as an immediate replacement for existing state institutions.

Those metrics should inform public decisions about adoption, expansion, reduction, suspension, or abandonment. They should not force those decisions.

## Rationale

A real country already has institutions, budgets, legal obligations, programs, employees, contracts, beneficiaries, and political constraints.

Therefore, the question is not whether the state should disappear immediately, but whether specific public functions can be opened to distributed coordination when the competent authority chooses that path and when performance evidence supports, questions, or limits that choice.

C020 clarifies that the platform does not force a country to leave tutored mode. A country may keep a function tutored, expand it, reduce it, suspend it, abandon it, or never adopt the platform. H054 supplies transition evidence for those decisions; it does not decide them.

## Core question

```text
What evidence helps a country decide whether a public function currently coordinated by a central institution should remain centralized, stay tutored, open a pilot, expand distributed coordination, pause, or return to traditional institutional control?
```

## Functional transition, not ideological elimination

An institution should not cede space because the model assumes it should.

Nor should the platform force the institution to cede space when metrics improve.

The implementing country or jurisdiction may choose to cede, expand, maintain, reduce, pause, or recover space based on law, political authority, risk, public value, administrative capacity, and platform evidence.

The transition should be evidence-informed, not ideology-driven or platform-mandated.

## Platform versus country decision

```text
Platform:
  measures, records, displays, and exports internal evidence.

Country / jurisdiction:
  interprets the evidence and decides policy, budget, operating mode,
  transition pace, and institutional role.

Citizens and civil society:
  audit the evidence, comment on governance resolutions,
  object to weak decisions, and compare authority explanations
  against observable results.
```

This separation is important because metrics can support democratic scrutiny without turning the platform into a sovereign political actor.

## Maturity levels

A possible maturity model:

```text
Level 0 — Central institution
  Institution designs, assigns, executes, fiscalizes, and evaluates.

Level 1 — Transparency increased
  Institution keeps control, but budgets, KPIs, results, and evidence become visible.

Level 2 — Distributed pilot
  A small percentage of the function's budget or projects enters distributed allocation.

Level 3 — Hybrid coordination
  Institution coexists with distributed projects, modelers, executors, fiscalizers, delegation, and reputation.

Level 4 — Distributed domain
  Most of the function operates through the distributed architecture.

Level 5 — Residual or transformed institution
  Institution becomes a guarantor, custodian, regulator, systemic auditor, or continuity/emergency layer rather than the central allocator.
```

These maturity levels are descriptive labels, not mandatory stages.

A public function may remain at Level 1 or Level 2 indefinitely if the country chooses that configuration. It may also move backward after failure, legal change, poor execution, fraud, emergency, or political decision. The platform should record the facts and make the decision traceable; it should not force the next level.

Example:

```text
Public function:
  Sports.

Initial country decision:
  Open 5% of the sports ministry budget to distributed governance
  under tutored mode.

Observed evidence:
  High project completion rate, good fiscalization coverage,
  low complaint rate, and shorter proposal-to-funding time
  than the prior institutional baseline.

Possible authority decisions:
  Keep the 5% pilot unchanged.
  Expand to 10%.
  Keep tutored mode permanently.
  Open only selected communes.
  Suspend the pilot after legal or budgetary change.
  Close the pilot and return to traditional allocation.

H054 role:
  Make the evidence visible and comparable.
  It does not choose among those political options.
```

## Universal transition KPIs

These KPIs should be usable across public functions, not only for a specific ministry or sector.

### 1. Citizen adoption

- number of participating citizens;
- number of delegators;
- direct versus delegated participation;
- participation frequency;
- territorial distribution;
- social and demographic distribution where lawful and ethical to measure.

### 2. Budget migration

- percentage of the function's budget assigned through distributed mechanisms;
- percentage effectively executed;
- percentage handled by default allocation rules;
- unused or reassigned funds;
- rate of budget migration over time.

### 3. Operational efficiency

- proposal-to-funding time;
- funding-to-execution time;
- administrative cost;
- milestone compliance;
- percentage of projects completed;
- percentage of projects paused, reformulated, or revoked;
- comparison with the prior institutional baseline.

### 4. Fiscalization capacity

- number of available fiscalizers;
- fiscalizer diversity and independence;
- percentage of projects fiscalized;
- time to resolve complaints;
- quality of evidence;
- cases of fraud or hidden information detected;
- guarantee execution rate.

### 5. Power distribution

- concentration of funding;
- concentration of execution;
- concentration of delegation;
- concentration of fiscalization;
- territorial concentration;
- role diversity among participants.

### 6. Accountability and traceability

- clarity of who proposed, funded, executed, fiscalized, and evaluated;
- availability of project evidence;
- auditability of decisions;
- reputation history of actors;
- public visibility of failures and corrections.

### 7. Value and results

- fulfillment of declared project KPIs;
- beneficiary satisfaction;
- perceived value by funders and communities;
- externalities or antivalue detected;
- sector-specific value indicators.

### 8. Institutional maturity

- ability to operate without continuous central intervention;
- reduction of institutional bottlenecks;
- quality of conflict resolution;
- platform usability;
- resilience of the distributed ecosystem;
- readiness to increase, maintain, reduce, pause, or reverse the distributed budget share.

## Sector-specific KPIs

The universal framework should be complemented by sector-specific KPIs.

Examples:

```text
Sports:
  athletes supported, clubs supported, infrastructure repaired, competitions funded.

Culture:
  artists supported, events held, audience reached, heritage protected.

Education:
  students served, hours of instruction, attendance, learning progress.
```

Sector-specific KPIs should not define the core model. They are templates for each public function selected for transition.

## Implementation pace

The model should not define automatic budget increases such as "if X happens, move from 5% to 10%."

The implementing state or jurisdiction determines the pace of adoption according to law, political constraints, risk, and results.

The model provides the maturity indicators that should inform that decision.

The same rule applies to operating modes. Metrics may support an argument for moving from tutored mode to semi-open or open mode, but they do not require that change. Permanent or indefinite tutored mode is not a contradiction under C020. The contradiction would be opaque tutored governance without public Governance Resolutions, Review Timeout Resolutions, review windows, timeout policies, and auditability.

## Relationship with C021 and H055

C021 separates observability into:

- simple user-facing signals for ordinary citizens and organizations;
- a minimal Core v0 administrative observability baseline generated by the platform;
- the broader H055 universal institutional observability panel as Extension v1+.

H054 should not be imported wholesale into the ordinary citizen interface or Core v0 administrative baseline.

Ordinary citizens should receive simple signals and drill-down access:

```text
Project:
  Multi-court complex in Macul.

Citizen-facing signals:
  Tutored review status.
  Review time remaining.
  Funding progress.
  Fiscalizer coverage.
  Evidence status.
  Governance Resolution, if rejected or modified.
```

Administrative and institutional users may later access deeper H054/H055 metrics:

```text
Sports pilot:
  5% budget opened.
  Average tutored review time.
  Review timeout resolutions.
  Projects rejected for duplication with existing ministry portfolio.
  Completion rate.
  Fiscalization coverage.
  Comparison with prior institutional baseline.
```

The platform should avoid a single maturity score because that would hide political and value judgments inside a number.

## Principle

> The pilot may be sector-specific, but transition metrics should be functionally universal. The platform measures; the country decides; society audits both.

## Remaining design risks

- Authorities may ignore strong positive or negative evidence.
- Authorities may select only favorable metrics to justify a prior political decision.
- A future implementation may reintroduce a single hidden maturity score.
- Administrative users may treat descriptive maturity levels as mandatory stages.
- Ordinary citizen interfaces may become too complex if H054 metrics are exposed without layering.

## Research note

Transition-evaluation hypothesis. Responds to Ricardo's question about whether the model is an end state or a transition and how institutional change should be measured without making the platform responsible for political adoption, budget migration, or institutional displacement.
