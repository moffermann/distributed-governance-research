# H004 — Institutional Monopoly Reduction

## Hypothesis

Many failures attributed to public institutions are not caused merely by public ownership, but by monopoly status and weak accountability. Reducing institutional monopolies may improve incentives and outcomes.

## Rationale

A public institution that performs poorly often continues receiving budget and authority. In a distributed model, institutions would need to demonstrate value continuously to maintain support.

## Important nuance

This does not imply eliminating all institutions. It implies distinguishing between:

- functions that require monopoly or strong central coordination;
- functions where multiple institutions can compete, cooperate, and be audited.

## Potential benefits

- better incentives;
- faster replacement of poor performers;
- visibility of failing institutions;
- competition on quality rather than political access;
- stronger accountability to users and funders.

## Status

Strong hypothesis. Needs safeguards for continuity of essential services.
