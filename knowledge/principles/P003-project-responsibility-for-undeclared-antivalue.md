# P003 — Project Responsibility for Undeclared Antivalue

## Principle

A project is responsible for the antivalue it fails to declare, even if that antivalue is discovered during execution rather than before financing. ^r0c7f3f7e

## Rationale

A project that asks society for resources assumes responsibility for understanding its risks, costs, externalities, and possible harms.

The system should not need to prove bad faith before activating responsibility. Otherwise, every undeclared externality becomes a dispute over intent.

## Rule

If relevant antivalue appears and was not declared in the proposal, the project should face responsibility mechanisms such as:

- guarantee execution;
- retained fund use;
- insurance activation;
- mitigation funding;
- compensation;
- reviewed role-specific reputation input where responsibility is established;
- pause;
- revocation if unresolved.

## Why this matters

This creates a strong incentive to declare risks honestly before receiving resources.

## Important nuance

Responsibility for undeclared antivalue does not necessarily mean immediate revocation. The first use of guarantees may be to finance mitigation, compensate affected parties, or pay for fiscalization.

It also does not mean immediate negative reputation. Discovery, complaint filing, admissibility, or systemic pause may be visible procedural signals, but formal reputation effects require final resolution, founded responsibility, confirmed non-compliance, or a role-specific Responsibility Event.

## Status

Core risk-allocation principle.
